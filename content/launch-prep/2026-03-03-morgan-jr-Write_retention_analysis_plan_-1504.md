**Retention Analysis Plan: D1/D7/D30 Benchmarks**

**1. Overall Goal:**

* To understand user behavior after initial acquisition and identify key factors driving retention (or churn).  This analysis will inform strategies to improve user engagement and ultimately, long-term retention.

**2. Target User Group:**

* **Clearly Define:** Specify the user group you're analyzing.  Examples:
    * New users acquired through a specific marketing campaign.
    * Users who signed up during a particular timeframe.
    * Users who completed a key onboarding step.

**3. Data Sources:**

* **Core Data:**  Your product/service database is the primary source.
* **Analytics Platforms:** Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or similar, for deeper behavioral insights.
* **CRM:** Salesforce, HubSpot, or similar, for user information and engagement data.
* **Marketing Automation:** Mailchimp, Marketo, etc., for email engagement.
* **Customer Support:**  Ticket tracking systems for identifying pain points.


**4. Key Metrics & Definitions:**

* **Retention Rate:** The percentage of users who are still active after a specific period.
* **Cohort:** A group of users who started using the product/service during the same time period. Crucial for comparing retention across groups.
* **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Number of unique users active in a 24-hour period.
* **WAU (Weekly Active Users):** Number of unique users active in a 7-day period.
* **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** Number of unique users active in a 30-day period.
* **Churn Rate:**  The percentage of users who stopped using the product/service during a period.  (1 - Retention Rate)
* **Time to Value (TTV):** The time it takes a user to experience the core value proposition of your product.
* **Feature Usage:** Tracking which features users are engaging with, and how frequently.
* **Session Duration/Frequency:** How long and how often users are using your product.
