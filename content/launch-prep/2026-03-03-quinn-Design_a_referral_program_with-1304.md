Okay, let's design a referral program with a focus on incentivizing both the referrer and the referred. Here’s a detailed plan, broken down into sections, with various options to suit different business types and budgets:

**1. Program Name & Theme:**

* **Example:** "Share the [Your Brand Name] Love" or "[Your Brand Name] Perks Program" - Keep it friendly and highlight the benefit.

**2. Target Audience & Goals:**

* **Define:**  Who are you trying to attract with this program? (New customers, specific demographics, loyal customers?)
* **Goals:** What do you want to achieve? (Increase sign-ups, boost sales, grow your user base, etc.)

**3. Referral Structure & Levels (Tiered is Highly Recommended):**

This is the core of the program. Let's outline a tiered structure for engagement.

* **Level 1: Basic Referral (Simple & Easy)**
    * **Referrer Reward:**  Small discount (e.g., 10% off first purchase) or a small credit to their account.
    * **Referred Reward:**  Discount on their first purchase (e.g., 15% off).
    * **Mechanics:**  Simple referral link or code that the referrer shares.
* **Level 2: Enhanced Referral (Increased Incentive)**
    * **Referrer Reward:**  Larger discount (e.g., 20% off), a free product/service (small value), or a gift card.
    * **Referred Reward:**  Higher discount (e.g., 20% off), Free Shipping, or a small gift (e.g., sample size product).
    * **Mechanics:** Same as Level 1, but with a more prominent reward.
* **Level 3: VIP Referral (For Loyal Customers – Optional)**
    * **Referrer Reward:**  Exclusive access to new product launches, premium content, or early bird discounts.
    * **Referred Reward:**  Significant discount (e.g., 30% off), Exclusive bundle offer, or a higher-value product.
    * **Mechanics:** Requires a certain number of successful referrals to unlock this tier.
