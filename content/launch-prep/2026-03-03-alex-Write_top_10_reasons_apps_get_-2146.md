**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store**

1. **Policy Violations (General):** This is the biggest category. Apple’s App Store Review Guidelines are extensive and constantly updated. Violations can range from misleading descriptions to inappropriate content.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Behavior:** Apps that don’t accurately represent their functionality, use tricks to get users to spend money, or collect data unfairly are often rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:** Collecting, storing, or sharing user data without proper disclosure and consent is a major red flag.  This includes not having a clear and accessible privacy policy.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted material (music, images, characters, code) without permission is a quick way to get rejected.
5. **Technical Issues:**  Bugs, crashes, poor performance, and unreliable functionality all lead to rejections. Apple prioritizes a stable and usable experience.
6. **Inappropriate Content:** This includes sexually suggestive content, violence, hate speech, or anything that violates Apple’s moral guidelines.
7. **Payment Issues:**  Incorrect payment processing, misleading in-app purchases, or failing to comply with Apple’s App Store Payment Terms can result in rejection.
8. **App Metadata Issues:** Problems with the app’s name, keywords, description, screenshots, and preview video can raise concerns and trigger rejections.
9. **Third-Party SDK Issues:**  Using third-party SDKs (Software Development Kits) that violate Apple’s guidelines or have known security vulnerabilities.
10. **Lack of Essential Information:**  Missing required information like app ID, bundle ID, or failure to provide accurate developer details.
