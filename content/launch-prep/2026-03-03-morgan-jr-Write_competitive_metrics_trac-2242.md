Okay, let's craft a competitive metrics tracking plan. This will be a framework you can adapt to your specific business and competitive landscape.

**I. Executive Summary**

* **Purpose:** This plan outlines a system for consistently monitoring and analyzing our key competitive metrics, enabling data-driven decision-making, proactive strategy adjustments, and ultimately, improved market share and performance.
* **Frequency:** Metrics will be tracked and reviewed on a [Daily/Weekly/Monthly - choose based on your needs & industry] basis.  More granular metrics will be tracked daily (e.g., website traffic), while broader trends will be reviewed weekly/monthly.
* **Ownership:** [Designated Team/Role - e.g., Marketing Analytics Team, Competitive Intelligence Team] – responsible for data collection, analysis, and reporting.


**II. Identifying Your Key Competitors**

* **List:** Create a comprehensive list of your direct and indirect competitors.  Categorize them (e.g., High, Medium, Low Threat) based on market share, product overlap, and strategic alignment.
* **Research:** Regularly update this list as new players emerge or existing ones shift their strategies.
* **Segmentation:**  Don't just look at overall market share.  Segment competitors by:
    * **Product/Service Offering:** What are they selling?
    * **Target Audience:** Who are they targeting?
    * **Pricing Strategy:** Are they premium, value, or somewhere in between?
    * **Distribution Channels:** Where are they selling (online, retail, etc.)?


**III. Key Metrics – Categorized**

This is the core of the plan.  Group your metrics into categories for clarity:

**A. Market Share & Sales Performance**

* **Our Market Share:** (Percentage of total sales in your segment) – Track this regularly.
* **Competitor Market Share:** (Estimate based on industry reports, revenue data, etc.) – This requires ongoing research.
* **Sales Growth (Our & Competitors):** Identify trends and see if competitors are outperforming.
* **Sales Volume:** Track units sold to understand relative performance.

**B. Marketing & Brand Performance**

* **Website Traffic:** (Total visits, unique visitors, bounce rate, time on site) – Track for all competitors.
* **Social Media Metrics:** (Follower count, engagement rate - likes, shares, comments, mentions) - Critical for brand awareness.
* **Brand Sentiment:** (Monitor online reviews, social media conversations, and forum discussions – use tools like Mention, Brandwatch, or even manual searches). - Gauge public perception.
* **SEO Rankings:** (Keyword rankings - Track against competitors' key terms) - Vital for organic visibility.
* **Paid Advertising Performance:** (Impressions, clicks, conversion rates, cost per acquisition – Track against competitor campaigns if possible) - Measure ROI.
* **Lead Generation:** (Number of leads generated, lead quality, cost per lead) - Important for sales effectiveness.

**C. Product & Innovation**

* **New Product Launches:** (Track competitor product releases and their reception) - Stay ahead of the curve.
* **Feature Updates:** (Monitor changes to competitor products - are they adding features you offer?) - Identify potential threats or opportunities.
* **Customer Reviews & Ratings:** (Track reviews on platforms like Amazon, Trustpilot, Google Reviews) – Reflects product quality and customer satisfaction.
* **Patent Activity:** (Monitor competitor patent filings to understand their R&D focus) – Provides insight into long-term strategies.

**D. Pricing & Promotions**

* **Pricing Changes:** (Monitor competitor price adjustments – frequency and magnitude) -  Understand their pricing strategy.
* **Promotional Activities:** (Track discounts, bundles, contests, and other marketing promotions) -  Assess their competitive advantage
