**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of late 2023/early 2024 – Apple’s rules evolve!)**

1. **Violation of App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the *biggest* reason. Apple's guidelines are incredibly detailed and constantly updated.  A single violation can lead to rejection.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Content:** Apps that falsely claim to do something they don't, or trick users into spending money, are swiftly rejected.
3. **Intellectual Property Infringement (Copyright/Trademark):** Using copyrighted images, music, or trademarks without permission is a serious issue.
4. **Privacy Violations:** Apps collecting or processing user data without proper consent, clear privacy policies, or adequate security measures. This includes things like tracking without permission.
5. **Malware or Security Risks:** Apps containing viruses, malware, or vulnerabilities.  Apple is *very* strict about security.
6. **Functionality Issues:** Apps that crash frequently, don’t work as described, have bugs, or have poor performance.
7. **Inappropriate Content:** Anything deemed offensive, violent, sexually explicit, or promoting harmful activities.
8. **Lack of Required Information:** Failing to include all necessary metadata, screenshots, videos, and descriptions (including App Store Category).
9. **Apple ID Issues:**  Problems with your Apple Developer Program account – unpaid subscriptions, security breaches, or violating developer agreements.
10. **Duplicate Apps:** Offering a functionally identical app to an already existing one (unless there's a very significant difference).
