## ASO A/B Test Plan for App Store Elements

This plan outlines an A/B testing strategy for optimizing key App Store elements – Title, Subtitle, Keywords, Description, Screenshots, and Videos – to improve app visibility, conversion rates, and ultimately, downloads.

**1. Goals & Metrics:**

* **Primary Goal:** Increase app downloads.
* **Key Metrics:**
    * **Conversion Rate (CVR):** Percentage of users who view the app page and then download. (Target: 1-3% improvement)
    * **Impressions:** Number of times the app page is displayed. (Target: Maintain or slightly increase)
    * **Click-Through Rate (CTR) from Search:** Percentage of users who click on the app in search results. (Target: 1-2% improvement)
    * **Install Rate (IR):** Percentage of users who click on the app and then install. (Target: 1-3% improvement)
    * **Keyword Ranking:** Monitor ranking for target keywords. (Target: Improve ranking for key keywords)


**2. Test Components & Variations:**

We’ll A/B test the following elements with variations designed to explore different approaches:

| Element        | Variation A (Control) | Variation B (Test) | Rationale                               |
|----------------|-----------------------|---------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Title**       | “[App Name]”          | “[App Name] - [Key Benefit]” |  Testing the inclusion of a benefit to improve appeal. |
| **Subtitle**    | “[App Category] App”  | “[Problem] Solution - [App Name]” |  Highlighting the app’s value proposition early. |
| **Keywords**    | [Existing Keyword List] | [Slightly Modified Keyword List] |  Testing variations in keyword combinations – consider synonym usage & long-tail keywords. |
| **Description** | [Existing Description] | [Rewritten Description with stronger calls to action] |  Focusing on clarity, benefits, and persuasive language. |
| **Screenshots** | [Current Screenshots] | [Screenshots with added visual focus & callouts] | Testing different visual arrangements and highlighting key features. |
| **Video**       | [Existing Video]      | [New Video showcasing a tutorial or benefit] |  Exploring different video content to engage users. |


**3. A/B Testing Methodology:**

* **Platform:** App Store Connect (Apple) or Google Play Console.
* **Testing Window:** 4-6 weeks per test. This allows for sufficient data collection and minimizes the impact of seasonality.
* **Traffic Allocation:**  We’ll use a 50/50 split for initial tests. As results become clearer, we can adjust the allocation based on performance.
* **Segmentation (Future):**  As the tests prove successful, we’ll segment based on demographics, device type, or country to refine targeting.

**4. Test Plan Timeline:**

* **Week 1-2:** Setup & Baseline Data Collection:  Run the control (A) for 2 weeks to establish a baseline understanding of app performance.  Collect data on impressions, CVR, CTR, and IR.
* **Week 3-4:** Introduce Variation B:  Switch to running Variation B (the test) alongside the control.
* **Week 5-6:** Data Analysis & Decision: Analyze the data collected during the test. Determine which variation performed better based on the key metrics.
* **Week 7-8:**  Implement Winning Variation:  Implement the winning variation across all users.
* **Week 9-10:** New Test Design: Start planning and designing the next A/B test based on the learnings
