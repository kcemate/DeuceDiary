**Retention Analysis Plan – D1/D7/D30 Benchmarks**

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Primary Goal:** Understand customer churn rates and identify key factors contributing to retention (or lack thereof).
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Track user behavior after onboarding to identify drop-off points.
    * Define and measure the impact of key interventions (e.g., onboarding emails, support interactions, feature releases).
    * Establish D1, D7, and D30 retention benchmarks as early indicators of success or potential problems.
    * Generate insights to improve user engagement and reduce churn.

**2. Data Sources:**

* **Product Analytics:** (e.g., Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics, Heap) – Core usage metrics (DAU/WAU/MAU, feature usage, session length, event tracking).
* **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot) – Customer demographics, contact history, support tickets, sales interactions.
* **Email Marketing Platform:** (e.g., Mailchimp, SendGrid) – Email open rates, click-through rates, and unsubscribe rates.
* **Customer Support System:** (e.g., Zendesk, Intercom) – Ticket volume, resolution times, customer satisfaction (CSAT) scores.
* **Onboarding Platform (If Applicable):** – Completion rates of onboarding steps, interactive tutorial engagement.

**3. Metrics & Definitions:**

* **Retention Rate (Overall):** Percentage of users who remain active after a given period.  Calculated as: `((Users at Time Period - New Users during Time Period) / Users at Start of Time Period) * 100`
* **D1 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to use the product within 1 day of signing up.
* **D7 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to use the product within 7 days of signing up.
* **D30 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to use the product within 30 days of signing up.
* **Cohort Analysis:** Grouping users based on their signup date to track retention trends over time.  Crucial for understanding trends.
* **Churn Rate:**  Percentage of users who stop using the product during a given period.  (Opposite of Retention Rate – often calculated monthly).
* **Voluntary Churn:**  Users who actively cancel their subscription.
* **Involuntary Churn:**  Users who cancel their subscription due to payment issues or account inactivity.

**4. Benchmarks & Targets (Initial):**

| Benchmark | Definition        | Target (Initial) | Rationale                               |
|-----------|--------------------|------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **D1**     | Return within 1 day | 60-70%           |  First impressions are critical; users must find immediate value |
| **D7**     | Return within 7 days| 30-40%           |  Early engagement is a strong predictor of long-term retention|
| **D30**    | Return within 30 days| 15-20%           |  Shows sustained value and product stickiness|

* **Important Note:** These are *initial* targets. They will be refined based on your industry, product, and customer base.  A B2C SaaS startup might have much higher D1 retention than a complex enterprise solution.
