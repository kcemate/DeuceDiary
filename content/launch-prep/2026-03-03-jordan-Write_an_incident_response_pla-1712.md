**1. Introduction & Purpose**

This Incident Response Plan (IRP) outlines the procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents affecting [Your Organization Name]. The goal is to minimize the impact of incidents, protect data and systems, and ensure business continuity. This plan is designed to be followed consistently across all departments and levels of the organization.

**2. Definitions**

* **Incident:** Any event that has the potential to violate security policies, compromise data, disrupt operations, or negatively impact the organization's reputation.
* **Severity Levels:**
    * **P0 (Critical):**  Immediate impact.  Severe disruption to critical business operations, significant data breach, or potential legal/regulatory violations. Requires immediate activation of the entire IRP.
    * **P1 (High):**  Significant impact. Moderate disruption to business operations, potential data breach, or noticeable security vulnerability. Requires immediate escalation and focused response.
    * **P2 (Medium):**  Limited impact. Minor disruption to operations, potential vulnerability requiring remediation, or suspicious activity. Requires investigation and remediation within a defined timeframe.

**4. Incident Response Process – The 5 Phases**

**Phase 1: Identification**

* **Trigger:**  Reports from users, security alerts, system logs, vulnerability scans, external notifications.
* **Actions:**
    * **Initial Assessment:** Determine if an incident has occurred.
    * **Reporting:**  Employees report suspected incidents via [Designated Reporting Channel - e.g., Help Desk, Security Email].
    * **Documentation:** Record all details of the reported incident (time, location, description, affected systems, users).

**Phase 2: Containment**

* **Actions:**  (Severity Level Dependent)
    * **P0 (Critical):** Immediately isolate affected systems from the network.  Activate backup systems.  Engage external experts (forensics, legal).
    * **P1 (High):** Isolate affected systems. Implement temporary security controls. Begin forensic analysis.
    * **P2 (Medium):** Identify and isolate affected systems. Implement security patches or updates. Monitor affected systems closely.
