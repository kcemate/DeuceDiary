**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

These reasons are based on Apple's App Review Guidelines, which are famously strict and evolving.  It's worth noting that the weighting of these reasons can shift slightly year-to-year, but these are consistently prevalent.

1. **Intellectual Property (IP) Violations:** This is *the* biggest offender. This includes copyright infringement (music, images, videos), trademark infringement, and using copyrighted game mechanics or assets without proper licenses.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Behavior:** Apps that don't accurately represent their functionality, collect data in a way that violates privacy policies, or trick users into spending money are routinely rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  This encompasses several things, including:
    *   Collecting more data than you disclose.
    *   Using tracking technologies without user consent.
    *   Not properly securing user data.
4. **Performance Issues:**  Apps that crash frequently, are slow, consume excessive battery, or have poor user experience are rejected.
5. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases, misleading pricing, or failing to comply with Apple’s payment standards.
6. **App Store Guidelines Violations (General):** This is a catch-all for anything that doesn't fit neatly into the other categories. This could be anything from misleading icons to using offensive content.
7. **Functionality Issues:**  Apps that don’t work as described, have broken features, or have significant bugs.
8. **Content Restrictions:** Apps containing explicit content (violence, nudity, hate speech) are automatically rejected.
9. **Technical Issues:** Problems with app security, app architecture, or the app failing to meet Apple's technical requirements.
10. **Lack of Metadata:** Missing or incomplete information about the app (keywords, description, screenshots) can slow down the review process and contribute to a rejection.
