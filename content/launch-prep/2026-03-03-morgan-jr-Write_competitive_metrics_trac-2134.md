Okay, let's craft a competitive metrics tracking plan. This plan focuses on building a system to understand how you're performing *relative* to your competitors, not just tracking your own internal data.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:** To identify opportunities for improvement and gain a competitive advantage by understanding where we're strong, weak, and how our competitors are positioning themselves.
* **Specific Objectives (Examples - Tailor these to your business):**
    * Identify competitor pricing strategies.
    * Determine competitor strengths in marketing and customer experience.
    * Track competitor market share changes.
    * Understand competitor innovation and new product launches.
    * Benchmark our key performance indicators (KPIs) against the leading competitors.


**II. Scope & Competitors:**

* **Define Your Key Competitors:**  Be specific.  Don't just say "Amazon."  List *direct* competitors (selling similar products/services) and *indirect* competitors (offering alternative solutions).  Categorize them by level of threat (High, Medium, Low).
    * **Example:**
        * **Direct:**  Company X, Company Y
        * **Indirect:** Company Z (offering a slightly different service)
* **Geographic Scope:** Where are your competitors active? (Local, Regional, National, Global)
* **Product/Service Focus:** Which specific offerings are you most concerned with tracking? (e.g., core product, related services, digital experiences)



**III. Metrics Categories & Specific Metrics:**

Here's a breakdown of categories and example metrics.  This is a starting point – adjust based on your business.

**A. Sales & Revenue:**
* **Market Share:** (Measured quarterly/annually) – A percentage of total market sales.  (Requires market research data - often purchased).
* **Revenue Growth Rate (vs. Competitors):** Track your revenue growth and compare it to the growth rates of your identified competitors.
* **Average Transaction Value:** How much are customers spending on average? (Compare to competitors)
* **Conversion Rates (Sales Funnel):**  Analyze the percentage of website visitors, leads, and qualified leads that convert into customers. (Benchmark against competitor data – harder to obtain, but worth the effort).

**B. Marketing & Brand:**
* **Website Traffic (vs. Competitors):** Use tools like SimilarWeb, SEMrush, or Ahrefs to estimate competitor website traffic.
* **Social Media Engagement (vs. Competitors):** Track follower counts, likes, shares, comments, mentions – across platforms your competitors use.  Sentiment analysis is valuable here.
* **Brand Mentions:** Monitor online mentions of your brand and competitors using tools like Google Alerts, Mention, or Brand24.
* **Advertising Spend (Estimate):** Try to estimate competitor ad budgets (difficult, but use tools like iSpot.tv to track their digital ad campaigns).
* **SEO Rankings:** Track your organic search rankings for key keywords and compare them to your competitors.
* **Content Marketing Metrics:**  Track traffic, engagement, and shares of competitor blog posts, articles, and videos.

**C. Customer Experience & Satisfaction:**

* **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Score:**  Measure customer satisfaction with specific interactions.
