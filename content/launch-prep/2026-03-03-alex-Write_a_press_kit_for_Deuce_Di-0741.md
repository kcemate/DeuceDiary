**About Deuce Diary**

“Deuce Diary” is a chilling, atmospheric indie horror film that explores the unsettling intersection of grief, obsession, and rural folklore. Written and directed by Elias Thorne, the film follows the story of Finn, a young man returning to his family’s dilapidated farmhouse after the sudden death of his twin sister, Lyra. As he attempts to process his grief and settle the estate, he begins to uncover a dark history surrounding the farm and the unsettling rumors of a local legend – the “Deuce,” a shadowy figure said to haunt the surrounding woods. 

Blending slow-burn suspense with striking visuals and a haunting score, “Deuce Diary” promises to deliver a truly unsettling cinematic experience.

**Synopsis:**

Finn returns to his family’s remote farmhouse in the Appalachian Mountains following the tragic death of his twin sister, Lyra. Overwhelmed by grief and haunted by fragmented memories of Lyra's final days, Finn attempts to inventory the property and begin the legal process of settling the estate. However, the farmhouse holds more than just memories. Strange occurrences, unsettling whispers, and a pervasive sense of dread begin to plague Finn’s investigation.  

He discovers an old diary – Lyra’s – filled with cryptic entries and unsettling drawings depicting the “Deuce,” a local legend of a shadowy figure said to prey on lost souls. As Finn delves deeper into the diary, his sanity unravels and he begins to question the reality of his own experiences, blurring the line between grief-stricken delusion and a genuine supernatural threat.  Is Lyra’s death truly accidental, or is she a victim of something far more sinister?
