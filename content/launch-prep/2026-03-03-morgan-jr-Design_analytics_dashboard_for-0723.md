# Design analytics dashboard for Deuce Diary launch metrics.

Okay, let's design an analytics dashboard for the launch of "Deuce Diary." We’ll aim for a blend of high-level overview and deeper dives, focusing on key metrics to assess success and guide future decisions.

**I. Dashboard Goals:**

* **Monitor Launch Performance:** Real-time and historical view of key metrics related to initial adoption and engagement.
* **Identify Strengths & Weaknesses:** Pinpoint what's working well and what needs improvement in marketing, content, and features.
* **Track User Acquisition:** Understand where users are coming from and their conversion rates.
* **Measure Engagement & Retention:** Gauge how actively users are engaging with the diary and if they are sticking around.
* **Inform Iteration:** Provide data-driven insights to optimize the product and marketing strategy.

**II. Dashboard Structure & Key Sections:**

We'll structure the dashboard into 4 main sections: Overview, Acquisition, Engagement, and Retention.

**A. Overview (Top Section - KPIs at a Glance)**

* **Total Users:**  (Big, bold number - daily/weekly/monthly) - The total number of users who've created a diary.
* **New Users (Past 7 Days):** - Tracks the volume of new sign-ups.  Crucial for gauging launch momentum.
* **Active Users (Past 7 Days):** - More valuable than new users – shows how many users are actively using the app.
* **Total Diary Entries:** - Indicates how much content is being created within the diary.
* **Average Diary Entries per User:** - Provides context on the level of engagement.
* **Daily/Weekly/Monthly Active User Trend:** - A line graph showing trends over time -  Critical for spotting patterns.

**B. Acquisition (Understanding Where Users Come From)**

* **Traffic Sources:**  (Pie Chart or Bar Chart) – Breakdown of where users are coming from:
    * App Store (iOS & Android)
    * Website (Organic Search, Paid Ads, Social Media Links)
    * Referral Program
    * Other (e.g., PR, Influencer Marketing)
* **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):** (Number -  for paid channels) – Shows how much it costs to acquire a new user through different channels.
* **Channel Conversion Rates:** (Smaller Bar Charts) – For key channels (e.g., Website -> Sign-up), track the percentage of visitors who become registered users.
* **Top Referring Sites/Social Platforms:** (List - clickable to drill down) – Reveals which sources drive the most traffic.


**C. Engagement (How Users Are Interacting)**

* **Daily/Weekly Diary Entries:** (Line Graph) – Track the number of entries created each day/week.
* **Average Entries per Diary:** (Number) – The average number of entries within each active diary.  Helps understand how deeply users are writing.
* **Most Used Diary Features:** (Bar Chart) -  Which features are most frequently used? (e.g., Photo Upload, Text Entry, Mood Tracking, Tagging). This will highlight popular aspects of the diary.
* **Time Spent in App:** (Average - for Daily/Weekly/Monthly) – Gauges overall session length.
* **Feature Usage Frequency:** (Heatmap or Table) - A table ranking the frequency of use for key diary features.

**D. Retention (Keeping Users Coming Back)**

* **Retention Rate (7-Day, 30-Day):** (Percentage) – The percentage of users who return to the diary after 7 and 30 days. *This is a key metric*.
* **Churn Rate:** (Percentage - calculated as 1 - Retention Rate) – The percentage of users who stop using the diary.
