**1. Understanding Your Target Influencers**

* **Subgenre Deep Dive:** Bathroom humor isn't monolithic. It ranges from dark comedy, observational silliness, relatable anxieties, and even absurdist scenarios. * **Platform Focus:** Where is your audience *really* active?
    * **TikTok:** This is CRITICAL for bathroom humor. Short-form, trending sounds, challenges, and rapid-fire edits are key.
    * **YouTube:** Longer-form skits, vlogs, or animated shorts have potential.
    * **Instagram:** Reels are similar to TikTok, but with a slightly more polished feel. Stories can be used for daily, quick jokes.
    * **Reddit:** Subreddits like r/funny, r/Showerthoughts, r/comedy are goldmines for engagement and finding creators.
* **Audience Size & Engagement:** Don’t just look at follower count. A smaller account with 10k *highly engaged* followers is far more valuable than 100k accounts with minimal interaction.  Pay attention to:
    * **Like-to-Follow Ratio:**  Are they genuinely engaged?
    * **Comment Engagement:** Do they respond to comments? Do their comments spark conversations?
    * **Average View/Watch Time:** Shows their content holds attention.


**2. Identifying Potential Influencers**

* **Keyword Search:** Use relevant hashtags (#bathroomhumor, #showerthoughts, #darkcomedy, #relatablestory, etc.) on TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram to find creators.

* **Tools (Use Sparingly - Authenticity is Key):**

**3. Outreach Strategy - The Personalized Approach**

* **Start Small:** Don't immediately target mega-influencers. Micro-influencers (1k - 10k followers) often have higher engagement rates and are more receptive to collaborations.
