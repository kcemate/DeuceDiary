**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store**

1. **Violation of App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the biggest offender. Apple's guidelines are incredibly detailed and cover a *huge* range of topics. Failing to meet even one aspect can lead to rejection.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Content:** Apps that falsely advertise features, promise results that are impossible, or trick users into spending money are swiftly rejected.
3. **Inappropriate Content:** This includes sexually suggestive content, violence, hate speech, and anything that could be considered offensive or harmful.
4. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting and handling user data without proper transparency, consent, and security is a major issue.  This covers everything from tracking location to accessing contacts.
5. **Functionality Issues:** Apps must actually *work* as advertised.  Bugs, crashes, performance problems, and broken features are common rejection reasons.
6. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted material (music, images, code) without permission is a serious violation.
7. **Circumventing Apple Pay/In-App Purchases:** Trying to find ways to avoid Apple’s in-app purchase system or circumventing payment mechanisms is strictly forbidden.
8. **Malware/Security Risks:** Apps that contain malware, viruses, or otherwise pose a threat to users’ devices or data will be rejected.
9. **Lack of Disclosure:** Not clearly disclosing required information like data collection practices, contact information, or terms of service.
10. **User Interface/Experience Issues:** While subjective, Apple’s guidelines dictate a consistent and intuitive user experience. Poor design, confusing navigation, and accessibility issues can lead to rejection.
