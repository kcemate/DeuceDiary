**Phase 1: Define Your Objectives & Goals**

* **What are you trying to achieve with segmentation?** Be specific. Examples:
    * **Increase Sales:** Target high-value segments with tailored promotions.
    * **Improve Customer Retention:** Identify at-risk segments and proactively address their needs.
    * **Optimize Marketing Spend:** Focus messaging on segments most receptive to your offer.
    * **Develop New Products/Features:** Understand unmet needs within specific segments.
* **Key Metrics:** Determine how you'll measure the success of your segmentation efforts (e.g., conversion rates, customer lifetime value, engagement rates).



**Phase 2: Data Collection & Identification**

This is where you gather the information you need to define segments.

1. **Internal Data:** This is your most readily available resource.
   * **CRM Data:** Customer demographics (age, gender, location, income), purchase history, website activity, email engagement, customer service interactions.
   * **Sales Data:** Product preferences, average order value, frequency of purchase.
   * **Marketing Data:** Campaign responses, channel preferences.

2. **External Data:**  Enhance your understanding.
   * **Social Media Analytics:**  Insights into interests, behaviors, and conversations on platforms where your audience engages.
   * **Market Research:** Surveys, focus groups, and industry reports to validate assumptions and uncover new segments.
   * **Third-Party Data Providers:**  Consider demographic data enrichment services (carefully – privacy is crucial!).
