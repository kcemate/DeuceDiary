**I. Understanding the Current State (Assessment - Weeks 1-4)**

* **Goal:**  Establish a baseline understanding of where revenue is coming from, where the leaks are, and identify quick wins.
* **Activities:**
    * **Revenue Analysis:**
        * **Granular Breakdown:** Analyze revenue by product/service, customer segment, channel (e.g., online, retail, wholesale), and geography.  Don't just look at total revenue; understand *where* it's coming from.
        * **Cohort Analysis:** Track revenue generated by customers acquired in different time periods (cohorts) to understand lifetime value and churn.
        * **Sales Cycle Analysis:** Map out the entire sales process – from initial contact to close – to identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies.
    * **Customer Analysis:**
        * **Customer Segmentation:** Define your customer segments based on demographics, behavior, purchase history, and value. (e.g., high-value, price-sensitive, new customer, repeat customer).
        * **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) Calculation:** Estimate the total revenue you expect to generate from a customer over their relationship with your business.
        * **Churn Rate Analysis:**  Understand why customers are leaving and identify patterns.
    * **Pricing Analysis:**
        * **Competitive Analysis:**  What are your competitors charging for similar products/services?
        * **Cost-Plus Pricing Review:** Ensure your pricing covers your costs and provides a reasonable profit margin.
        * **Value-Based Pricing Exploration:** Can you charge more based on the *perceived* value to the customer?
    * **Channel Analysis:**
        * **Channel Performance:**  Evaluate the revenue generated and profitability of each channel (e.g., website, marketplaces, retail stores).
* **Metrics:**
    * Total Revenue
    * Revenue by Product/Service
    * Revenue by Customer Segment
    * Average Order Value (AOV)
    * Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
    * Churn Rate
    * CLTV
    * Conversion Rates (across key stages of the sales funnel)
