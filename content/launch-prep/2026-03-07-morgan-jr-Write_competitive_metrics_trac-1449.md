**I. Executive Summary**

* **Goal:** To proactively monitor competitor activity, identify trends, and inform strategic decisions related to product development, marketing, pricing, and overall market positioning.
* **Frequency:**  This plan calls for tracking at varying frequencies (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly) depending on the importance and volatility of the metric.
* **Responsibility:**  Assign a dedicated team or individual (Competitive Intelligence Analyst, Marketing Analyst, Product Manager, etc.) to oversee the tracking and analysis.
* **Tools:**  This plan utilizes a combination of free and paid tools – we'll outline these in the Tools section.


**II. Scope & Competitive Identification**

* **Define “Competitive”:**  Don’t just think about direct competitors. Consider:
    * **Direct Competitors:** Companies offering similar products/services to the same target audience.
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Companies offering alternative solutions to the same customer need.
    * **Potential Competitors:** New entrants, startups, or companies expanding into your market.
* **Create a Competitor Matrix:**  A table to list your key competitors and track data across various categories (more on this in Metrics section).  Start with 3-5 key competitors initially, and expand as needed.
* **Regular Review:**  Reassess your competitor list every 6-12 months to ensure it’s still accurate and relevant.
