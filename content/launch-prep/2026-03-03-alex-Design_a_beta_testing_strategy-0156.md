**I. Overall Goals & Philosophy**

* **Focus on Core Functionality & User Flow:** We're not aiming for perfect polish in the beta phase. Instead, we need to validate the core diary functionality, ensure intuitive navigation, and understand how users are *actually* using the app for journaling.
* **Feedback Quality over Quantity:**  A small, highly engaged beta group providing detailed, specific feedback is far more valuable than a large group churning out superficial comments.
* **Iterative Improvement:** Beta feedback drives rapid iterations – ideally, we’re releasing small, focused updates based on feedback every 1-2 weeks.
* **Transparency & Communication:** Maintain open communication with beta testers - acknowledge feedback, explain decisions, and build a positive relationship.

**II. Beta Tester Selection (Phase 1: Core Group - 10-15 Users)**

* **Criteria:**
    * **Journaling Enthusiasts:** Individuals actively engaged in journaling, regardless of format (physical, digital, etc.).
    * **Tech Savvy (But Not Necessarily Expert):** Comfortable navigating apps, providing screenshots/screen recordings, and articulating their experiences.
    * **Diverse Backgrounds:** Aim for a mix of ages, genders, writing styles, and potentially different motivations for journaling (e.g., self-reflection, mental health, creativity).
    * **Willingness to Provide Feedback:** This is *crucial*.  We need people who actively want to help us improve.
* **Sourcing:**
    * **Initial Network:** Start with your development team, friends, and family who fit the criteria.
    * **Reddit Communities:** Target subreddits like r/journaling, r/selfimprovement, r/mentalhealth – carefully vetted for genuine engagement.
    * **Social Media:**  Post a targeted ad (small budget) on platforms like Instagram and Facebook, focusing on interests related to journaling and self-care.
    * **User Testing Platforms:** Consider using a low-cost user testing platform like UserTesting.com for recruitment.
