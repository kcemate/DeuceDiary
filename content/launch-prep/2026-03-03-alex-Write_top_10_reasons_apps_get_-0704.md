**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store**

1. **Violation of App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the *biggest* reason. Apple has very specific rules regarding content, functionality, user privacy, and more. A seemingly minor infraction can lead to rejection.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that trick users into paying for something they don't get, making false claims about functionality, or using deceptive advertising are almost guaranteed to be rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  This is getting *increasingly* strict. Apps must clearly explain how they collect, use, and share user data. Failing to provide a privacy policy or obtaining consent for data collection can be a huge problem.
4. **Intellectual Property Issues:**  Using copyrighted images, music, trademarks, or code without permission is a major red flag.
5. **Functionality Issues (Bugs & Stability):**  Apps that crash frequently, have obvious bugs, or don’t perform as advertised will be rejected. Apple’s TestFlight program is designed to catch these.
6. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases (incorrect pricing, broken purchase flows, etc.) are a frequent source of rejection.
7. **Content Restrictions (Adult Content, Violence, etc.):**  The App Store has very strict rules about content.  Apps containing explicit content, violence, hate speech, or promoting illegal activities are automatically rejected.
8. **Malware or Security Issues:**  Apps suspected of containing malware, exploiting vulnerabilities, or posing a security risk are swiftly rejected.
9. **App Store Metadata Issues:** Inaccurate descriptions, misleading keywords, or using inappropriate titles/screenshots can lead to rejection.
10. **Lack of Clear Value Proposition:** Sometimes, apps are rejected because they don’t clearly communicate what problem they solve for the user.
