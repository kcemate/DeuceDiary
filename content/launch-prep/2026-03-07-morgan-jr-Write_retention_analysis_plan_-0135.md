**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:** To understand the key drivers of user retention and identify opportunities to improve it.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    *  Determine the initial drop-off rate (D1).
    *  Analyze the churn rate after 7 days (D7).
    *  Evaluate retention at 30 days (D30) and identify potential issues.
    *  Identify key user segments with varying retention patterns.
    *  Develop actionable recommendations to improve long-term retention.


**2. Data Collection & Tracking:**

* **Essential Data Points:**
    * **User ID:** Unique identifier for each user.
    * **Registration Date/First Activity Date:** Date when the user first engaged with the product/service. This is critical for calculating time-based cohorts.
    * **Key Actions:** Track the specific actions users take within your product (e.g., creating an account, completing a profile, using a key feature, making a purchase, inviting a friend).  Categorize these by importance/impact on retention.
    * **Session Data:** Number of sessions, session duration, frequency of use.
    * **Engagement Metrics:**  Number of interactions, specific feature usage, content consumption (e.g., articles read, videos watched).
    * **Demographic Data (if available and ethically collected):** Age, location, industry (can help with segmentation).
    * **User Feedback:** Collect qualitative feedback through surveys, in-app prompts, or customer support interactions.
* **Tracking Tools:**
    * **Analytics Platform:** Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap, etc. – Choose one that provides robust event tracking and cohort analysis.
    * **CRM/User Database:** Store user information and track their interactions.
    * **Survey Tools:** SurveyMonkey, Google Forms, Typeform for collecting feedback.

**3. Cohort Analysis Methodology & Benchmarks:**

We'll use cohort analysis to track retention.  A cohort is a group of users who started using the product within the same period (e.g., all users who registered in January 2024).

* **Cohort Definition:** Define cohorts based on registration date (e.g., January cohort, February cohort, etc.)
* **Retention Metrics Calculated:**
    * **Retention Rate:** Percentage of users from a cohort who are still active after a specific time period.
    * **Churn Rate:** 1 - Retention Rate (represents the percentage of users who stopped using the product)
* **Benchmarks:**
    * **D1 (Day 1 Retention):**  This is the most important benchmark.  A low D1 retention rate often indicates a poor initial onboarding experience or a mismatch between expectations and reality.
        * **Target:**  Generally, a D1 retention rate of 50% or higher is considered good.  Anything below 30% is a serious concern.
    * **D7 (Day 7 Retention):** Reveals if the initial excitement translates into sustained engagement.
        * **Target:**  A D7 retention rate of 30-40% is often considered a good starting point.
    * **D30 (Day 30 Retention):** This represents the long-term viability of your product.
        * **Target:**  A D30 retention rate of 15-25% or higher is generally desirable.  This will vary dramatically by industry
