## Deuce Diary Launch: Crisis Playbook

This playbook outlines a tiered response strategy for the Deuce Diary launch, covering potential crises and prioritizing immediate actions. It's built around rapid response, transparent communication, and a flexible approach.

**Core Principles:**

* **Speed is Key:** Acknowledge and address issues immediately. Delaying response exacerbates problems.
* **Transparency:** Be honest with users. Don’t hide issues or make excuses. Own the problem.
* **Prioritize User Experience:**  Above all, focus on minimizing user frustration and demonstrating a commitment to their experience.
* **Team Coordination:** Clear roles and responsibilities are critical. Designated team leads are essential for each crisis.
* **Data-Driven Decisions:** Monitor metrics (server performance, app store reviews, social mentions) to understand the scope and impact of the crisis.


**I. Server Crash Scenario:**

**Severity:** High - Immediate Impact on User Experience

**Timeline:** 0-6 Hours

**Phase 1: Immediate Response (0-2 Hours)**
* **Team Lead:** CTO/Head of Engineering
* **Action:**
    * **Confirm & Isolate:** Verify the crash, determine the scope (affected users, region, feature).
    * **Rollback (If Possible):**  Immediately revert to the last known stable version.
    * **Communicate (Internal):** Notify key stakeholders – marketing, PR, support.
    * **Initial Status Update (Social/Website):** "We're aware of an issue affecting some users accessing Deuce Diary. Our team is working diligently to resolve it. We'll provide updates shortly." (Short, empathetic, and promising action).

**Phase 2: Active Resolution & Communication (2-6 Hours)**
* **Team Lead:** CTO/Head of Engineering, PR Lead
* **Action:**
    * **Root Cause Analysis:** Determine the precise cause of the crash.
    * **Fix Implementation:** Implement a permanent fix.
    * **Regular Status Updates:** Provide frequent updates – every 30 minutes initially, then hourly.  Use social media (Twitter, Facebook, Reddit - relevant subreddits) and the Deuce Diary website.
    * **Dedicated Support Channel:**  Set up a temporary support channel (e.g., a dedicated Slack channel, temporary helpdesk) for users reporting issues.
    * **Consider Incentives (Small):** If the downtime is significant, consider a small, meaningful gesture – a temporary discount, exclusive content, etc.

**II. 1-Star Reviews & Negative Feedback:**

**Severity:** Medium - Potential for Negative PR

**Timeline:** 0-24 Hours

**Phase 1: Monitoring & Triage (0-4 Hours)**
* **Team Lead:** Head of Marketing/Community Manager
* **Action:**
    * **Review Reviews:**  Actively monitor app store reviews and social media mentions.
    * **Categorize Feedback:** Identify common themes (bugs, UI issues, feature requests, complaints).
    * **Prioritize Responses:** Focus on negative reviews requiring immediate attention.

**Phase 2: Engagement & Resolution (4-24 Hours)**
* **Team Lead:** Head of Marketing/Community Manager, Support Team
* **Action:**
    * **Respond Individually:**  Acknowledge each review/comment – apologize for the frustration, explain the situation, and offer a solution (e.g., troubleshooting steps, link to support documentation, promise of a fix).
    * **Flag Critical Issues:**  Escalate recurring issues to the development team for immediate investigation.
    * **Proactive Engagement:**  Post helpful tips, FAQs, and show that the community is being heard.
