## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines a strategy for consistently monitoring and analyzing your competitors to inform your own business decisions, identify opportunities, and stay ahead of the curve.

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:** To gain a comprehensive understanding of the competitive landscape and leverage that knowledge to improve our product, marketing, and strategy.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key competitors (direct and indirect).
    * Track their product offerings, pricing, and features.
    * Monitor their marketing activities and messaging.
    * Analyze their strengths and weaknesses relative to our business.
    * Identify emerging trends and potential threats.
    * Inform product roadmap, pricing strategy, and marketing campaigns.

**2. Competitor Identification & Prioritization:**

* **Direct Competitors:** Companies offering the same or very similar products/services to the same target audience. (e.g., Coca-Cola vs. Pepsi)
* **Indirect Competitors:** Companies offering alternative solutions that address the same customer need. (e.g., Tea vs. Coffee for a morning beverage)
* **Prioritization Matrix:** Create a matrix to prioritize competitors based on:
    * **Market Share:** Higher market share = higher priority.
    * **Growth Rate:** Rapid growth = higher priority.
    * **Strategic Importance:**  Competitors directly threatening our core business = higher priority.
    * **Innovation Level:**  Companies consistently introducing new features/products = higher priority.
* **Initial List:** Start with a list of 3-5 primary competitors and expand as needed.

**3. Key Metrics Tracking - Categorized:**

This section outlines the key metrics to track, broken down by category:

**A. Product & Features:**

* **New Product Launches:** Track announcements, release dates, and key features. (Source: Press releases, company websites, product blogs)
* **Feature Updates:** Monitor updates to existing products – frequency, scope, and impact. (Source: Release notes, blog posts, social media)
* **Product Roadmap:**  If available, track their announced roadmap to anticipate future developments. (Source: Company blogs, investor presentations)
* **Product Reviews:**  Monitor reviews on platforms like G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, and industry-specific review sites. (Source: Review websites, social media)

**B. Pricing & Packaging:**

* **Pricing Changes:** Record any changes to their pricing tiers, promotions, and subscription models. (Source: Company websites, email marketing, competitor websites)
* **Bundling & Add-ons:** Track any new bundles or add-on services they offer. (Source: Company websites, sales materials)

**C. Marketing & Sales:**

* **Website Traffic:** Utilize tools like SimilarWeb or SEMrush to monitor their website traffic, sources, and bounce rates.
* **Social Media Activity:** Track their engagement on platforms like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, and TikTok. (Key Metrics: Follower count, engagement rate, post frequency, sentiment)
* **Content Marketing:** Analyze their blog posts, ebooks, webinars, and whitepapers. (Key Metrics: Download numbers, website traffic from content, social shares)
* **Advertising Campaigns:**  Monitor their online advertising efforts (Google Ads, Social Media Ads) – keywords, ad copy, landing pages. (Source: Third-party ad intelligence tools)
* **Promotional Offers:** Track their discounts, coupons, and special promotions. (Source: Company websites, email marketing)
