**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:**  Identify key drivers of user retention (or churn) and develop targeted strategies to increase the percentage of users who remain active over time.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Determine the baseline retention rates for our product/service.
    * Identify segments of users with varying retention rates.
    * Pinpoint the stages where users are most likely to drop off.
    * Develop and implement targeted interventions to address churn risks.



**2. Data Collection & Tracking:**

* **Key Metrics:**
    * **Day 1 Retention:** Percentage of users who log in or perform a key action (e.g., create a profile, make a purchase) on their first day.
    * **Day 7 Retention:** Percentage of users who are still active (performing a key action) 7 days after their initial activity.
    * **Day 30 Retention:** Percentage of users who are still active 30 days after their initial activity.
    * **Activation Rate:** Percentage of users who complete a critical onboarding step or key activation process.
    * **Feature Usage:** Tracking which features users are using, and when they stop using them.
    * **Cohort Analysis:**  Group users based on their acquisition date (e.g., all users who signed up in June) to track retention trends over time.
    * **Churn Reasons (Qualitative):**  Collect reasons users leave (e.g., through exit surveys, support tickets, or direct feedback).
* **Data Sources:**
    * **Product Analytics:**  (e.g., Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics) - For tracking user behavior, event tracking, and feature usage.
    * **CRM:** (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot) - For user profiles, contact information, and marketing engagement data.
    * **Support Tickets/Feedback:**  For understanding user pain points and reasons for churn.
    * **Onboarding Data:** To understand how users are experiencing the initial product setup.
