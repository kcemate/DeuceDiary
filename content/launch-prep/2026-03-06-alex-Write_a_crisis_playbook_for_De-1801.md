**Crisis Playbook: Deuce Diary Launch**

**I. Core Principles**

* **Transparency:** Be honest and open with your audience. Trying to hide or downplay issues will almost always backfire.
* **Speed:**  A rapid response is crucial.  Time is your biggest enemy in a crisis.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge and validate the concerns of those affected.
* **Collaboration:**  Involve key team members (marketing, PR, community managers, legal) in all decisions.
* **Documentation:**  Meticulously record all communications, decisions, and actions taken. This is vital for legal defense and analysis.

**II. Potential Crisis Scenarios & Response Levels**

We'll categorize potential crises by severity:

**Level 1: Minor Issues (Within 24-48 Hours)** - Low Impact, Easily Resolved

* **Scenario 1: Website Glitches/Technical Issues:** Slow loading times, broken links, checkout errors.
    * **Response:**
        * **Immediate:** Monitor site performance using tools like Google Analytics, Pingdom, or UptimeRobot.
        * **Team:** IT/Web Dev Lead immediately investigates and fixes.
        * **Communication:**  “We’re aware of a minor technical issue impacting some users. Our team is working urgently to resolve it. We appreciate your patience!” (Social media, website banner).  Provide estimated resolution time if possible.
* **Scenario 2: Minor Social Media Backlash:** A few negative comments or questions that don't escalate.
    * **Response:**
        * **Community Manager:**  Addresses comments directly, politely, and professionally.  Focus on answering questions and clarifying points.
        * **Tone:** Empathetic and helpful.  Avoid defensiveness.



**Level 2: Moderate Issues (48-72 Hours)** - Moderate Impact, Requires Active Management

* **Scenario 1: Negative Reviews on Major Platforms (Amazon, Goodreads, etc.):** Several critical reviews highlighting a specific problem (e.g., pacing issues, character inconsistencies, offensive content)
    * **Response:**
        * **PR Lead:** Monitors review sites closely.
        * **Marketing Lead:**  Drafts a response acknowledging the concerns, reiterating the author’s intentions (if appropriate and legal), and offering a solution (e.g., a FAQ page addressing common criticisms).
        * **Author (if possible):**  Potentially respond to key reviews directly (carefully – consult PR).
        * **Communication:** “We’ve heard your feedback and appreciate you bringing these points to our attention. [ Briefly address the key concerns] We’re committed to delivering the best possible experience.”
* **Scenario 2: Social Media Trend Against the Book:** A hashtag or viral discussion expressing widespread dissatisfaction.
    * **Response:**
        * **Community Manager:**  Actively monitors the trend, identifies the root cause, and attempts to engage in a constructive conversation (if possible).
        * **PR Lead:** Develops a broader communication strategy to address the underlying issue.
