Okay, let's design an analytics dashboard for the Deuce Diary launch metrics. This dashboard will focus on tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) to understand user acquisition, engagement, and retention, crucial for a successful launch.

**I. Overall Dashboard Goal:**

To provide a real-time and historical view of Deuce Diary's performance, highlighting trends, identifying areas for improvement, and enabling data-driven decisions about marketing, product features, and user engagement strategies.

**II. Dashboard Sections & KPIs:**

We’ll break the dashboard into logical sections, each focusing on a specific area.

**1. Acquisition (Top Left)**

* **Headline KPI:** **New User Sign-Ups:** (Number of new users signing up daily/weekly/monthly) - *This is the most critical metric.*
    *  Trend Line Chart: Showing new sign-ups over time (7 days, 30 days, 90 days).
    *  Breakdown by Source: Pie chart or stacked bar chart showing where new users are coming from (e.g., App Store, Google Play, Social Media campaigns, referral programs, organic search).
* **Secondary Metrics:**
    * **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):**  (Total marketing spend / Number of new users) - Track efficiency of acquisition channels.
    * **Install Rate:** (Number of installs / Number of impressions) -  Important for paid advertising campaigns.
    * **App Store/Play Store Ranking Change:** Track daily/weekly changes in keyword rankings.



**2. Engagement (Middle Left)**

* **Headline KPI:** **Daily Active Users (DAU):** (Number of unique users who use the app in a day) - A key indicator of engagement.
    * Trend Line Chart: DAU over time (7 days, 30 days, 90 days).
    * Segmented DAU: (By Key Activities - see below)
* **Key Activity Metrics:**  (Tracked separately for deeper insights)
    * **Diary Entries Created:** (Number of new diary entries created daily/weekly/monthly) -  Shows content creation activity.
    * **Average Diary Entry Length:** (Average words per entry) - Indicates the depth of user engagement.
    * **Time Spent in App:** (Total time spent in the app per user, daily/weekly/monthly) - How long users are actively using the app.
    * **Feature Usage:** (Track usage of key features - e.g., Mood Tracking, Prompts, Photo Uploads, Sharing to Social Media) –  Helps identify popular and underutilized features.  Use counts and percentages.


**3. Retention (Top Right)**

* **Headline KPI:** **User Retention Rate:** (Percentage of users who return to the app after a specific period - e.g., Day 1, Day 7, Day 30) - The most crucial metric for long-term success.
    *  Cohort Analysis Chart:  Plot retention rates across different cohorts (users acquired on different dates). This will visually show how retention changes over time.
* **Secondary Metrics:**
    * **Churn Rate:** (Percentage of users who stop using the app) –  The inverse of retention.
    * **Uninstall Rate:** (Number of uninstallations / Total number of users) – Can indicate issues with the app.
