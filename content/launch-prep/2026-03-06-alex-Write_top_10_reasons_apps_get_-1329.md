**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of late 2023/early 2024)**

1. **Policy Violations (General):** This is the *biggest* reason. Apple’s App Store Review Guidelines are extensive and constantly evolving.  Violations can be technical or philosophical – anything from misleading claims to violating Apple’s stance on data privacy.
2. **Privacy Violations:**  Specifically, improper handling of user data, unclear privacy policies, collecting excessive data, or failing to obtain necessary user consent.
3. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that don't deliver on their promises, use manipulative tactics (dark patterns), or make false claims about features or capabilities.
4. **Performance Issues:** Apps that crash frequently, are slow, drain excessive battery, or consume too much storage space.
5. **Inaccurate Metadata:**  Incorrect app titles, descriptions, keywords, or category classifications that misrepresent the app’s content.
6. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted material (images, music, videos, code) without proper licenses or permission.
7. **Malicious Behavior:**  Apps that contain malware, viruses, or otherwise pose a security risk to users.
8. **Content Violations:**  Apps that contain inappropriate content (violent, sexually explicit, hateful, discriminatory) or violate Apple’s community guidelines.
9. **UI/UX Problems (Poor User Experience):**  This is evolving. While Apple used to be more lenient, a truly awful or confusing user experience can lead to rejection, especially if it significantly hinders functionality.
10. **App Not Meeting Apple’s Technical Requirements:** Failing to meet minimum OS requirements, having incompatible file formats, or not adhering to Apple’s development guidelines.
