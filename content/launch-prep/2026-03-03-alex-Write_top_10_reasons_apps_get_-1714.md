**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

Apple’s App Review Guidelines are notoriously strict. Here’s a breakdown of the most common reasons for rejection, grouped for clarity:

1. **Violating Content Guidelines (Most Common):** This is the BIGGEST culprit.  This includes:
   * **Adult Content:**  Anything sexual, suggestive, or exploiting children.
   * **Violence/Graphic Content:** Excessive violence, gore, or depictions of harm.
   * **Hate Speech & Discrimination:** Content promoting hate, discrimination, or violence based on protected characteristics.
   * **Misinformation:**  Spreading false or misleading information, particularly about health, safety, or politics.

2. **Privacy Violations:**
   * **Unjustified Data Collection:** Collecting user data beyond what’s necessary for the app’s core functionality *without explicit consent*.
   * **Lack of Transparency:** Not clearly explaining in the privacy policy what data is collected, how it’s used, and with whom it’s shared.
   * **Tracking Without Permission:** Utilizing tracking technologies (e.g., IDFA) without user consent.

3. **Functionality & Performance Issues:**
   * **Bugs & Crashes:**  App crashes or significant bugs are a major red flag.
   * **Poor User Experience:**  Difficult navigation, confusing UI, or generally frustrating usage.
   * **Unresponsive App:** App takes an excessively long time to load or respond to user actions.

4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:**
   * **Copyrighted Material:** Using images, music, or other content without proper licenses.
   * **Trademark Infringement:** Using a name or logo that is confusingly similar to an existing trademark.

5. **Misleading App Store Listing:**
   * **False Claims:**  Making exaggerated or misleading claims about the app's features or capabilities.
   * **Misleading Screenshots/Videos:** Showing the app doing things it doesn't actually do.

6. **Payment Issues:**
    * **Incorrect Pricing:** Setting the wrong price for the app or in-app purchases.
    * **Fraudulent Activities:**  Suspect payment processing methods.

7. **App Store Guidelines Violations (General):**  This is a catch-all for anything that doesn't fit neatly into the other categories. Apple has broad discretion here.

8. **Malicious Code/Security Concerns:**  Apps with vulnerabilities that could compromise user data or device security.

9. **Terms of Service Violations:**  Not adhering to Apple’s terms of service for developers.

10. **Duplicate Apps:**  Submitting an app that is substantially similar to an existing one without a legitimate differentiating factor.
