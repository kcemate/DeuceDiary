**I. Overall Philosophy:**

* **User-Centric:** Every decision should be driven by understanding user behavior, value perception, and willingness to pay.
* **Iterative Testing:** Treat everything as a hypothesis. Run experiments, analyze results, and adapt quickly.
* **Data-Driven:** Use analytics to guide every step, from understanding user cohorts to measuring the impact of pricing changes.
* **Phased Rollout:** Introduce changes gradually to minimize disruption and allow for course correction.


**II. Paywall Strategy - When & How**

* **Trigger Points for Paywall Display:**
    * **Session Duration:** After a certain amount of time (e.g., 15-30 minutes) in the free version - shows users are engaged and valuable.
    * **Content Consumption:** When a user has viewed a specific number of key pieces of content (e.g., 3 high-value articles, watched a certain amount of a video series).
    * **Behavioral Signals:**  Scrolling speed, time on page for particular sections (indicating interest), using specific advanced features.
    * **User Segmentation:**  Users who have consistently used specific features are more likely to convert.
* **Paywall Types:**
    * **Full Paywall:** Blocking all content after a certain threshold. (Risky – can lead to immediate churn)
    * **Progressive Paywall:** Gradually unveiling premium features or content. Start with limited access, then increase based on engagement.
    * **Metered Paywall:**  Users get a certain number of free articles/views per month, then pay for additional access. (Popular, but requires careful metering)
* **Personalization:** Dynamically adjust the paywall triggers based on individual user behavior and segment characteristics.


**III. Free Trial Length & Structure**

* **Length:** This *highly* depends on your product.
    * **Short (7-14 days):**  For products with a quick “aha” moment – demonstration of value within a short timeframe.  Good for SaaS, where users see rapid ROI.
    * **Medium (30 days):** Most common – allows users enough time to fully explore the product and understand its value.
    * **Long (60-90 days):** For complex products with a deeper learning curve (e.g., advanced software, courses).
* **Structure:**
    * **Limited Access:** Offer a subset of key features or content during the trial.
    * **"Sandboxed" Experience:** Allow users to try out a core workflow without restrictions.
    * **Onboarding & Tutorials:** Provide proactive guidance to help users quickly experience the value.
* **Trial End Messaging:**  Don't simply disappear! Send a targeted email reminding users of the value they're missing, offering a discount, and highlighting key features.
