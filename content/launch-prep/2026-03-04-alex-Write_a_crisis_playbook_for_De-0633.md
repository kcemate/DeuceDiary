**III. Potential Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Website/Server Outage**

* **Severity:** High – Immediately impacts user access and perception of launch success.
* **Immediate Actions (TL & CL):**
    * **Verification:** Confirm outage with IT/Hosting Provider. Determine root cause.
    * **Communication (PR):** Issue initial statement on social media acknowledging the issue and estimated resolution time. "We're aware of a temporary outage and our team is working hard to restore access to *Deuce Diary*. We’ll provide updates shortly."
    * **Regular Updates:** Provide updates every 30-60 minutes via social media, website status page (if available), and email to registered users.
* **Resolution:** IT resolves the issue. PR confirms resolution and apologizes for inconvenience.


**Scenario 2: Negative Social Media Reaction (e.g., backlash to content, perceived disrespect)**

* **Severity:** Medium – Potentially damaging to brand reputation and user engagement.
* **Immediate Actions (SM & PR):**
    * **Monitoring:** Constantly monitor social media for mentions, sentiment, and emerging issues.
    * **Initial Response (SM):** Acknowledge the feedback (even if negative) – “We’re seeing your concerns about [issue] and appreciate you bringing it to our attention.”
    * **Escalation (PR):** Draft a more detailed response addressing the specific criticism, outlining steps taken/being taken to address the issue, and reinforcing brand values. **LC approval REQUIRED before posting.**
* **Longer-Term:** Engage in constructive dialogue, address concerns directly, and demonstrate a willingness to learn.


**Scenario 3: Bugs/Technical Issues within the App/Website**

* **Severity:** Medium – User frustration and potential loss of interest.
* **Immediate Actions (TL & CSL):**
    * **Identification:**  Prioritize identified bugs based on severity.
    * **Communication (PR):**  Acknowledge the issue and confirm a fix is in development – “We’ve identified a bug affecting [function] and our team is working on a solution. We’ll let you know when it’s resolved.”
    * **Patch Release:**  Release a patch
