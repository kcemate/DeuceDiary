## Deuce Diary Launch: Crisis Playbook

**Executive Summary:** This playbook outlines our response strategies for potential crises during the launch of Deuce Diary, covering critical scenarios and prioritized actions. Speed, transparency, and a proactive, empathetic approach are key.

**I. Core Principles - Across All Crises:**

* **Acknowledge Immediately:** Don't ignore issues. Acknowledging a problem shows you're listening and taking it seriously.
* **Prioritize Communication:** Designate a single point of contact for internal and external communication.
* **Be Transparent:**  Honest, clear communication builds trust. Avoid jargon and sugarcoating.
* **Empathy:** Understand user frustration and acknowledge their concerns.
* **Data-Driven Decisions:**  Base your actions on accurate data, not speculation.
* **Document Everything:**  Record every action taken, communication sent, and decisions made.

**II. Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Server Crash**

* **Severity:** High - Immediate impact on user experience.
* **Trigger:** Unexpected downtime, increased server load, error messages.
* **Immediate Actions (0-30 mins):**
    * **Verify the Issue:** Confirm the server crash and its scope.
    * **Alert Tech Support:** Engage our engineering team immediately.
    * **Internal Alert:** Notify key stakeholders – marketing, PR, customer support.
    * **Status Page Update:** Post a clear, concise status update on our website and social media, stating the issue and estimated time to resolution. (e.g., "We're experiencing technical difficulties. Our team is working to restore service. We'll update you shortly.")
* **Ongoing Actions (30 mins - 4 hours):**
    * **Regular Updates:** Provide updates every 15-30 minutes on the status page and social media.
    * **Root Cause Analysis:**  Engineering team investigates the cause.
    * **Customer Support Monitoring:**  Customer support is prepared to answer questions and offer solutions (e.g., acknowledging the issue and providing an ETA).


**Scenario 2: 1-Star Reviews & Negative Feedback**

* **Severity:** Medium - Damage to reputation, potential for further negative reviews.
* **Trigger:** Consistent negative reviews across app stores, social media complaints.
* **Immediate Actions (0-60 mins):**
    * **Monitor Reviews:**  Actively monitor app store reviews and social media mentions.
    * **Identify Trends:**  Look for patterns in the negative feedback (e.g., bugs, confusing UI, missing features).
    * **Team Prioritization:** Engineering team prioritizes bug fixes based on frequency and impact.
* **Ongoing Actions (60 mins - 24 hours):**
    * **Respond to Reviews:**  Respond to individual reviews – thanking positive ones and addressing negative ones with empathy and a commitment to improvement. (Example: "We’re so sorry you’re experiencing [issue].  We're actively working on a fix and appreciate your feedback.")
    * **Feature Requests:**  Start collecting and evaluating user feature requests for future updates.



**Scenario 3: Apple Rejection (App Store)**

* **Severity:** High – Delays launch, significant setback.
* **Trigger:** Receiving a rejection notification from Apple App Store.
* **Immediate Actions (0-60 mins):**
    * **Review Rejection Reasons:** Carefully analyze Apple’s rejection notification.  Understand the specific reason(s) for rejection.
    * **Technical Support:** Immediately engage our technical support team specializing in Apple App Store guidelines.
    * **Documentation Review:**  Double-check that our app meets all Apple App Store guidelines, including screenshots, privacy policy, and terms of service.
