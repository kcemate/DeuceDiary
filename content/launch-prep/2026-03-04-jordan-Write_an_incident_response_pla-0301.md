**1. Introduction**

This Incident Response Plan (IRP) outlines the procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and learning from security incidents impacting [Organization Name]. The goal is to minimize disruption, protect data, maintain business operations, and demonstrate a proactive approach to security. This plan utilizes a tiered severity level system (P0-P3) to prioritize responses based on potential impact.

**2. Definitions**

* **Incident:** Any event that has, or could have, a negative impact on the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information assets. This includes but is not limited to malware infections, data breaches, unauthorized access, denial-of-service attacks, and system failures.
* **Severity Levels:**
    * **P0 - Critical:** Represents a severe threat with immediate and significant impact, potentially leading to major business disruption, significant financial loss, and/or legal ramifications. Requires immediate activation of the entire IRP.
    * **P1 - High:** Represents a significant threat with a substantial impact, causing noticeable disruption to business operations and potentially compromising sensitive data. Requires rapid activation of specific IRP phases.
    * **P2 - Medium:** Represents a moderate threat with limited impact, likely causing minor disruption and potentially impacting a small number of users or systems. Requires immediate investigation and mitigation.
    * **P3 - Low:** Represents a minor threat with minimal impact, typically detected through monitoring or alerts, requiring standard security procedures.

**4. Incident Response Phases**

This plan follows the NIST Incident Response Lifecycle framework:

**Phase 1: Preparation**

* Regularly update this IRP.
* Conduct regular security awareness training.
* Implement and maintain security tools and technologies (SIEM, IDS/IPS, Antivirus, etc.).
* Maintain accurate asset inventories.
* Establish communication channels.

**Phase 2: Identification**

* **Trigger:**  Alerts from security systems, user reports, vulnerability scans, or external notifications.
* **Triage:** Security Analyst assesses the initial report to determine if it’s an incident and its potential severity.
* **Documentation:** Record all details of the incident, including time, date, source, and affected systems.
