This plan outlines the key steps, metrics, and considerations for tracking and improving customer retention.  It’s designed to be a starting point and should be customized to your specific business and product.

**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:**  To understand why customers are leaving and proactively implement strategies to increase customer retention rates.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key drivers of churn (what’s causing customers to leave?).
    * Establish baseline retention rates for D1, D7, and D30.
    * Develop targeted interventions to improve retention rates at each stage.
    * Continuously monitor and refine retention strategies based on data insights.


**2. Data Collection & Tracking:**

* **Data Sources:**
    * **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):**  Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, etc. – This is your primary source for customer information, interactions, and purchase history.
    * **Analytics Platform (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude):** Track user behavior within your product –  feature usage, time spent, conversion rates, etc.
    * **Support Tickets:**  Capture reasons for customer issues, complaints, and requests for help.
    * **Survey Data:**  Net Promoter Score (NPS), Customer Satisfaction (CSAT), Exit Surveys (for customers who are leaving) – Qualitative and quantitative data.
    * **Payment Gateway:** Track subscription status, payment failures, and cancellations.
* **Key Metrics:**
    * **Retention Rate:** (Number of customers retained / Number of customers at the beginning of the period) * 100% –  Track this at D1, D7, and D30.
    * **Churn Rate:** 100% - Retention Rate (This is the opposite of retention rate)
    * **Cohort Analysis:** Group customers based on their acquisition date (e.g., “January 2024 cohort”) to identify trends over time.
    * **Feature Usage:**  Which features are being used by retained customers vs. churned customers.
    * **Support Ticket Volume & Resolution Time:**  Indicates the ease of use and responsiveness of your product/service.
    * **NPS & CSAT Scores:** Measure customer sentiment and satisfaction.
    * **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV):** Estimate the revenue a customer will generate over their entire relationship with your business. (Useful for justifying retention investments)

**3. Benchmarks & Targets:**

| Benchmark Period | Target Retention Rate (%) | Justification                               |
|------------------|---------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **D1 (Day 1)**    | 80% - 90%                 | Initial excitement, setup phase; expect some drop-off.   |
| **D7 (Day 7)**    | 60% - 75%                 | Critical evaluation period; users determine value.  |
| **D30 (Day 30)**  | 40% - 60%                 |  Significant drop-off expected; need to address core issues.|

* **Note:** These are *targets*. Your starting retention rates will determine your initial targets.  Set realistic goals based on your industry and product.


**4. Analysis & Insights (Timeline)**

* **Phase 1: D1 - Initial Assessment (Days 1-7)**
    * **Focus:**  High-level retention rates, identifying immediate drop-off.
    * **Activities:**
        * Analyze initial signup conversion rates.
        * Identify early churn patterns based on demographics
