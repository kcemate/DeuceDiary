Okay, let's design a referral program with incentives. Here's a breakdown, considering different aspects and offering options for scalability.

**Program Name:** (Example) “Share the [Your Brand] Love” or “Bring a Friend & Get Rewarded”

**1. Core Structure & Mechanics:**

* **Referral Link/Code:**  Each referrer receives a unique referral link or code. This is *essential* for tracking.
* **Two-Tier System (Recommended):** This significantly increases motivation.
    * **Level 1 (Referral):** The referrer gets a reward for successfully referring a new customer.
    * **Level 2 (Referred):** The *new customer* also gets a reward for joining (typically smaller than the referrer's).
* **Tracking:** Implement a robust system to track referrals – ideally with an integrated platform or app that automatically recognizes referrals.

**2. Incentives - Tiered & Varied:**

This is the most important part! Offer a range of rewards to appeal to different customers.

* **Referrer Rewards (Level 1):** (These are for the person sending the referral)
    * **Option 1 (Smaller, Frequent):** 10% discount on their next purchase. (Good for building repeat business)
    * **Option 2 (Moderate, Milestone-Based):** $10-$25 store credit. (More impactful, attractive to higher-value customers)
    * **Option 3 (High-Value, Limited Time):**  Free Premium Feature Access for 6 months. (Great for SaaS or subscription businesses)
    * **Option 4 (Gamified):**  Points system – Refer a certain number of people to earn points that can be redeemed for rewards.
* **New Customer Rewards (Level 2):** (These are for the person being referred)
    * **Option 1 (Welcome Discount):** 10-15% off their first order. (Classic and effective)
    * **Option 2 (Small Gift):** Free small product sample or accessory. (Adds excitement)
    * **Option 3 (Tiered Introduction):**  New customer gets a discount *and* a small welcome gift after a certain period (e.g., 30 days of using the service).
* **Bonus Incentives (Optional - for Increased Engagement):**
    * **Referral Leaderboard:**  Publicly display top referrers (with their consent) to foster competition.
    * **Early Access:** Reward top referrers with early access to new products or features.
    * **Personalized Thank You:**  A handwritten note or personalized email from the company. (Adds a human touch).


**3.  Timeline & Rules:**

* **Referral Period:** Define a timeframe for the program (e.g., 3 months, 6 months). After that, the referral links might expire to maintain interest.
* **Earning Requirements:** Specify how many successful referrals are needed to unlock the reward (e.g., "Refer 2 friends to receive a 10% discount").
* **Eligibility Criteria:**  Clearly state who can participate (e.g., new customers only).
* **Redemption Rules:** Outline how rewards are claimed (e.g., using the referral code at checkout, contacting customer support).
* **Terms & Conditions:** Include standard terms (e.g., one-time use, restrictions on bulk referrals).

**4. Promotion & Communication:**

* **Website Placement:**  Prominently display the referral program on your website.
* **Email Marketing:**  Announce the program to your existing customer base. Create segmented emails for new customers encouraging them to participate.
* **Social Media:** Run targeted social media campaigns
