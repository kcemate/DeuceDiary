**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024)**

These reasons, while subject to Apple’s ever-shifting priorities, remain consistently prevalent:

1. **App Store Guidelines Violation (General):** This is the biggest culprit. Apple’s guidelines are extensive and complex. A simple misunderstanding or oversight can lead to rejection.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Content:** Apps promising unrealistic results, falsely claiming integrations, or tricking users into spending money are frequently rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:** Improper handling of user data (without clear consent, collecting excessive data, or failing to meet privacy regulations like GDPR/CCPA) is a major concern.
4. **Intellectual Property Issues:** Copyright infringement (using copyrighted images, music, or code), trademark violations, or similar claims are aggressively pursued.
5. **Malware/Security Risks:** Any app suspected of containing malware, vulnerabilities, or exploiting users is immediately rejected.
6. **Functionality Issues:** Bugs, crashes, poor performance, or features not working as advertised – these lead to rejections.
7. **Lack of Disclosure:** Failing to clearly disclose important information like data collection practices, third-party integrations, or subscription terms.
8. **Payment Issues:** Problems with the app store payment process, incorrect pricing, or failing to provide proper receipts.
9. **App Category Misrepresentation:**  Trying to categorize an app into a category it doesn't genuinely belong in (e.g., a complex simulation game appearing in the "Health & Fitness" category).
10. **Inappropriate Content:**  Anything that violates Apple’s standards for age appropriateness (violence, nudity, hate speech, etc.).
