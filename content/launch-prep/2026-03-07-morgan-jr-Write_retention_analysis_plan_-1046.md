## Retention Analysis Plan - With D1, D7, and D30 Benchmarks

This plan outlines a comprehensive approach to analyzing user retention, focusing on key milestones – Day 1, Day 7, and Day 30 – to identify areas for improvement and optimize the user experience.

**I. Objectives:**

* **Understand User Retention Rates:** Quantify the percentage of users who remain active after specific periods (D1, D7, D30).
* **Identify Key Retention Drivers:** Determine the factors most strongly correlated with continued engagement.
* **Pinpoint Drop-off Points:** Identify specific moments or events where users are most likely to churn.
* **Develop Targeted Interventions:**  Based on findings, create strategies to improve retention at vulnerable stages.


**II. Data Collection & Definition:**

* **Definition of "Active User":** Clearly define what constitutes an "active user" for your product. (e.g., logged in, completed a key action, used a specific feature). This is CRUCIAL for consistent analysis.
* **Data Sources:**
    * **Product Analytics:** Amplitude, Mixpanel, Google Analytics, Firebase – Track user activity, feature usage, and engagement metrics.
    * **CRM System:** Salesforce, HubSpot – Capture user demographics, onboarding data, and support interactions.
    * **Email Marketing Platform:** Mailchimp, SendGrid – Track email open rates, click-through rates, and unsubscribe rates.
    * **In-App Surveys/Feedback:**  Qualitative data to understand user motivations and pain points.
* **Data Points to Track:**
    * **New User Sign-Ups:** Total number of users who signed up during a specific period.
    * **Daily/Weekly/Monthly Active Users (DAU/WAU/MAU):** Measure user engagement over time.
    * **Feature Usage:** Track which features users are leveraging and how frequently.
    * **Churn Reasons (if available):** Collect data on why users are leaving through exit surveys or support interactions.
    * **User Demographics:**  Age, location, industry (where relevant), acquisition channel.
