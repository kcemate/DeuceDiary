**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of late 2023/early 2024)**

These reasons are based on common developer feedback and App Store review guidelines. Apple's guidelines are notoriously broad, but these are the most frequently cited issues:

1. **Violation of App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the broadest category and encompasses almost everything else. Apple’s guidelines are constantly updated, and simply claiming ignorance doesn't get you out of trouble.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:**  This includes false advertising, misrepresented features, or "dark patterns" that trick users into spending money or sharing data.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  Failing to comply with Apple’s privacy policies, collecting excessive user data without clear consent, or not properly disclosing data collection practices.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted images, music, trademarks, or game mechanics without permission.
5. **Malware & Security Issues:**  Apps that contain malware, viruses, or security vulnerabilities, or are designed to exploit users.
6. **Functionality & Stability Issues:** Apps that crash frequently, have bugs, or don't perform as advertised.  Apple's testing is stringent.
7. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases, including misleading prices, failing to honor subscriptions, or using non-compliant payment gateways.
8. **Content Violations:**  Apps containing inappropriate content (nudity, violence, hate speech, etc.) – even if it’s ‘just for kids’.
9. **Apple Trademark Infringement:**  Using Apple's brand names (like "iPhone," "iPad," "Apple Watch") in a way that suggests affiliation or endorsement.
10. **App Metadata Issues:** Inaccurate or misleading app titles, descriptions, screenshots, or keywords.
