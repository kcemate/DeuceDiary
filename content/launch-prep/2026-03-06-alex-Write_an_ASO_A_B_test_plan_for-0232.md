## ASO A/B Test Plan: App Store Element Optimization

**Goal:** To identify the most effective App Store elements (title, subtitle, keywords, description, screenshots, preview video, app icon) to drive installs, improve conversion rates, and enhance app discoverability.

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks (with ongoing monitoring)

**Metrics:**
* **Installs:** Primary KPI - Measuring the impact of changes on install volume.
* **Conversion Rate (CVR):** Percentage of users who view the app’s page and then install.
* **App Page Views:** Indicates interest and visibility.
* **Keyword Rankings:** Tracking position in relevant search terms.
* **Screenshot Click-Through Rate (CTR):**  Measures engagement with screenshots.
* **Preview Video CTR:**  Measures engagement with the preview video.
* **App Store Ratings & Reviews (indirect impact):**  A strong App Store presence often influences this.


**Testing Phases & Elements:**

**Phase 1: Baseline & Initial A/B Tests (Weeks 1-2)**

* **Element Tested:** Title & Subtitle
* **Variation A (Control):** Current Title & Subtitle
* **Variation B:**  Alternative Title & Subtitle –  Focus on benefits, strong value proposition, and relevant keywords. (Example: Instead of "Fitness Tracker," try “Boost Your Fitness - Track Progress & Reach Goals”)
* **Testing Approach:** Split traffic between A & B using the App Store Connect A/B testing feature.
* **Monitoring:**  Track Installs, CVR, and Keyword Rankings daily.
* **Analysis:**  After 2 weeks, identify the winning title/subtitle based on install performance and keyword rankings.


**Phase 2: Expanding the Test (Weeks 3-4)**

* **Element Tested:** Keywords & Description
* **Variation A (Control):** Existing Keywords & Description
* **Variation B:**  Revised Keywords & Description –  Refine keywords based on Phase 1 learnings, incorporate user language (benefit-oriented), and expand on key features. (e.g., Use long-tail keywords)
* **Testing Approach:** Continue using App Store Connect A/B testing. Introduce a small group of new, high-potential keywords.
* **Monitoring:** Track Installs, CVR, App Page Views, and Keyword Rankings.
* **Analysis:** Identify the winning keywords and description based on overall performance and impact on keyword rankings.


**Phase 3: Visual Enhancements (Weeks 5-6)**

* **Element Tested:** Screenshots & Preview Video
* **Variation A (Control):** Existing Screenshots & Preview Video
* **Variation B:** Revised Screenshots & Preview Video - Experiment with different visuals:
    * **Screenshots:**  Focus on core user flows, highlight key features, and showcase the app's benefit clearly.  Try 3-5 carefully selected screenshots.
    * **Preview Video:** Short, engaging video showcasing the app in action - best features, user interface, and problem-solving capabilities. (Under 60 seconds)
* **Testing Approach:** Split traffic – A/B test different screenshot combinations and preview video versions.  Focus on high-impact visuals.
* **Monitoring:** Track Installs, CVR, Screenshot CTR, Preview Video CTR, and App Page Views.
* **Analysis:** Determine which visual assets drive the highest engagement and installs.


**Detailed Breakdown & Considerations:**

* **A/B Testing Tool:**  Leverage Apple’s App Store Connect A/B Testing feature.  It’s the most direct and efficient way to test in-store elements.
* **Sample Size:**  Ensure a statistically significant sample size for each variation to avoid drawing inaccurate conclusions.  App Store Connect provides guidance.
