**I. Understanding the Landscape (Before Outreach)**

* **Identify Your Target Creators:** Don't just go for the biggest numbers.  Focus on:
    * **Niche Alignment:** Creators who *specifically* create bathroom humor, not just vaguely edgy content.
    * **Engagement Rate:**  This is *crucial*.  High follower counts are meaningless if their audience doesn't interact. Look for creators with a 3-5%+ engagement rate (comments, likes, shares) on their posts.
    * **Content Style:**  Match your brand's voice. Are they dark and sarcastic, silly and slapstick, observational, or a mix?
    * **Platform Focus:**  TikTok is *the* dominant platform for this type of content, but YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and even Twitch (for live streams) could be relevant.
* **Research Their Audience:**  Spend time in their comments sections. What are people responding to? What’s their sense of humor like?  This informs your messaging.
* **Analyze Their Past Collaborations:** How do they work with brands? What types of products/services do they typically promote?


**II. Outreach Strategy – The Layers**

1. **Initial Contact (Warm & Personalized - Key!)**
   * **Don't send generic emails:**  Seriously, this will get you ignored.
   * **Start with a Direct Message (DM) on TikTok/Instagram:** This feels more authentic and less like a mass email blast.
   * **Personalized Message Template (Adapt this heavily):**
      * “Hey [Creator Name]! Huge fan of your [Specific Type of Humor - e.g., 'dark toilet humor,' 'relatable awkward bathroom moments'].  I especially loved [mention a specific video/trend they did].  We’re [Your Brand Name], and we’re building [briefly explain what you do - 1-2 sentences max].  I think your audience would really appreciate [relate to their content - e.g., 'our self-cleaning toilet bowl cleaner,' 'our ridiculously comfortable bath mats']."
   * **Focus on THEM, not just you:** Show you’ve *watched* their content. 

2. **Tiered Outreach Approach:**

   * **Tier 1: Micro-Influencers (1k - 10k followers)** - Highest potential for engagement and authenticity.  Focus on building relationships here.
      * **Offer:** Small gifts (branded merch, product samples), affiliate commissions, or a small flat fee for a review/sponsored content.
   * **Tier 2: Mid-Tier Influencers (10k - 100k followers)** - Larger reach, but potentially lower engagement.  May require a more substantial investment.
      * **Offer:**  Combination of product + commission, or a larger flat fee for a series of posts/shorts.
   * **Tier 3: Macro-Influencers (100k+ followers)** -  Highest reach, but can be the hardest to connect with authentically.  Generally require a significant budget.
      * **Offer:**  Full-scale sponsorships with clear deliverables and budget agreements.
