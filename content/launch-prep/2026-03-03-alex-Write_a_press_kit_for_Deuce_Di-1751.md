**About the Book:**

Deuce “Duke” Maxwell is spending his senior year of high school adrift. His best friend, Caleb, died a year ago, and the grief still sits heavy, a constant shadow over everything.  He’s stuck in a small town, working a dead-end job, and communicating mostly through a worn-out notebook he calls the “Diary.”  Through the diary’s pages, Deuce chronicles his daily life - the awkward encounters, the fleeting connections, the frustrating encounters with his overbearing mother, and the persistent ache of loss.  But as he writes, he starts to realize that moving on isn’t about erasing Caleb, but about finding a way to carry his memory with him, and maybe, just maybe, finding a reason to believe in the future.
