**1. Purpose:**

This Incident Response Plan outlines the procedures to be followed in the event of a security incident affecting [Organization Name]’s systems, data, or reputation. The goal is to minimize damage, restore operations quickly, and prevent future occurrences.

**2. Definitions:**

* **Incident:** Any event that has the potential to compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information assets.
* **Severity Levels:**
    * **P0 – Critical:**  Imminent and severe impact.  Potential for widespread disruption, significant data loss, major legal/regulatory consequences, or substantial reputational damage.  Requires immediate, full-scale response.
    * **P1 – High:** Significant impact. Likely to cause disruption to critical business functions, potential data breach, or requires immediate investigation.  Requires rapid response and escalation.
    * **P2 – Medium:** Moderate impact.  May cause some disruption to business operations, requires investigation and containment, and potential for limited data exposure.
    * **P3 – Low:** Minor impact.  Limited disruption, primarily affecting a single system or user.  Requires monitoring, investigation, and documentation.

**4. Incident Response Process (Phases):**

**Phase 1: Identification & Detection**

* **Monitoring:** Continuous monitoring of systems, network logs, security tools, and user reports.
* **Reporting:** Employees and systems should be trained to report suspected incidents immediately.
* **Initial Triage:** Security Analyst assesses the reported event to determine its scope and severity (using the defined levels).

**Phase 2: Containment**

* **Short-Term Containment:** Immediate actions to prevent further damage (e.g., isolating affected systems, disabling compromised accounts, changing passwords). – *Severity dictates the scope of containment.*
* **Long-Term Containment:** Implementing more robust measures to stop the incident's spread (e.g., firewall rules, network segmentation).
