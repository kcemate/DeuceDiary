**I. Understanding the Current State (Assessment - 2-4 Weeks)**

* **1. Define “Revenue”:** What exactly are we trying to maximize?  Is it:
    * **Total Revenue:** Overall sales dollars.
    * **Gross Revenue:** Sales before deductions (returns, discounts).
    * **Net Revenue:** Sales after deductions – the true profit potential.
* **2. Data Collection & Analysis:**
    * **Sales Data:**  Analyze past sales trends (by product/service, channel, customer segment, time of year, promotions).  Look for seasonality, correlations, and outliers.
    * **Customer Data:**  Segment customers based on:
        * **Demographics:** Age, location, income, etc.
        * **Behavioral Data:** Purchase history, website activity, email engagement, product usage.
        * **Value Segmentation:**  High-value, mid-value, low-value – based on lifetime value.
    * **Pricing Data:**  Analyze current pricing strategies, competitor pricing, cost of goods sold (COGS), and profit margins.
    * **Marketing Data:** Channel performance (e.g., paid advertising, social media, email marketing), ROI of campaigns.
    * **Operational Data:**  Identify bottlenecks in the sales process, fulfillment, or customer service.
* **3. SWOT Analysis:**  Identify Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats related to revenue generation.
* **4. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**  Establish baseline metrics. Examples:
    * **Conversion Rate:** Percentage of website visitors who become customers.
    * **Average Order Value (AOV):** Average amount spent per order.
    * **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV):**  Predicted revenue a customer will generate over their relationship with the business.
    * **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Cost of acquiring a new customer.
    * **Revenue per Employee:** A measure of sales productivity.
