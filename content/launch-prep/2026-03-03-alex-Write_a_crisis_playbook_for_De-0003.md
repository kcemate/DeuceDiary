## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

This playbook outlines a phased approach to handling potential crises during the launch of Deuce Diary, prioritizing swift action, clear communication, and a data-driven response.

**Phase 1: Immediate Response (0-24 Hours)**

* **Trigger:** Server Crash, Initial Negative Reviews (1-star), Apple Rejection
* **Priority:** Damage Control, Verification, Initial Communication
* **Team Lead:** Head of Marketing & PR
* **Actions:**
    * **Server Crash:**
        * **Immediate (5 mins):** Alert IT & Hosting Provider - Understand the scope, root cause, estimated recovery time.
        * **Communication (15 mins):** Draft a brief, honest statement acknowledging the issue ("We're aware of a temporary service disruption and our team is working tirelessly to restore full functionality.") - Post on Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Discord (if applicable).
        * **Transparency (60 mins):**  Update status regularly (every 30 mins) on all channels.  Consider a dedicated status page if available.
    * **1-Star Reviews:**
        * **Review & Categorize (30 mins):**  Identify the common themes - bugs, usability issues, content complaints?
        * **Respond to Key Reviews (60 mins):**  Acknowledge concerns, apologize, and direct users to the support channel.  Don't engage in arguments. “We're sorry you're experiencing this. We appreciate the feedback and are actively working to improve the app.”
    * **Apple Rejection:**
        * **Review Rejection Email (15 mins):** Understand the specific reason for rejection.
        * **Assemble a Response Team (30 mins):** Include legal, development, and marketing – identify the exact issue and formulate a plan.
        * **Immediate Appeal (60 mins):** Submit a revised version to Apple with clear explanations and documentation.  Prepare for potential further communication.



**Phase 2: Stabilization & Assessment (24-72 Hours)**

* **Trigger:** Continued Negative Reviews, Apple Rejection Not Addressed, Competitor Copy, Initial Viral Trend
* **Priority:** Addressing Root Causes, Monitoring Sentiment, Strategic Response
* **Team Lead:** Head of Marketing & PR, Head of Product
* **Actions:**
    * **Review Monitoring:**  Use social listening tools (Hootsuite, Brand24, etc.) to track sentiment & identify emerging issues.
    * **Competitor Copy:** Investigate the source. Is it a legitimate copy or a derivative?  Consult legal counsel. Prepare a defensive statement emphasizing originality.
    * **Viral Trend (Positive or Negative):**  
        * **Positive Viral:**  Leverage the momentum - create shareable content, run contests, engage with users.
        * **Negative Viral:**  Immediately control the narrative - craft a statement addressing the concerns, identify key influencers to debunk misinformation, consider a PR outreach to relevant media outlets.
    * **Apple Rejection (Ongoing):** Continue to address Apple's concerns and submit revised versions.

**Phase 3: Long-Term Management & Proactive Strategy (72+ Hours)**

* **Trigger:** Sustained Negative Reviews, Repeated Crises, Significant Market Shifts
* **Priority:** Long-Term Strategy, Brand Reputation, Continuous Improvement
* **Team Lead:** CEO, Head of Marketing & PR, Head of Product
* **Actions:**
    * **Community Engagement:**  Establish a robust community forum/Discord server for direct user interaction and feedback.
    * **Product Roadmap Review:** Analyze feedback and prioritize bug fixes and feature updates.
