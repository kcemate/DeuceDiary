**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024 - rules can shift, so always check current guidelines!)**

1. **Violation of Apple’s App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the biggest one. It's a catch-all that encompasses everything else. The guidelines are extensive and cover everything from content, functionality, data privacy, and user experience.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Advertising:** Apps that make false claims about their capabilities or trick users into paying for unnecessary features are heavily penalized.
3. **Privacy Violations:** Failing to clearly explain data collection practices, obtaining proper user consent, and adhering to privacy regulations (like GDPR or CCPA) can lead to rejection.
4. **Security Vulnerabilities:** Apps with potential security risks – like hardcoded credentials, insecure data storage, or vulnerabilities that could be exploited – are immediately rejected.
5. **Functionality Issues:** Bugs, crashes, unresponsive elements, and a generally poor user experience can lead to rejection. Apple’s focus on a “polished” experience is key.
6. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted material (music, images, software code, etc.) without proper licenses is a major issue.
7. **Malware or Suspicious Behavior:** Any app exhibiting behavior that could be considered malware (e.g., unauthorized data access, sending user data without consent) is rejected.
8. **Inappropriate Content:** Apps containing offensive, harmful, or illegal content are, obviously, rejected.
9. **Lack of a Clear Value Proposition:** Apple wants to see that an app provides a genuine benefit to the user and is well-suited for the iOS platform. A vague or confusing app will be rejected.
10. **Metadata Issues:** Incorrect app name, description, keywords, category misclassification, or misleading screenshots can all trigger rejections.
