**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be upfront and honest with your audience. Denial and obfuscation will amplify negativity.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge and validate any concerns or frustrations expressed by readers.
* **Speed:** Respond quickly – initial reactions are crucial.
* **Accuracy:** Ensure all information released is accurate and verified.
* **Proactive Monitoring:**  Don't just wait for a crisis to hit; actively monitor channels.

* **Severity:** High - Immediately impacts user experience and potential sales.
* **Detection:** Monitoring website uptime (UptimeRobot, Pingdom), social media mentions, support tickets.
* **Immediate Action (Within 5 minutes):**
    * **Team Lead Activation:**  Notify relevant teams (Dev, Ops, Support).
    * **Initial Public Statement (Social Media – Twitter/X, Facebook):** “We’re aware of some technical difficulties on the Deuce Diary website. Our team is working hard to resolve this. We'll provide updates shortly.” (Include a link to a status page if available).
* **Short-Term Response (Within 30 minutes - 1 hour):**
    * **Detailed Status Page:**  Create a dedicated status page with estimated resolution time, identified issues, and troubleshooting steps.
    * **Regular Updates:** Post updates on all social media channels, acknowledging the issue and providing progress.
    * **Dedicated Support Channel:**  Ensure your support team is prepared to handle increased volume of inquiries.
* **Long-Term Response (Within 24-48 hours):**
    * **Root Cause Analysis:** Determine the cause of the problem and implement preventative measures.
    * **Post-Incident Report:**  Document the event, the response, and the lessons learned.
