**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024)**

2. **Intellectual Property Issues:** Copyright infringement (music, images, video) or trademark violations are frequent grounds for rejection.
3. **App Store Guidelines Violations – Category Misclassification:**  Submitting an app into the wrong category can lead to rejection, particularly if it doesn't meet the requirements of that category.
4. **Malware/Security Issues:**  Anything that poses a threat to user security – like malicious code, unauthorized access, or data breaches – is immediately rejected.
5. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting and handling user data without proper transparency, consent, or compliance with regulations (like GDPR or CCPA) is a serious concern.  This includes unclear privacy policies, excessive tracking, and improper data storage.
6. **Functionality Issues:** Apps that don’t perform as advertised, crash frequently, or have major bugs will be rejected.  Apple has increased scrutiny on this area.
7. **Lack of Essential Information:** Missing required information like app name, description, keywords, screenshots, contact information, or age ratings.
8. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases, subscriptions, or billing systems can trigger rejection.
9. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that trick users into spending money, engaging in unwanted behavior, or making false promises are rejected.
10. **Content Restrictions (Adult Content, Violence, etc.):**  If an app contains content that violates Apple's content guidelines (e.g., excessive violence, adult content, hate speech), it will be rejected.
