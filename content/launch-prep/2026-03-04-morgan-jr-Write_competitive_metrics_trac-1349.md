Okay, let's build a competitive metrics tracking plan. This will be a framework you can adapt to your specific business, industry, and goals.  It's broken down into phases and includes considerations for data collection, analysis, and action.

**I. Phase 1: Define Your Competitive Landscape & Objectives (Weeks 1-2)**

* **1.1 Identify Key Competitors:**
    * **Direct Competitors:**  Companies offering similar products/services to the same target market. (Be specific - "all online shoe retailers" vs. "Nike")
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Companies that meet a similar customer need, even if through a different product/service. (e.g., a meal kit delivery service vs. grocery stores).
    * **Emerging Competitors:** Companies that are currently smaller but show potential for growth or innovation.
* **1.2 Define Your Objectives (SMART Goals):** What are you trying to achieve by tracking competition? Examples:
    * **Increase Market Share:**  Gain X% market share within Y timeframe.
    * **Improve Brand Perception:**  Increase positive sentiment around your brand by Z% based on social media monitoring.
    * **Drive Sales Growth:** Increase sales by X% by understanding competitor pricing and promotions.
    * **Identify Innovation Opportunities:**  Uncover new features or services competitors are offering.
* **1.3  Map Competitor Strategies (High-Level):**  Start with a basic assessment of *what* they’re doing. This isn’t detailed research yet, just broad strokes. Consider:
    * **Product/Service Offering:** What do they sell? What are their key features/benefits?
    * **Pricing:**  Are they premium, budget-friendly, or somewhere in between?
    * **Marketing Channels:** Where are they advertising? (Social media, search, email, etc.)
    * **Customer Service:** What’s their reputation for customer service? (Reviews, social mentions)

This is where you gather the data.  Automate as much as possible.
