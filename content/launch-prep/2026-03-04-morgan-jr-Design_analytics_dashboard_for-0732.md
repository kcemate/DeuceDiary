**I. Overall Goals of the Dashboard:**

* **Real-time Visibility:** Provide a quick overview of the most important launch metrics.
* **Trend Monitoring:**  Track metrics over time to identify patterns and emerging issues.
* **Performance Evaluation:** Measure the success of marketing campaigns and identify which channels are most effective.
* **Data-Driven Decisions:**  Support informed decisions about content strategy, promotion, and user acquisition.

**II. Dashboard Sections & KPIs:**

We'll break the dashboard into several key sections, each focusing on a specific aspect of the launch.

**A. Acquisition (How Users are Finding Deuce Diary)**

* **Metric:** *Unique Downloads/Installs* (Most Important)
    * **Visualization:** Line Chart (Time Series) - Shows the trend of downloads over days/weeks.
    * **Target:**  Set a daily/weekly download target based on pre-launch projections.
* **Metric:** *Source of Downloads* (Breakdown by Channel)
    * **Visualization:** Pie Chart / Bar Chart - Show the percentage/number of downloads from each source:
        * App Store (iOS)
        * Google Play Store (Android)
        * Direct Website Download
        * Social Media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram - Track links & referral traffic)
        * Paid Advertising (Google Ads, Facebook Ads, etc.)
* **Metric:** *Cost Per Install (CPI)* (For Paid Campaigns)
    * **Visualization:**  Gauge or Number - Track the average cost per install for each paid channel.
* **Metric:** *Organic Downloads*
     * **Visualization:** Number - Track the number of downloads coming directly from searches and word-of-mouth.


**B. Engagement (How Users are Using Deuce Diary)**

* **Metric:** *Daily Active Users (DAU)*
    * **Visualization:** Line Chart (Time Series) - Key indicator of daily interest.
* **Metric:** *Weekly Active Users (WAU)*
    * **Visualization:** Line Chart (Time Series) - Provides a longer-term view of engagement.
* **Metric:** *Session Duration*
    * **Visualization:** Average/Median Bar Chart - How long users are spending in the app per session.
* **Metric:** *Screens Per Session*
    * **Visualization:** Average/Median Bar Chart - Shows how deeply users are exploring the app's features.
* **Metric:** *Feature Usage (Most Frequently Used Features)*
    * **Visualization:**  Stacked Bar Chart or Heatmap -  Identify which features are most popular (e.g., "Entry", "Journal Entry", "Mood Tracking", "Reminders"). This helps understand what's resonating with users.

**C. Retention (Are Users Coming Back?)**

* **Metric:** *Day 1 Retention Rate*
    * **Visualization:**  Percentage -  Percentage of users who open the app one day after installing.  *Crucial early indicator.*
* **Metric:** *Day 7 Retention Rate*
    * **Visualization:** Percentage - Percentage of users who open the app seven days after installing.
* **Metric:** *Day 30 Retention Rate*
    * **Visualization:** Percentage - Percentage of users who open the app 30 days after installing.  *Important for long-term success.*
* **Metric:** *Churn Rate* (Percentage of Users who Stop Using)
    * **Visualization:** Line Chart (Time Series) – Tracks the rate at which users are leaving.
