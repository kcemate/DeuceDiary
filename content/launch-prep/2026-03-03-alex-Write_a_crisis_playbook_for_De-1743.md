## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

**Objective:** To mitigate negative publicity and maintain a positive brand image during and immediately following the launch of "Deuce Diary." This playbook outlines roles, responsibilities, and response strategies for various potential crises.

**I. Core Team & Roles:**

* **Crisis Lead (CL):** [Name & Contact Info] - Overall responsibility, decision-making, communication coordination.
* **Social Media Manager (SMM):** [Name & Contact Info] - Monitors social media, drafts rapid responses, manages community engagement.
* **PR/Communications Manager (PRM):** [Name & Contact Info] - Handles media inquiries, prepares formal statements, manages external communication.
* **Legal Counsel (LC):** [Name & Contact Info] - Provides legal advice and guidance, ensures all communications are compliant.
* **Product/Creative Lead (PCL):** [Name & Contact Info] - Provides context and accuracy regarding the diary's content and concepts.


**II. Potential Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**1. Negative Social Media Sentiment (High Priority)**

* **Trigger:** Viral negative comments, trending hashtags, widespread complaints about content, perceived misrepresentation of the diary's themes.
* **Severity:** High – Rapid action required.
* **Immediate Response (SMM & CL):**
    * **Monitor:** Constantly track relevant hashtags, keywords, and social media platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Twitter, Reddit – identify key communities).
    * **Acknowledge:** Publicly acknowledge the concerns (even if vague) within 30-60 minutes: “We’re seeing your feedback and appreciate you bringing it to our attention. We’re taking this seriously.”
    * **Gather Information:**  DM individuals expressing concerns to understand the specific issues.
    * **Don’t Delete:** Avoid deleting negative comments unless they violate platform rules.
* **Medium-Term Response (SMM, PRM, PCL):**
    * **Craft a Detailed Response:**  Address the core concerns with empathy and factual information.  If errors were made, acknowledge them.  (PRM drafts, CL approves).
    * **Shift to Proactive Engagement:**  Answer questions, provide clarification, and demonstrate that the team is actively listening.
    * **Consider a Video Response:**  If the issue is complex or visually sensitive, a short, honest video from the Creative Lead can be effective.

**2. Content Controversy (High Priority)**

* **Trigger:**  Specific content within the diary sparks outrage (e.g., perceived offensive imagery, problematic themes, misinterpretation of intended message).
* **Severity:** Very High – Immediate, decisive action is crucial.
* **Immediate Response (CL, PCL, LC):**
    * **Assess the Issue:** Quickly determine the nature and extent of the controversy. LC advises on potential legal ramifications.
    * **Stop Further Content (If Necessary):** Temporarily pause production or release of any content that could exacerbate the situation.
* **Medium-Term Response (CL, PRM, PCL, LC):**
    * **Issue a Formal Statement:**  PRM drafts a clear, measured statement addressing the controversy, explaining the intention behind the content, and acknowledging any misinterpretations.
    * **Consider a Public Apology (If Appropriate):**  Only issue an apology if a genuine mistake was made and the team takes responsibility.
    * **Review Content & Messaging:** PCL reviews all related content for potential misunderstandings and adapts messaging accordingly.

**3. Technical Issues/Website Downtime (Medium Priority)**

* **Trigger:** Website crashes, issues with online ordering, errors in digital products (e.g., ebook downloads).
* **Severity:** Medium – Requires rapid technical and communication efforts.
