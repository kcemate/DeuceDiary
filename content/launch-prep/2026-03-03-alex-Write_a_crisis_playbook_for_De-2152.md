## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

**Document Version:** 1.0

**Purpose:** To outline procedures for handling potential crises arising during and immediately following the launch of *Deuce Diary*.
**Team Lead:** [Name & Title - e.g., Sarah Miller, Marketing Director]

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be honest and open with the public, even if the news isn’t ideal.
* **Speed:** Rapid response minimizes damage and demonstrates commitment.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge and validate the feelings of those affected.
* **Accuracy:** Verify information before sharing and correct misinformation quickly.
* **Collaboration:** Maintain clear communication and coordination across all teams.


**II. Potential Crisis Categories & Initial Response (Triage):**

| Crisis Category          | Severity Level | Initial Response (Within 15-30 minutes) | Responsible Team(s) |
|---------------------------|----------------|------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Technical Issues**       | High           | Monitor system status, deploy hotfixes, notify technical support, acknowledge on social media. | Tech, Dev, Ops |

| **Security Breach**         | Critical       | Activate incident response plan, notify legal & security teams, engage external experts. | Security, Legal, Tech |

**III. Detailed Response Protocols (By Category):**

**A. Technical Issues:**

* **Monitoring:** Utilize monitoring tools (e.g., New Relic, Datadog) to track performance.
* **Communication:**
    * **Internal:** Slack channel dedicated to outage management.
    * **External (Initial):** “We're aware there’s an issue with [brief description] and our team is working hard to resolve it. We'll provide updates as soon as possible.” (Social Media, Website Banner)
* **Updates:** Regular, clear updates on resolution progress – frequency based on severity.

**B. Negative Reviews/Backlash:**

* **Monitoring:** Utilize social listening tools (e.g., Brandwatch, Hootsuite Insights) to track sentiment.
* **Response:**
    * **Acknowledge:**  “We appreciate your feedback and are taking your comments seriously.”
    * **Address Concerns:**  Respond directly to reviews, offering solutions where possible. Don’t engage in arguments.
    * **Highlight Positives:**  Subtly promote positive reviews.


**C. Content Controversy:**

* **Immediate Action:** Pause all promotional activities.  Consult with Legal *immediately* for a thorough content review.
* **Assessment:** Determine the nature of the controversy – is it truly harmful or simply a difference of opinion?
* **Statement Preparation:** Draft a carefully worded statement acknowledging the issue, expressing concern, and outlining the steps being taken.  (Legal review is crucial).
* **Communication:** Controlled release of the statement through official channels.
