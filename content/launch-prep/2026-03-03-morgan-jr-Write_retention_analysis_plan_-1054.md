**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goal:**

*   To understand user churn patterns and identify key drivers of retention (or attrition) within the first 30 days of using our product/service.
*   To establish baseline retention metrics and set targets for improvement.
*   To inform product development, marketing, and customer support strategies.

**2. Methodology**

*   **Cohort Definition:** We will segment users into cohorts based on their initial signup date (e.g., all users who signed up in week 1, week 2, etc.).  This allows us to track the retention of a group of users who started their journey together.
*   **Data Sources:**
    *   **Product Database:**  Tracks user activity (e.g., logins, feature usage, time spent in app)
    *   **CRM (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot):** Stores user information, support tickets, and communication history.
    *   **Analytics Platform (e.g., Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude):** Tracks user behavior and provides cohort analysis capabilities.
*   **Metrics:**
    *   **Retention Rate:** Percentage of users who remain active after a specific period. (e.g., 7-day retention rate is the percentage of users who logged in at least once within 7 days of their signup).
    *   **Churn Rate:**  The inverse of retention rate (percentage of users who stopped using the product).
    *   **DAU/MAU (Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users):** A common engagement metric.  Trends in this ratio are important.
    *   **Time to Value (TTV):** How long it takes for a user to experience the core value proposition of the product. Shorter TTV generally correlates with higher retention.
    *   **Feature Usage:**  Analyzing which features users engage with most often, and whether feature adoption is correlated with retention.
    *   **Support Interactions:** Tracking the number of support tickets and their severity can highlight pain points.
