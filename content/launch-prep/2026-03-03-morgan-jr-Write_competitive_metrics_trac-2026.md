**II. Key Metric Categories & Specific Metrics**

We'll categorize metrics for better organization and understanding.  Here's a breakdown – adjust based on your industry and competitive landscape:

**A. Product & Features:**

* **New Product Launches:** Track dates, features, and initial reception.
* **Feature Updates:** Monitor frequency and scope of feature changes.
* **Product Roadmap Visibility:**  Attempt to identify competitor’s upcoming plans (using job postings, conference presentations, etc.)
* **Innovation Metrics:** Number of patents filed, research publications, new technology integrations.
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** Track sentiment and volume of reviews on major platforms (e.g., G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon).

**B. Pricing & Promotions:**

* **Competitor Pricing:** Monitor regularly (daily/weekly) for changes in pricing on key products/services.  Document variations by region/segment.
* **Promotional Offers:** Track discounts, bundles, free trials, and other promotional activities.
* **Payment Options:**  Note accepted payment methods – this can be a significant differentiator.
* **Subscription Models:** Track any shifts in pricing tiers or subscription terms.

**C. Marketing & Sales:**

* **Website Traffic:** Monitor competitor website traffic using tools like Similarweb. (Volume, trends, referral sources)
* **SEO Performance:** Keyword rankings, organic traffic, backlink profile (using tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs).
* **Paid Advertising:** Track competitor ad spend, keywords, and ad creative (using tools like SpyFu).
* **Social Media Activity:** Track follower growth, engagement rates (likes, shares, comments), content themes, and influencer activity.
* **Content Marketing:** Analyze competitor blogs, ebooks, webinars, and other content – assess topics, quality, and distribution.
* **Lead Generation Tactics:**  (If applicable) Track competitor lead magnets, landing pages, and email marketing campaigns.
* **Sales Cycle Length:** (If data is available) Comparing competitor sales cycle length can provide insight.


**D. Customer Perception & Brand Health:**

* **Social Sentiment:** Track positive, negative, and neutral mentions of competitors on social media platforms.
* **Online Reviews (Beyond Product Reviews):**  Monitor reviews on forums, industry-specific websites, and communities.
* **Brand Mentions:** Use social listening tools to track mentions of the competitor's brand name.
* **Customer Satisfaction Scores:** (If accessible - through industry reports or competitor data)
