## Influencer Outreach Strategy: Bathroom Humor Creators

This strategy focuses on building relationships with influencers who create bathroom humor content – think toilet jokes, awkward bathroom moments, cringe-worthy situations, and generally relatable, slightly uncomfortable humor. 

**I. Understanding Your Goal & Target Audience**

* **Goal:** Drive brand awareness, increase engagement, generate leads (if applicable), or promote a specific product/service (e.g., cleaning supplies, humor-related merchandise).
* **Target Audience:** Primarily millennials and Gen Z who appreciate dark humor, relatable awkwardness, and don’t take themselves too seriously. These creators often have a dedicated, niche following.
* **Brand Alignment:** Your brand *must* align with the self-deprecating, slightly unsettling nature of bathroom humor. A luxury brand likely won't fit.


**II. Identifying Potential Influencers**

* **Platforms:** Primarily TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and potentially Reddit (specific subreddits).
* **Keyword Research:** Use relevant hashtags on these platforms: #toiletjokes, #bathroomhumor, #awkwardmoments, #cringe, #relatable, #funny, #toilet, #pottytalk, #grosshumor.
* **Identify Creators with:**
    * **High Engagement Rates:** Don’t just look at follower count; focus on likes, comments, shares, and saves. A smaller, highly engaged audience is often more valuable than a large, passive one.
    * **Consistent Posting:** Look for creators who regularly post content in this niche.
    * **Authenticity:** Do their videos feel genuine and aligned with the vibe of bathroom humor?
    * **Similar Aesthetic:** Does their video style and editing fit your brand’s visual identity (if relevant)?
* **Tools:**
    * **TikTok Creator Marketplace:** Useful for finding TikTok creators.
    * **Heepsy:** Excellent for finding creators across multiple platforms.
    * **Upfluence:**  A more sophisticated platform for influencer discovery and outreach. (Can be pricier)
    * **Manual Search:** Don’t underestimate the power of scrolling through relevant hashtags and accounts.


**III. Outreach Strategy - The "Relatable" Approach**

* **Personalized Messages are Key:**  Generic emails and DMs will be ignored. Demonstrate you’ve actually *watched* their content.
* **Initial Contact - Build Rapport (Phase 1: 1-2 Weeks)**
    * **Start with a genuine compliment:** "I really enjoyed your video on [specific topic]. It was hilarious/relatable/weird."
    * **Reference specific content:** "I especially laughed at [specific detail in the video] – it's fantastic how you capture those awkward moments."
    * **Introduce yourself and your brand briefly:**  “I’m with [Your Brand] and we create [brief description of your product/service]. We admire your humor and think our brand aligns with your audience’s taste.”
    * **Don't immediately pitch:** Focus on establishing a connection.
* **Follow-Up & Collaboration (Phase 2: 2-4 Weeks - after rapport)**
    * **Offer value, not just promotion:** 
        * **Product Reviews/Sponsored Content:**  Offer a free product or service in exchange for an honest review or integration into a video.
        * **Content Collaboration:**  Suggest a collaborative video idea that plays into their style and your brand (e.g., a “Bathroom Hacks” video sponsored by your cleaning product).
        * **Contests/Giveaways:** Host a giveaway on their channel to generate excitement and engagement.
        * **Exclusive Discount Codes:**  Offer a discount code for their followers.
    * **Be Flexible:**  Be open to their ideas and creative input.  They know their audience best
