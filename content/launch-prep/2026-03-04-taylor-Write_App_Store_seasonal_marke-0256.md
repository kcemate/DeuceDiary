**Q1: January - March – New Year, New Habits**

* **Theme:** New Year’s Resolutions, Goal Setting, Self-Improvement, Productivity
* **January (Focus: Reset & Inspiration)**
    * **Date Range:** January 1st - January 31st
    * **Campaign Activities:**
        * **Launch Promo:** Announce your app as a tool for achieving New Year’s resolutions. 
        * **Social Media:** Focus on motivational content, tips related to your app's use, and user testimonials. Run contests related to goal-setting.
        * **App Store Optimization (ASO):**  Update keywords to reflect goal-setting/self-improvement.
    * **Metrics to Track:** App Store page views, Downloads, Social Media Engagement (likes, shares, comments), New User Acquisition Cost (CAC).
    * **Notes/Considerations:**  Lots of competition – stand out with a clear value proposition.  Consider a limited-time offer.
* **February (Focus: Love & Connections)**
    * **Date Range:** February 1st - February 28th
    * **Campaign Activities:**
        * **Highlight Features:** If your app relates to relationships, connection, or community, emphasize those features.
        * **Valentine's Day Promotion:**  Run a special offer (e.g., premium features discount) themed around love/connection.
        * **Influencer Outreach:**  Partner with influencers in relevant niches.
    * **Metrics to Track:** As above, plus Conversion Rates (for promotions).
    * **Notes/Considerations:** Be mindful of sensitivities – ensure your app’s features align with ethical and respectful relationships.
* **March (Focus: Spring Cleaning & Organization)**
    * **Date Range:** March 1st - March 31st
    * **Campaign Activities:**
        * **Content Marketing:** Create blog posts or articles related to productivity, decluttering (if applicable to your app), and starting fresh.
        * **ASO:**  Focus on keywords related to organization, productivity, and cleaning.
    * **Metrics to Track:** As above, plus Website Traffic, Blog Post Engagement.
    * **Notes/Considerations:**  Shift focus towards longer-term habits.
