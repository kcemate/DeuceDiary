**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

Apple’s App Review Guidelines are notoriously strict, and even minor violations can lead to rejection. Here’s a rundown of the most common reasons:

1. **Policy Violations (General):** This is the biggest category. It covers a huge range of things, but essentially, your app must adhere to Apple’s overall philosophy of providing a safe, high-quality user experience.
2. **Misleading Representations:**  If your app’s description, screenshots, or icon don’t accurately represent what the app *actually* does, you’re in trouble. “Free” apps that require in-app purchases to function are a frequent culprit.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting or using user data without proper consent, transparency, or following Apple’s Data Privacy Guidelines can lead to immediate rejection. This includes location data, contact information, and any data that could be considered sensitive.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:**  Using copyrighted material (music, images, fonts, software code) without permission is a huge red flag.  Even subtle similarities to existing apps can be problematic.
5. **Malware & Security Risks:** Apps containing viruses, malware, or vulnerabilities that could compromise user devices will be rejected. This includes suspicious code, exploits, or insecure data handling.
6. **App Behavior Issues:** Apps that crash frequently, have bugs that significantly impair functionality, or perform poorly are likely to be rejected.
7. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases (incorrect pricing, misleading prompts, failing to deliver purchased content) will trigger rejection.
8. **Advertising Restrictions:** Apple has strict rules around advertising, especially in apps for children. Ads must be clearly marked and adhere to Apple’s standards.
9. **Content Guidelines (Adult Content):** Apps containing inappropriate or sexually explicit content are strictly prohibited. Even seemingly innocuous content can be problematic if deemed too suggestive.
10. **Functionality Issues & User Experience:**  An app that’s confusing to use, poorly designed, or doesn’t meet user expectations can be rejected – especially if it’s inconsistent with Apple’s design principles.
