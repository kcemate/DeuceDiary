## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** October 26, 2023
**Purpose:** This playbook outlines the steps to take in the event of a crisis during the launch of *Deuce Diary*. It aims to minimize damage, maintain trust, and swiftly address issues impacting the book and its readers.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Open and honest communication is paramount.
* **Speed:** Rapid response is crucial to mitigating damage.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge and validate reader concerns.
* **Collaboration:** Involve key stakeholders – marketing, PR, editorial, customer support – throughout the process.
* **Documentation:** Meticulously record all actions taken, conversations, and decisions.


**II. Potential Crisis Scenarios & Response Levels:**

We’ll categorize potential crises by severity, with corresponding response levels:

**Level 1: Minor Issues (Low Impact - Immediate Attention)**

* **Scenario:**
    * Minor technical glitches (website downtime, limited availability).
    * Small number of negative reviews (factual, constructive criticism).
    * Social media chatter about minor bugs or questions.
* **Response Level:**
    * **Immediate Action:**  Assign a dedicated person (likely Social Media Manager) to monitor and respond in real-time.
    * **Communication:**
        * **Website/Tech Team:** Fix the issue immediately and post a brief update. “We’re aware of a minor technical issue and our team is working to resolve it.  Thank you for your patience!”
        * **Review Platform:** Monitor reviews, flag constructive criticism for editorial review.
        * **Social Media:** Acknowledge and address general inquiries, redirect complex questions to customer support.
    * **Timeline:** Within 30-60 minutes.


**Level 2: Moderate Issues (Medium Impact - Dedicated Team)**

* **Scenario:**
    * Significant website downtime impacting sales.
    * Surge of negative reviews detailing significant issues (e.g., corrupted files, shipping delays).
    * Viral negative social media comments regarding a controversial element in the book.
* **Response Level:**
    * **Dedicated Crisis Team:** Assemble a team including Marketing, PR, Editorial, and Customer Support.
    * **Communication:**
        * **Website/Tech Team:** Prioritize fixing the issue, provide regular updates on progress.
        * **PR Team:** Draft a holding statement addressing the situation – focus on empathy and commitment to resolution.
        * **Customer Support:** Train reps to handle increased inquiries with consistent messaging.
        * **Social Media:** Monitor closely, respond directly to comments, escalate to PR team if necessary.
    * **Timeline:** Within 1-2 hours (initial response), ongoing updates every 30-60 minutes.



**Level 3: Major Issues (High Impact - Executive Involvement)**

* **Scenario:**
    * System-wide outage preventing access to the book or website.
    * Widespread public outcry fueled by a significant controversy (e.g., offensive content, accusations of plagiarism).
    * Significant damage to brand reputation.
* **Response Level:**
    * **Executive Involvement:** CEO/Publisher immediately aware and involved.
    * **Crisis Communication Plan Activation:** Full crisis communication plan executed – PR team, legal counsel, and potentially a crisis PR firm.
    * **Communication:**
        * **Public Statement:** Release a formal statement acknowledging the issue, expressing concern, and outlining immediate steps being taken.
        * **Ongoing Updates:** Frequent, transparent updates via multiple channels – website, social media, email.
        * **Stakeholder Engagement:** Reach out to key influencers and media outlets.
    * **Timeline:** Within 1 hour (initial statement), continuous
