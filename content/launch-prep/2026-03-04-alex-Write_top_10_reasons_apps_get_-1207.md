**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

1. **Policy Violations:** This is the *biggest* reason. Apple's App Store Review Guidelines are incredibly detailed, and any deviation, no matter how small, can lead to rejection. This covers a broad range of areas.
2. **Misleading Representations:**  Apps that don't accurately represent their features, functionality, or data collection practices. (e.g., claiming a free app isn't free, or promising features that don’t exist).
3. **Privacy Issues:** Collecting, storing, or using user data in a way that violates privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA), or Apple's own guidelines, is a frequent cause of rejection.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted content (images, music, trademarks, code) without permission.
5. **Malware/Security Risks:** Apps with vulnerabilities that could expose users to malware, data theft, or other security threats. This includes overly broad permissions requests.
6. **Performance Issues:** Apps that are slow, buggy, crash frequently, or drain excessive battery.
7. **Lack of a Clear Value Proposition:**  Apps that don’t clearly communicate what they do and why users should use them.  It’s about the “hook.”
8. **Metadata Issues:** Incorrect or misleading app titles, descriptions, keywords, or age ratings.
9. **Functionality Issues:** Apps that don’t work as described, have broken features, or have a poor user experience.
10. **Design and User Interface Issues:** Poor navigation, confusing UI, or a design that doesn’t adhere to Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines.
