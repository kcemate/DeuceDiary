**Phase 1: Research & Identification (1-2 Weeks)**

* **Define Your Brand/Product:** Before you do anything, clearly articulate what you're selling. Is it a cleaning product? A novelty item? A subscription box? The more specific you are, the better you can target.
* **Identify Relevant Creators:** Don't just look for “meme accounts.” Look for these categories:
    * **Meme Accounts (Specifically Focused on Humor):**  Seriously, dig deep.  Don't just filter by follower count.
    * **Reddit Influencers:** Many subreddits (r/Showerthoughts, r/funny, r/memes) have active communities with influential users.
    * **TikTok Creators (Short-Form Humor):** Bathroom humor thrives on quick, relatable sketches and reactions.
    * **Instagram Storytellers:** Creators known for relatable daily life humor often have a strong following.
    * **YouTube Shorts Creators:** Similar to TikTok, but with a potentially slightly older demographic.
* **Tools for Research:**
    * **TikTok Creator Marketplace:** Good starting point, but filter *aggressively*.
    * **Upfluence, Heepsy, BuzzSumo:** Paid tools to find influencers and their engagement rates.
    * **Manual Searching:** Use relevant hashtags (#bathroomhumor, #relatable, #meme, etc.) on platforms.  See who’s getting engagement.
    * **Look at Competitors:** Who are they working with?
* **Qualify Creators:**  Don't just look at follower count.  Assess:
    * **Engagement Rate:**  Crucial.  A creator with 100k followers but a 0.5% engagement rate is less valuable than one with 10k followers and 5% engagement.  (Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments) / Followers)
    * **Content Style Alignment:**  Does their humor mesh with your brand? Are their videos/posts genuinely funny, or just reposting memes?
    * **Audience Demographics:** Make sure their audience matches your target demographic.
    * **Authenticity:**  Do they seem genuine?  Are their followers real?  Look for signs of bots or fake engagement.

* **Craft a Personalized Message:**
