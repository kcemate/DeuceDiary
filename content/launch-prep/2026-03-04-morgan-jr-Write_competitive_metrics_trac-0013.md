## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines a strategy for consistently monitoring and analyzing our competition, informing our strategy and identifying opportunities for growth.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Strategic Awareness:** Understand competitor activities, strengths, weaknesses, and emerging trends.
* **Opportunity Identification:** Spot gaps in the market or areas where we can differentiate ourselves.
* **Response & Defense:** Be prepared to react to competitor moves and protect our market share.
* **Performance Benchmarking:**  Compare our performance against key metrics to identify areas for improvement.

**II. Competitor Identification & Prioritization:**

* **List Key Competitors:** Create a detailed list of all direct, indirect, and potential competitors.
    * **Direct Competitors:** Offering similar products/services to the same target audience.
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Meeting the same customer need through a different product/service.
    * **Potential Competitors:** Companies that could potentially enter our market.
* **Prioritization Matrix:**  Rank competitors based on:
    * **Market Share:** Percentage of the market controlled.
    * **Revenue:** Relative financial strength.
    * **Growth Rate:**  Rate of increase in sales or market share.
    * **Innovation:**  Recent product/service launches or technological advancements.
    * **Customer Perception:**  Brand reputation and customer sentiment (as indicated by reviews, social media, etc.).

**III. Key Metrics to Track (Categorized):**

This is a core list - tailor this to your specific industry and business.

**A. Product & Service Metrics:**

* **New Product Launches:** Track frequency, features, and pricing.
* **Product Updates & Enhancements:** Monitor improvements and innovations.
* **Feature Comparison:** Document feature differences & perceived value.
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** Analyze sentiment on platforms like Google, Trustpilot, Capterra, etc.
* **Product Innovation Score:**  (Subjective, but track the frequency and significance of innovation efforts)

**B. Marketing & Sales Metrics:**

* **Website Traffic:** Overall visits, bounce rate, time on site, pages per session (use tools like Google Analytics).
* **Search Engine Ranking (SERP):** Track our ranking for relevant keywords.
* **Social Media Engagement:**  Likes, shares, comments, follower growth – across all relevant platforms.
* **Social Media Ads Performance:**  Impressions, clicks, conversion rates, cost-per-click (CPC), cost-per-acquisition (CPA).
* **Lead Generation:** Number of leads generated (tracked through CRM).
* **Conversion Rates:** Percentage of leads converting to customers.
* **Sales Volume & Revenue:** Track overall sales figures and revenue generated.
* **Average Order Value (AOV):** Average amount spent per purchase.
* **Marketing Campaign Performance:** Analyze ROI of all marketing campaigns (PPC, email, content, etc.).
* **Promotional Offers & Discounts:**  Monitor competitor pricing and promotions.

**C. Customer & Brand Metrics:**

* **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Score:** Measured through surveys.
* **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Measures customer loyalty and willingness to recommend.
* **Customer Retention Rate:** Percentage of customers who remain loyal over a specific period.
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Cost of acquiring a new customer.
* **Brand Mentions:** Track mentions of our brand and competitors across the web and social media (using social listening tools).
* **Sentiment Analysis:** Analyze the overall tone of online conversations about our brand and competitors.


**IV. Data Collection & Tracking Tools:**

* **Google Analytics:** Website traffic and user behavior.
* **Social Media Analytics Platforms:** (e.g., Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Buffer) –  Social
