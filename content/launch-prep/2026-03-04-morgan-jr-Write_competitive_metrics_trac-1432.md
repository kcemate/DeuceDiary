1. **Identify Competitors:**

**III. Key Metrics to Track (Categorized)**

This is where you’ll tailor the metrics based on your industry and business. Here's a breakdown with examples:

**A. Market Share & Sales Performance:**
   * **Market Share:** (Most important!) – Percentage of the total market your company controls. (Track this monthly/quarterly)
   * **Sales Volume:** Number of units sold.
   * **Revenue:** Total revenue generated.
   * **Sales Growth Rate:** Percentage change in sales volume/revenue over time.
   * **Win/Loss Ratio:** Number of deals won vs. lost (crucial for B2B).
   * **Average Deal Size:**  The average value of a won deal.

**B. Customer Acquisition & Engagement:**
   * **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Cost to acquire a new customer. (Track this monthly)
   * **Lead Generation Volume:** Number of leads generated.
   * **Conversion Rates:**  Percentage of leads that become customers at each stage of the funnel (MQL, SQL, Customer).
   * **Customer Churn Rate:** Percentage of customers who stop using your product/service. (Track this monthly)
   * **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Measures customer loyalty and willingness to recommend.
   * **Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT):** Measures customer satisfaction with specific interactions.
   * **Social Media Engagement:** Likes, shares, comments, followers across relevant platforms.

**C. Product/Service Performance & Innovation:**
   * **Feature Usage Rates:**  How frequently customers are using specific features.
   * **Customer Feedback (Reviews, Ratings, Surveys):** Track sentiment and identify areas for improvement.
   * **New Product/Service Launches:** Monitor competitor releases and their impact.
   * **Innovation Rate:** (Difficult to quantify precisely, but track the frequency of new product launches, feature updates, and technological advancements.)
