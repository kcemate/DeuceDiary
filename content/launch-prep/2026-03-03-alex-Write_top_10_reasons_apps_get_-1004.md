3. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting or using user data without proper consent, clear disclosures, and adhering to Apple’s privacy guidelines (especially around tracking) is a huge red flag.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Copying copyrighted material (code, images, music, etc.) without permission is a frequent cause of rejection.
5. **Payment Issues:**  Problems with in-app purchases, fraudulent payment methods, or unclear pricing are heavily scrutinized.
6. **App Stability/Technical Issues:** Crashes, bugs, performance problems, and a generally poor user experience are serious concerns. Apple requires rigorous testing.
7. **Content Issues:**  Apps containing inappropriate content (violence, nudity, hate speech, etc.) are immediately rejected.
8. **Advertising Issues:**  Excessive or intrusive ads, or ads that violate Apple’s advertising guidelines, can lead to rejection.
9. **App Store Guidelines Violations (Specific):** This encompasses a wide range of issues that don’t fit into the above categories – things like overly aggressive push notifications, violating rules about app categorization, etc.
10. **Lack of App Metadata:** Missing or incomplete information like app title, description, keywords, screenshots, and app preview video can lead to rejection.
