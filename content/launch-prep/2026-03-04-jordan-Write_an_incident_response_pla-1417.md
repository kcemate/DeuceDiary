**Purpose:** This Incident Response Plan (IRP) outlines the procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and documenting security incidents affecting [Your Organization Name]. **Guiding Principles:**

* **Prioritization:** Focus on immediate threats and impact.
* **Communication:** Maintain clear and timely communication with stakeholders.
* **Collaboration:** Foster teamwork and information sharing.
* **Continuous Improvement:** Regularly review and update this plan based on lessons learned and changes in the threat landscape.

**Definitions:**

* **Incident:** Any event that could potentially compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information or systems.
* **Severity Levels:**  Defined below based on impact and urgency.
* **Incident Response Team (IRT):** The designated group responsible for managing and executing the IRP.
* **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by an incident (e.g., executive leadership, legal, public relations).

**II. Incident Response Team (IRT) Roles & Responsibilities**

* **IRT Leader:** (e.g., Security Manager/CISO) – Overall command, decision-making, communication with stakeholders.
* **Security Analyst:** Investigates the incident, analyzes root cause, recommends containment and eradication strategies.
* **IT Support:** Provides technical support for containment, recovery, and system restoration.
* **System Administrator:** Responsible for system patching, configuration changes, and user account management.
* **Communications Lead:** Handles internal and external communications, manages public relations.
* **Legal Counsel:** Provides legal guidance and ensures compliance with regulations.
