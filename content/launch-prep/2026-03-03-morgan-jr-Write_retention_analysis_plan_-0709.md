## Retention Analysis Plan – D1/D7/D30 Benchmarks

This plan outlines a framework for tracking and analyzing user retention, focusing on key milestones: Day 1, Day 7, and Day 30. It includes data tracking, metrics, analysis, and actionable recommendations.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Understand User Behavior:** Identify patterns and key drivers of retention and churn.
* **Measure Product Value:**  Determine if users perceive the product as valuable enough to continue using it.
* **Optimize Onboarding:**  Improve the initial user experience to increase the likelihood of long-term engagement.
* **Target Intervention Strategies:**  Identify segments of users most at risk of churn and tailor interventions accordingly.

**II. Data Tracking & Metrics:**

* **Key Metric: Retention Rate:** Percentage of users retained after specific periods. (e.g., Day 1 Retention, Day 7 Retention, Day 30 Retention)
* **Segmented Retention Rates:** Analyze retention across different user segments based on:
    * **Acquisition Channel:** (e.g., Organic, Paid Ads, Referral) - Tracks which channels are driving the most sustainable users.
    * **User Persona/Demographics:** (e.g., Age, Location, Job Title) -  Reveals different needs and preferences.
    * **Product Usage:** (e.g., Feature Adoption, Frequency of Use, Session Length) - Highlights which features are most important to retention.
    * **Onboarding Completion Rate:** Measures the percentage of users who successfully complete the initial onboarding process.
* **Churn Rate:** The inverse of retention – percentage of users who stop using the product.
* **Cohort Analysis:** Group users based on their acquisition date to track retention trends over time.  This is critical for understanding the long-term impact of changes.
* **Event Tracking:** Track specific user actions within the product (e.g., profile creation, feature usage, content consumption) to understand user behavior and identify potential drop-off points.
* **Technical Data:** Track app crashes, errors, and network connectivity issues that may contribute to churn.


**III. Benchmarks & Targets:**

| **Timeframe** | **Target Retention Rate (%)** | **Rationale**             |
|---------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Day 1**      | 40% - 60%                    | Initial excitement & immediate value are key. Aim for a strong first impression. |
| **Day 7**      | 20% - 30%                    | Critical window - users are evaluating if the product meets their needs.  Low Day 7 retention is a significant red flag. |
| **Day 30**     | 10% - 15%                    |  Long-term engagement depends on sustained value. This is the most challenging benchmark. |

**Note:**  These benchmarks are *targets*.  Your specific targets will depend on your industry, product, and business model.  Establish benchmarks based on industry averages and competitor data.

**IV. Analysis & Reporting (By Milestone):**

**A. Day 1 Analysis:**

* **Focus:**  Onboarding Experience & Immediate Value
* **Questions to Answer:**
    * What percentage of users completed the onboarding flow?
    * Which onboarding steps led to the highest drop-off?
    * Are users immediately engaging with core features?
* **Report Frequency:** Weekly
* **Tools:** Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics (Event Tracking), CRM


**B. Day 7 Analysis:**

* **Focus:** Initial Engagement & Feature Adoption
* **Questions to Answer:**
    * Are users continuing to engage with the product?
    * Are users adopting the key features that drive product value?
