Okay, here are three email drip campaign sequences, each with a different goal and designed for varying lengths. I've included subject lines, email content, and estimated timing. Remember to adjust these based on your specific audience and brand voice!

**Campaign 1: Welcome & Brand Introduction (New Subscriber - 5 Days)**

* **Goal:** Introduce your brand, build excitement, and encourage immediate engagement.
* **Audience:** New subscribers who signed up via a lead magnet (e.g., ebook, free trial, webinar).

**Sequence:**

* **Day 1: Welcome Email (Subject: 🎉 Welcome to the [Your Brand Name] Family!)**
    * **Content:**
        * Warm welcome and thank you for subscribing!
        * Briefly reiterate the value they’ll receive.
        * Link to your website homepage and key resources.
        * Ask a quick question to encourage a reply (e.g., “What are you hoping to achieve with [your product/service]?”).
* **Day 3: Brand Story & Values (Subject: The Story Behind [Your Brand Name])**
    * **Content:**
        * Share your brand’s mission, vision, and history.  Focus on *why* you do what you do.
        * Include a compelling image or video (if possible).
        * Connect your brand values to what your audience cares about.
* **Day 5:  Resource Spotlight & Offer (Subject: Level Up Your [Area] with These Resources!)**
    * **Content:**
        * Highlight 2-3 of your most popular resources (blog posts, templates, guides).
        * Offer a small, low-commitment incentive to encourage engagement (e.g., 10% off their first purchase, access to a bonus checklist).

**Campaign 2:  Lead Nurturing – Problem/Solution (Mid-Stage Lead - 7 Days)**

* **Goal:**  Establish your brand as a trusted expert, address a common pain point your audience experiences, and subtly introduce your product/service as a solution.
* **Audience:** Subscribers who’ve engaged with your content (e.g., downloaded a guide, watched a webinar) but haven’t yet purchased.

**Sequence:**

* **Day 2: Recognizing the Problem (Subject: Struggling with [Specific Pain Point]? You're Not Alone.)**
    * **Content:**
        * Acknowledge a common challenge your target audience faces.
        * Validate their feelings – let them know they’re not the only one experiencing this.
        * Briefly describe the negative impact of this problem.
* **Day 4:  Digging Deeper – Tips & Insights (Subject: 3 Ways to Tackle [Pain Point] Today)**
    * **Content:**
        * Provide 3-5 actionable tips to help them address the pain point.
        *  Position yourself as an expert offering valuable advice.
* **Day 6: Introduce Your Solution (Subject:  Finally, a Simple Way to [Solve the Pain Point])**
    * **Content:**
        * Gently introduce your product/service as a solution to the pain point. *Don’t* directly sell – focus on the benefits.
        * Include a short case study or testimonial (if available).
* **Day 7:  Limited-Time Offer (Subject:  Don't Miss Out: [Discount/Offer] on [Your Solution])**
    * **Content:**
        * Create a sense of urgency with a limited-time offer.
        * Clear call to action – link directly to your product/service page.
