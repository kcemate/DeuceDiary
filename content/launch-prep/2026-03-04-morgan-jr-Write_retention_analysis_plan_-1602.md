## Retention Analysis Plan – With D1, D7, and D30 Benchmarks

This plan outlines a strategy for analyzing user retention, focusing on key milestones: Day 1 (Initial Activation), Day 7 (First Experience), and Day 30 (30-Day Retention). This framework allows for early identification of issues and proactive interventions to improve long-term user engagement.

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Primary Goal:** Understand user churn rates and identify key drivers impacting retention at each milestone.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Define and track key metrics related to each retention stage.
    * Identify segments of users exhibiting high or low retention rates.
    * Discover pain points and friction points experienced by users at each stage.
    * Develop and implement targeted interventions to improve retention.


**2. Key Metrics:**

* **D1 (Day 1 Retention):**
    * **Activation Rate:** Percentage of users who complete the initial setup and core feature usage. (e.g., Profile creation, initial tutorial completion)
    * **Success Metric:**  % of Users who completed key onboarding tasks.
* **D7 (7-Day Retention):**
    * **7-Day Retention Rate:** Percentage of users who return and engage with the product/service after 7 days.
    * **Daily Active Users (DAU) / Weekly Active Users (WAU) at D7:**  Shows continued engagement.
    * **Success Metric:** % of Users who returned and used a key feature.
* **D30 (30-Day Retention):**
    * **30-Day Retention Rate:** Percentage of users who continue to use the product/service after 30 days.
    * **Monthly Active Users (MAU) at D30:**  A core measure of long-term engagement.
    * **Success Metric:** % of Users who consistently use core features over 30 days.

**3. Data Collection & Tracking:**

* **Analytics Platform:** Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or similar – Choose based on your needs and budget.
* **Event Tracking:** Implement detailed event tracking to capture user behavior:
    * **Onboarding Events:**  Button clicks, form submissions, tutorial steps, feature explorations.
    * **Feature Usage:** Tracking of how frequently and deeply users utilize key features.
    * **User Engagement:**  Time spent in app/platform, number of sessions, content consumption.
* **User Segmentation:**  Segment users based on:
    * **Acquisition Channel:** (e.g., Paid Ads, Organic Search, Social Media) - Helps understand channel-specific retention.
    * **Demographics:** (Age, Location, etc.) - Identifies demographic-related patterns.
    * **Behavioral Groups:** (New Users, Power Users, Inactive Users) - Allows for tailored interventions.
    * **Trial Status:** (Free Trial, Paid Subscription) - Critical for understanding conversion and retention.
