**I. Executive Summary:**

* **Goal:**  To systematically increase revenue over a defined period (e.g., 6 months, 1 year) by a specific percentage (e.g., 10%, 20%).
* **Approach:** A data-driven, iterative process combining strategies for attracting new customers, retaining existing ones, increasing average order value (AOV), and potentially exploring new revenue streams.
* **Key Metrics:** We’ll track metrics like Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV), Conversion Rates, AOV, Churn Rate, and Revenue Growth Rate.


**II. Phase 1: Assessment & Diagnostics (Weeks 1-4)**

* **1.1 Data Audit:**
    * **Sales Data:** Analyze past sales trends - seasonality, product performance, customer segments, geographic areas.
    * **Marketing Data:** Review marketing channel performance – which channels bring in the most leads/customers?  What’s the cost per lead/acquisition for each channel?
    * **Website Analytics:**  Understand user behavior - bounce rates, time on page, popular pages, checkout drop-off points. Tools like Google Analytics are crucial.
    * **Customer Data:** Segment your customer base – demographics, purchase history, engagement level.
* **1.2 Competitive Analysis:**
    * Identify your key competitors and analyze their pricing, promotions, and marketing strategies. What are they doing well? Where are they vulnerable?
* **1.3 Customer Feedback:**
    * **Surveys:** Send out surveys to understand customer satisfaction, pain points, and feature requests.
    * **Reviews:** Monitor online reviews (Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, etc.) - identify common themes and address negative feedback.
    * **Customer Interviews:** Conduct brief interviews with a small sample of customers to get in-depth insights.
* **1.4 SWOT Analysis:**  Conduct a thorough SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis of your business.
