Okay, here are 3 email drip campaign sequences, each designed for a different purpose, with a breakdown of the timing, content, and goals.  I've aimed for a realistic approach, assuming you have a basic level of segmentation (e.g., new subscriber vs. existing customer).

**Campaign 1: Welcome & Brand Introduction (New Subscribers - 5-7 Days)**

* **Goal:** Build initial engagement, introduce your brand, and set expectations.
* **Segmentation:** New email subscribers who signed up through your website, social media, or a lead magnet.
* **Timing:**
    * **Day 1: Welcome Email** - Immediately after signup.
    * **Day 3: Value Delivery Email**
    * **Day 5: Brand Story Email**
    * **Day 7: Gentle Reminder/Offer (Optional)**

* **Email Content:**
    * **Day 1: Welcome Email (Subject: Welcome to [Your Brand]!)**
        *  Personalized greeting ("Hi [Name]")
        *  Express gratitude for subscribing.
        *  Briefly reiterate what they signed up for (e.g., "You’re now part of the [Your Brand] community and will receive updates on [Key Product/Service Category]").
        *  Link to your website homepage.
        *  Simple call to action: “Explore our website!”
    * **Day 3: Value Delivery Email (Subject: [Helpful Tip Related to Your Niche] )**
        *  Provide a valuable tip, resource, or piece of content that aligns with your brand's expertise. (e.g., "3 Tips for Starting a Blog," "How to Choose the Right [Product]").
        *  Include an image or short video if possible.
        *  Link back to a relevant blog post or resource page.
    * **Day 5: Brand Story Email (Subject:  Our Story:  Why We Started [Your Brand])**
        *  Briefly tell your brand's story – the “why” behind your business. Focus on your mission, values, and what makes you unique. (Keep it concise – 1-2 paragraphs max.)
        *  Include a photo of the team or your product/service.
        *  Reinforce your brand values.
    * **Day 7: Gentle Reminder/Offer (Optional) (Subject: Still Exploring [Your Brand]? Here’s a Little Something...)**
        *  “We noticed you haven't explored our entire website.  Here's a [small discount code/free resource] to help you get started!” (Use a limited-time offer to create urgency.)
