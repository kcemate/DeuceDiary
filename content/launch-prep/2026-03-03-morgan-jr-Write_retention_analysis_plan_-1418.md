**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goal:**

* **Primary Goal:**  Understand the early-stage user behavior that dictates long-term retention, ultimately informing product strategy, onboarding, and engagement efforts.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify the critical drop-off points within the first 7 and 30 days.
    * Determine the key drivers of user retention (what’s working, what’s not?).
    * Establish baseline retention rates for comparison with future improvements.
    * Provide data-driven recommendations for optimizing the user journey.

**2. Data Collection & Tracking:**

* **Key Metrics:**
    * **Day 1 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to the product/service on the day after signing up.
    * **Day 7 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to the product/service 7 days after signing up.
    * **Day 30 Retention:** Percentage of users who return to the product/service 30 days after signing up.
    * **Churn Rate (Overall & Segmented):**  The percentage of users who stop using the product/service over a given period.
    * **Active Users (DAU/WAU/MAU):** Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Active Users - This will show the scale of your user base and the trends in engagement.
    * **Feature Usage:** Track which features are used most frequently by retained users vs. churned users.
    * **Session Length & Frequency:** How long are users spending in the product/service, and how often are they returning?
    * **User Segmentation:**  Categorize users based on key attributes (e.g., acquisition channel, demographic, initial behavior) to analyze retention rates across segments.
* **Tracking Methods:**
    * **Analytics Platform:** Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap, or similar.
    * **Product Database:** Record user activity events (e.g., login, feature usage, content consumption).
    * **CRM System:**  Connect user data with customer support interactions and feedback.
