Okay, let's craft a competitive metrics tracking plan. This plan will help you understand where you stand against your competitors, identify areas for improvement, and ultimately, drive better strategy and performance.

**1. Define Your Scope & Objectives**

* **What are you competing against?** (Be specific!)  Are you competing for market share, customer acquisition, brand awareness, price, innovation, etc.?  List your key competitors (3-5 is a good starting point).
* **What's your goal?** What are you trying to achieve by tracking this data? (e.g., Increase market share by X% in Y timeframe, improve customer satisfaction, gain a competitive advantage in a specific area).
* **Timeframe:**  How frequently will you track these metrics? (Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly? – depends on industry and data availability).
* **Team Responsibility:** Who is responsible for collecting, analyzing, and presenting the data? (Marketing, Sales, Product, Data Analytics?)

**2. Key Metric Categories & Specific Metrics**

Here’s a breakdown of metric categories and specific metrics you should consider.  Prioritize based on your scope and objectives.

**A. Market Share & Sales Performance:**

* **Market Share:** (Percentage of total market sales) - *Source: Industry Reports, Company Reports*
* **Sales Volume:** (Units sold) - *Source: CRM, POS Systems*
* **Revenue:** (Total Sales Revenue) - *Source: Accounting Systems*
* **Sales Growth Rate:** (Percentage change in sales over time) - *Calculated*
* **Win Rate:** (Percentage of leads that convert into sales) - *Source: CRM*
* **Average Deal Size:** (Average revenue per sale) - *Source: CRM*

**B. Customer Acquisition & Marketing Effectiveness:**

* **Website Traffic:** (Unique visitors, page views, bounce rate) - *Source: Google Analytics, Web Analytics Platforms*
* **Lead Generation:** (Number of leads, cost per lead) - *Source: CRM, Marketing Automation Platforms*
* **Conversion Rates:** (Website visitors to leads, leads to customers) - *Source: Analytics, CRM*
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** (Total marketing & sales cost / number of new customers) - *Calculated*
* **Social Media Engagement:** (Likes, shares, comments, followers) - *Source: Social Media Analytics*
* **Brand Mentions:** (Tracking mentions of your brand online – sentiment analysis) - *Source: Social Listening Tools (e.g., Mention, Brand24)*
* **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):** (Total marketing cost / number of new customers) - *Calculated*
* **Reach/Impressions:** (How many people saw your marketing campaigns) - *Source: Social Media, Advertising Platforms*

**C. Product & Innovation:**

* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** (Overall rating, sentiment analysis) - *Source: Amazon, Google Reviews, Trustpilot, Industry Review Sites*
* **New Product Launches:** (Number of new products, launch success metrics) – *Source: Internal Records*
* **Feature Adoption Rate:** (Percentage of users using new features) – *Source: Product Analytics*
* **Innovation Rate:** (Number of patents filed, new technologies developed) - *Source: Public Patent Databases, Industry Reports*
