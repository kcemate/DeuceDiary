## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

This playbook outlines our response strategy to various potential crises surrounding the launch of Deuce Diary. It’s designed to be reactive, adaptable, and focused on minimizing damage and maintaining a positive brand image.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Honesty and open communication are paramount. Don’t hide issues; address them head-on.
* **Speed:** Rapid response is crucial, especially in the digital age. Pre-defined escalation paths and communication templates will expedite action.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge user frustration and demonstrate genuine concern.
* **Data-Driven:** Base decisions on real-time data from monitoring tools, reviews, and social channels.
* **Team Coordination:** Clear roles and responsibilities within the core launch team (Marketing, Development, Customer Support, PR).


**II. Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Server Crash**

* **Severity:** High - Immediate impact on user experience.
* **Initial Response (0-30 mins):**
    * **Verification:** Development team confirms crash and investigates root cause.
    * **Internal Alert:**  Notify core team via designated Slack channel.
    * **Status Update:**  Post a brief, honest update on our social channels & website: "We’re aware of a technical issue impacting Deuce Diary. Our team is working hard to resolve it.  We'll provide updates shortly. #DeuceDiary #StatusUpdate"
* **Ongoing Response (30 mins - 4 hours):**
    * **Regular Updates:**  Provide 15-30 minute updates on progress (even if minimal) via social, website, and email subscribers.
    * **Technical Support Focus:** Customer support prioritizes affected users, offering troubleshooting tips & estimated resolution time.
    * **Root Cause Analysis:** Development team documents the crash to prevent recurrence.
* **Post-Resolution:**  Send a thank you message to users for their patience & offer a small token of apology (e.g., extended free trial).


**Scenario 2: 1-Star Reviews & Negative User Feedback**

* **Severity:** Medium - Signals potential user dissatisfaction, needs immediate attention.
* **Initial Response (0-60 mins):**
    * **Monitoring:**  Aggressively monitor app store reviews, social media sentiment, and support channels.
    * **Identify Patterns:**  Determine the common issues driving negative reviews (e.g., UI bugs, feature requests, performance).
* **Ongoing Response (60 mins - 24 hours):**
    * **Customer Support Engagement:**  Respond to *every* negative review with empathy & offer solutions (even if just acknowledging the issue).
    * **Feature Prioritization:**  Immediately flag the most common issues for development prioritization.
    * **Community Engagement:**  Create a dedicated channel (Reddit, Discord) for users to discuss the app & provide feedback.
* **Long-Term Response:**  Address identified issues in subsequent app updates, showcasing how user feedback is being incorporated.



**Scenario 3: Apple Rejection (App Store)**

* **Severity:** High -  Significant delay in launch, potential damage to credibility.
* **Initial Response (0-60 mins):**
    * **Root Cause Analysis:** Development team quickly investigates the reason for rejection (detailed documentation required?).
