**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store**

1. **Policy Violations (General):** This is the *biggest* reason. Apple’s App Store Review Guidelines are incredibly detailed and cover everything from content to functionality. Violations can be broadly categorized as:
   * **Misleading Content:** Inaccurate descriptions, deceptive screenshots, or presenting something as it's not.
   * **Security Issues:** Malware, vulnerabilities, data privacy problems, tracking without consent.
   * **Intellectual Property Infringement:**  Using copyrighted music, images, trademarks, or code without permission.
   * **Prohibited Content:**  Anything illegal, harmful, or offensive (hate speech, violence, drug references, etc.).

2. **App Store Guidelines Violations (Specific):**  This expands on the above – rules about specific categories (e.g., gaming, health & fitness), features, or user interactions.  For example, requiring users to enter email addresses to play a game is often flagged.

3. **Functionality Issues:**  The app simply doesn’t work as described. Bugs, crashes, inconsistent behavior, or features not functioning correctly.

4. **Performance Problems:** The app is slow, drains battery excessively, uses too much data, or has poor graphics.

5. **UI/UX Problems:**  A confusing, poorly designed, or frustrating user interface. This can lead to negative reviews and potential policy violations if it’s perceived as misleading.

6. **Privacy Concerns:**  Not disclosing data collection practices clearly, collecting excessive personal data, or failing to comply with privacy regulations (like GDPR or CCPA).

7. **Lack of Transparency:** Hiding information about how the app works, its data practices, or its developers.

8. **Misleading User Reviews:**  Inflated positive reviews obtained through fraudulent means (buying reviews, fake accounts).

9. **Unclear App Purpose:** The app’s function isn’t immediately obvious to the user, leading to confusion and potentially a violation of clarity guidelines.

10. **Metadata Issues:** Incorrect or missing metadata (e.g., app name, keywords, category) can hinder discoverability and raise red flags.
