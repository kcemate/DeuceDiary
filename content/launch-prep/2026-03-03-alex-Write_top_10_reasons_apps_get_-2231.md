**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

Apple’s App Review Guidelines are notoriously strict. Here’s a breakdown of the most common rejection reasons, grouped by category:

1. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted images, music, characters, or code without permission is a *massive* red flag.
2. **Misleading Representations:** Apps that falsely claim to do something they don't, exaggerate functionality, or use deceptive marketing are routinely rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  Not adequately disclosing data collection practices, tracking user activity without consent, or storing sensitive information insecurely are huge no-nos. (Especially following GDPR and CCPA scrutiny).
4. **Malware/Security Issues:**  Any code that could potentially harm a user’s device or steal data is immediately rejected. This includes vulnerabilities, backdoors, and malicious scripts.
5. **Functionality & Performance Issues:**  Apps that crash frequently, are unresponsive, have significant performance issues (e.g., excessive battery drain), or don't meet Apple's performance standards will be rejected.
6. **Violation of Apple’s Rules Regarding Third-Party Services:** Using external APIs without proper authorization, or circumventing Apple's payment systems, can trigger rejection.
7. **Content Guidelines Violations:** This covers a broad range of issues, including offensive content, hate speech, sexually explicit content (even if seemingly innocent), and content that promotes violence or illegal activities.
8. **Lack of Required Information:** Missing key information in the app’s metadata (app name, description, keywords, screenshots, support contact) is a common error.
9. **UI/UX Issues (Subjective):** While less common, Apple can reject apps if the user interface is confusing, poorly designed, or doesn’t adhere to their general design standards.
10. **Circumventing User Restrictions:** Apps that try to bypass parental controls, restrictions imposed by schools, or other security measures are rejected.
