Okay, let's craft a robust revenue optimization plan, integrating all the requested elements. This plan is designed to be iterative and data-driven, constantly refining our approach.

**I. Overall Philosophy:**

* **User-Centric:** We prioritize understanding user behavior and motivations. Don't just focus on conversion; focus on maximizing value for the user *while* increasing revenue.
* **Data-Driven Decisions:** Every decision will be backed by analytics, A/B testing, and feedback.
* **Phased Rollout:** Introduce changes incrementally, monitoring impact closely before scaling.
* **Transparency (Where Appropriate):** Be open with users about the value they’re receiving and the rationale behind pricing.

**II. Key Components of the Plan:**

**1. Paywall Strategy - When & How to Show It:**

* **Trigger Points:** Don't blanket-apply paywalls. Use a layered approach based on user behavior:
    * **Phase 1: Initial Exploration (Days 1-7):**  Minimal Paywall.  Focus on onboarding and letting users experience value.  Maybe a subtle ‘Premium Features’ callout.
    * **Phase 2: Engagement Building (Days 8-30):**  Introduce contextual paywall prompts *after* users demonstrate engagement:
        * **Content Consumption:**  If a user spends significant time (e.g., >30 minutes) on a premium piece, show a “Unlock Full Access” button.
        * **Feature Usage:** If a user repeatedly uses a key premium feature, prompt with "Upgrade for Unlimited Access"
        * **User Segment:**  Identify users who spend the most time browsing, or who seem to be seeking advanced functionality.  Target them with more prominent paywall prompts.
    * **Phase 3: Limited Access (Days 31-60):** Offer a limited free trial (see below) and continued paywall prompts based on engagement.
* **Paywall Styles:** Experiment with different visuals – pop-ups, embedded banners, dedicated landing pages.
* **Personalization:** Tailor the paywall messaging to the user’s past behavior and interests.


**2. Free Trial Length & Structure:**

* **Length:**  **7 Days** is a commonly effective length.  It's long enough for users to genuinely experience the value, but short enough to prevent churn.
* **Tiered Free Trial (Highly Recommended):**
    * **Basic:** Full access to core features. (The initial experience)
    * **Premium (Limited Time Only):**  Access to advanced features, content, or support – this creates a sense of urgency.
* **Trial Reset:**  On trial end, offer a compelling reminder of the value lost and a clear call to action to subscribe.

**3. Pricing Experiments:**

* **A/B Testing:** Crucial! Test multiple pricing tiers and features included in each tier.
* **Tier Options (Example):**
    * **Basic:**  $9.99/month – Core features, limited content.
    * **Standard:** $19.99/month – All features, expanded content library, priority support.
    * **Pro:** $49.99/month – All features, unlimited access, dedicated account manager (for high-value users).
* **Psychological Pricing:**  Consider using prices like $9.99 instead of $10.
* **Value-Based Pricing:**  Instead of just competition, price based on the *value* users perceive. What are they *really* getting?

**4. Feature Gating Strategy:**

* **Gradual Rollout of Premium Features:**  Don’t launch *everything* at once.  This helps manage user expectations and allows you to gather feedback
