Okay, let's design an analytics dashboard for the Deuce Diary launch metrics. This dashboard will focus on tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) to understand user engagement, growth, and overall success.

**I. Overall Goals & Target Audience:**

* **Goal:** To provide a centralized view of Deuce Diary’s launch performance, enabling quick identification of successes, areas for improvement, and actionable insights for the team.
* **Target Audience:** Product Managers, Marketing Team, Development Team, and potentially early investors.

**II. Dashboard Structure & Key Sections:**

We'll organize the dashboard into sections, each focusing on a specific aspect of the launch:

**1. High-Level Overview (Top Section - KPIs at a Glance):**

* **Total Downloads/Installs:**  (Number) - A fundamental measure of adoption.
* **Daily Active Users (DAU):** (Number) -  Shows how many users are actively engaging with the app each day.
* **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** (Number) - Provides a longer-term trend of user engagement.
* **Monthly Active Users (MAU):** (Number) - Gives a total view of the user base.
* **Retention Rate (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30):** (Percentage) -  Crucial for understanding user stickiness.  This is broken down into cohorts based on signup date.
* **Average Session Duration:** (Minutes:Seconds) -  How long users are spending in the app.
* **Overall App Rating:** (Score/Stars - from App Store/Google Play) - A reflection of user sentiment.



**2. Acquisition & Marketing Performance:**

* **Source Breakdown:** (Pie Chart/Bar Chart) -  Percentage of downloads/installs attributed to each marketing channel (e.g., Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Influencer Marketing, App Store Search, Organic, Referral).
* **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):** (Currency) - The cost to acquire a new user through each channel. (Important for ROI analysis).
* **Impressions & Click-Through Rates (CTR):** (Numbers) - Metrics from paid advertising campaigns to assess campaign effectiveness.
* **Website Traffic:** (Line Chart) -  Monitor website visits and their source (organic, referral, paid).
* **Social Media Engagement:** (Summary - Likes, Shares, Comments) -  Track the reach and interaction of marketing efforts on social media.



**3. User Engagement & Behavior:**

* **Feature Usage:** (Stacked Bar Chart or Heatmap) - Visualizes how frequently users are interacting with key features within the app. (e.g., Daily Log Entry, Mood Tracking, Journal Prompts, Photo Uploads).
* **Entry Frequency:** (Average Entries per User) -  How many times each user is logging a diary entry.
* **Time of Day Usage:** (Bar Chart) -  Identifies when users are most active in the app. This allows for targeted promotions or reminders.
* **Completion Rates (for Key Activities):** (Percentage) - For example: "Percentage of users completing a full mood log entry".
* **User Flows:** (Funnel Chart) - Visualize the user journey from app download to key actions (e.g., creating first entry, adding a photo).  This helps identify drop-off points.



**4.  Retention & Churn (Important for long-term success)**

* **Retention Cohort Analysis:** (Cohort Chart) - This is *critical*.  Track the retention of users acquired in different periods (e.g., January cohort, February cohort) over time.  This shows the long-term impact of your marketing and product.
* **Churn Rate:** (Percentage) -  The percentage of users who stop using
