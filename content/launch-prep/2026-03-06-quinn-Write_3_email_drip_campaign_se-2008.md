Okay, here are 3 email drip campaign sequences, each with a different goal and designed for a business selling online courses. I’ve included suggested timing, content, and calls-to-action (CTAs).  Remember to tailor these to your specific course and audience!

**Campaign 1: Awareness & Value (7 Days - Focus: Building Interest)**

* **Goal:** Introduce your brand, highlight the problem your course solves, and establish yourself as a knowledgeable resource.
* **Audience:** New subscribers who signed up through a lead magnet (e.g., free guide, checklist, webinar).
* **Timing:**  Every 24-48 hours
* **Email Sequence:**
    * **Email 1 (Day 1): Welcome & Problem Introduction:**
        * **Subject:** Welcome to [Your Brand Name] – Are You Struggling With…?
        * **Content:**  Warm welcome, thank them for subscribing, and directly address the pain points your course solves.  "We know many people struggle with [specific challenge]. That's why we created [Your Brand Name] to help you..." Include a short, relatable anecdote.
        * **CTA:** "Learn More About [Problem]" (Links to a blog post or page that expands on the problem)
    * **Email 2 (Day 3):  Highlighting Benefits (Without Selling):**
        * **Subject:** 3 Ways [Problem] is Holding You Back
        * **Content:**  Focus on the *consequences* of the problem.  List 3-4 key drawbacks.  Don’t mention the course directly yet.
        * **CTA:**  "Take Our Quick Assessment" (Links to a short quiz related to the problem - great for lead generation)
    * **Email 3 (Day 5):  Testimonial/Social Proof:**
        * **Subject:**  See How [Similar Person] Transformed Their [Area]
        * **Content:**  Share a short, compelling testimonial from a satisfied customer or a success story related to the problem.  Visuals (image or short video) are ideal.
        * **CTA:** “Read More Success Stories” (Links to a case study or testimonials page)
    * **Email 4 (Day 7): Final Reminder & Introduction to Solution (Soft Sell):**
        * **Subject:** Ready to Change Things?
        * **Content:** Recap the problem and offer a gentle introduction to your brand's solution. "We've shared some of the challenges you might be facing... We believe there's a better way."
        * **CTA:** "Explore Our Programs" (Links to your course catalog – don't focus on one specific course yet)



**Campaign 2:  Course Specific Promotion (5-7 Days - Focus: Driving Sales)**

* **Goal:**  Get subscribers to seriously consider and ultimately purchase a specific course.
* **Audience:**  People who have engaged with your previous emails (e.g., downloaded the lead magnet, completed the quiz).
* **Timing:** Every 24-48 hours
* **Email Sequence:**
    * **Email 1 (Day 1):  Introducing the Course – Problem/Solution Alignment:**
        * **Subject:**  Finally! A Solution for [Specific Problem]
        * **Content:** Introduce the *specific* course you're promoting. Clearly connect the course content directly to the problems they’ve identified and the benefits they’re seeking.  Focus on transformation.
        * **CTA:** "Learn More About [Course Name]" (Links to the course landing page)
    * **Email 2 (Day 3):  Course Curriculum Overview:**
        * **Subject:**  What You’ll Learn in [Course Name]
