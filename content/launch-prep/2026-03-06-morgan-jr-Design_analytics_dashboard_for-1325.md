Okay, let's design an analytics dashboard for the launch of "Deuce Diary." This dashboard will focus on key metrics to gauge the success of the launch, identify areas for improvement, and guide future marketing efforts.

**I. Overall Dashboard Goal:**

To provide a single, clear view of Deuce Diary’s launch performance, tracking critical metrics and highlighting trends to inform strategic decisions.

**II. Dashboard Sections & Metrics:**

We'll break the dashboard into sections for clarity. Each section will focus on a specific area of the launch.

**1. Acquisition (Top of Funnel):** Where are people coming from?

* **Metric:**
    * **Total Downloads/Installs:** (Big Number – Primary KPI) - Shows overall interest.
    * **Source Breakdown (%):**
        * **App Store Search:** (Percentage of installs from searching "Deuce Diary" directly) - Important for organic discovery.
        * **Paid Advertising (Google Ads, Facebook Ads, etc.):** (Split by Campaign & Ad Group) - Key for paid marketing ROI. Track Cost Per Install (CPI) and Return on Ad Spend (ROAS).
        * **Social Media (Organic):** (Impressions, Engagement – Likes, Shares, Comments) – Shows brand awareness and audience interest.
        * **Influencer Marketing:** (Downloads/Installs attributed to specific influencers) - Measures influencer campaign effectiveness.
        * **Referral Program:** (Downloads/Installs from Referral Links) - Assess the effectiveness of the referral program.
        * **Direct Downloads:** (Users typing in the URL) -  Indicates brand recognition or word-of-mouth.
* **Visualization:** Pie chart for source breakdown, Line chart showing downloads over time by source.
* **Time Range:** Last 30 days, with selectable ranges (7 days, 30 days, 90 days)

**2. Engagement (Mid-Funnel):** Are people using the app?

* **Metric:**
    * **Daily Active Users (DAU):** (Number of unique users who opened the app in a day) – Core engagement indicator.
    * **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** (Number of unique users who opened the app in a week) – Provides a broader view of engagement.
    * **Monthly Active Users (MAU):** (Number of unique users who opened the app in a month) –  Long-term user base size.
    * **Session Length:** (Average time users spend in the app per session) –  Indicates the value users are finding.
    * **Screen Views Per Session:** (Average number of screens viewed during a session) - Shows how deeply users are exploring the app’s features.
    * **Feature Usage:** (Percentage of users engaging with key features - e.g., journaling prompts, mood tracking, goal setting) -  Helps identify popular features and potential areas to promote.
* **Visualization:** Line charts for DAU, WAU, MAU over time.  Stacked bar chart showing feature usage breakdown.
* **Time Range:** Last 30 days, with selectable ranges (7 days, 30 days, 90 days).

**3. Retention (Bottom of Funnel):** Are users sticking around?

* **Metric:**
    * **Retention Rate (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30):** (Percentage of users who return to the app after 1 day, 7 days, and 30 days respectively) -  Critical for understanding long-term growth.
    * **Churn Rate:** (Percentage of users who stop using the app) –  The inverse of retention.
    * **Uninstall Rate:** (Percentage of users who uninstall the app) -  Indicates dissatisfaction.
