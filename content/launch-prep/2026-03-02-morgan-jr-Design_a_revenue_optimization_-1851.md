Okay, let’s build a comprehensive revenue optimization plan. This will be a layered approach, constantly testing and adjusting based on data.

**I. Core Principles & Goals**

* **Customer-Centricity:** Every decision should be made with the user experience in mind. Don't be overly aggressive; focus on value delivery first.
* **Data-Driven:**  We'll rely heavily on analytics to understand user behavior, conversion rates, and overall performance.
* **A/B Testing:**  The engine of this plan. We’ll constantly run experiments to identify what works best.
* **Iterative Improvement:**  Don’t aim for perfection immediately. Start with hypotheses, test, learn, and adapt.

**II. Paywall Strategy – When & How**

* **Trigger Points:**
    * **First-Time User:** Immediately after a user has spent 5-10 minutes engaging with the core value of the product. This triggers a subtle prompt: "Unlock Unlimited Access for Just $X/Month" with a clear benefit statement.
    * **Content Deep Dive:** When a user spends a significant amount of time (15-30 minutes) on a specific piece of content (especially premium content).
    * **Behavioral Triggers:**
        * **Repeated Visits:**  If a user visits the site/app multiple times in a short period, suggesting a subscription becomes more appropriate.
        * **Feature Usage:**  If a user repeatedly uses a feature that's only available with a paid plan.
        * **Cart Abandonment:**  If a user adds something to their cart but doesn’t complete the purchase.
* **Paywall Types (Initially):**
    * **Pop-Up Paywall:**  A gentle, non-intrusive pop-up offering a trial or discounted price.  (Use sparingly - too many pop-ups are annoying).
    * **Progressive Disclosure:**  Show limited content initially, with a clear path to unlock full access.
    * **Contextual Paywall:** Present the paywall directly within the content itself. (e.g., “Read the Full Article”)
* **Messaging:**  Focus on the *value* they're missing.  “Unlock exclusive insights,” “Go beyond the basics,” “Don’t miss out on…”

**III. Free Trial Strategy**

* **Length:**  This *heavily* depends on the product/industry.
    * **Short Trial (7-14 Days):** Best for products with a clear, immediate value proposition (e.g., SaaS tools).  Forces quicker decisions.
    * **Medium Trial (30 Days):** Good for products with a more gradual learning curve or deeper value (e.g., online courses, premium software).
    * **Long Trial (90 Days):**  Suitable for very complex or expensive products – allows users time to fully integrate and experience the value.
* **Trial Tier:** Offer a limited version of the paid plan during the trial, allowing users to experience the core benefits.
* **Trial Onboarding:**  Excellent onboarding is *crucial* for trial conversions. Walk users through key features and show them how to get value.
* **Automated Reminders:**  Gentle, personalized emails reminding users of the trial ending, highlighting the benefits they're missing.
