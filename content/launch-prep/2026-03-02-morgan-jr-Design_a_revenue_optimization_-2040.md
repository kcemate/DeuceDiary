**I. Overall Philosophy:**

* **Focus on Value:**  The entire strategy revolves around demonstrating the *value* your product/service provides to the user. This dictates when and how you introduce paywalls and other monetization tactics.
* **User Segmentation:** Recognize that not all users are created equal.  Segmentation allows you to tailor your approach for maximum impact.
* **Data-Driven Iteration:**  Constant monitoring, analysis, and A/B testing are *essential*.  Don’t rely on gut feeling; let the data guide your decisions.

**II. When to Show Paywall (Triggering & Sequencing)**

This needs a phased approach:

* **Phase 1: Awareness & Engagement (Free Content - First 7-14 Days)**
    * **Trigger:**  User signs up, completes onboarding, explores basic features.
    * **Content:**  Offer core free content.  This is about letting them experience the value.
    * **Goal:** Get users to spend time in the product and experience the 'aha' moment.
* **Phase 2: Value Demonstration (14-30 Days - Limited Free Trial)**
    * **Trigger:** User interacts with core features for a set period (e.g., 14 days).
    * **Content:** Limited access to a subset of premium features. Offer a strong ‘sneak peek’ into what they’re missing.
    * **Goal:** Increase perceived value of the paid offering.  Highlight features they *can’t* do in the free version.
* **Phase 3: Soft Conversion (30-60 Days - Reduced Paywall Access)**
    * **Trigger:**  User continues to engage, perhaps showing a slight increase in feature usage or time spent.
    * **Content:** Offer a low-cost, limited-time subscription (e.g., $9.99/month) unlocking *all* features for a week or two.
    * **Goal:**  Capture early adopters who are committed to using the product.
* **Phase 4: Hard Conversion (60+ Days - Full Paywall)**
    * **Trigger:** User consistently uses the product and demonstrates a high potential for lifetime value (see below).  Also, after a longer free trial, if engagement hasn't dropped significantly.
    * **Content:** Full paywall access.



**III. Free Trial Length:**

* **Segmented Approach:**
    * **High-Value Users:** 14-30 days (as described above) – they need time to truly embed the product.
    * **Lower-Value Users:** 7-14 days – Shorter trial to avoid churn if they don’t immediately see value.
    * **Specific Product Types:** (e.g., SaaS) – Longer trials (30-60 days) if the product requires a significant onboarding process or integration.
