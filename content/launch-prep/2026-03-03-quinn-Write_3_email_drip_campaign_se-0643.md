Okay, here are 3 email drip campaign sequences, each tailored to a different goal and length. I’ve included estimated timing, content ideas, and key metrics to track.

**Campaign 1: Welcome & Brand Introduction (Short & Sweet - 3-5 Days)**

* **Goal:** Immediately welcome new subscribers, introduce your brand, and set expectations.
* **Target Audience:** New email subscribers
* **Timing:** Days 1, 3, 5
* **Subject Lines:**
    * Day 1: “Welcome to [Your Brand]! 🎉”
    * Day 3: “Discover What Makes [Your Brand] Special”
    * Day 5: “A Little Something Just For You…”
* **Content:**
    * **Day 1 (Immediately after signup):**
        * **Email 1:**  Warm welcome, thank you for subscribing, brief brand story (1-2 sentences), what they can expect from your emails (e.g., exclusive discounts, tips, product updates).  Include a friendly image.  Button: “Explore Our Website”
    * **Day 3:**
        * **Email 2:**  Highlight 1-2 key benefits of your product/service. Use a testimonial quote if possible.  Showcase a key feature with a visual.  Button: “Learn More”
    * **Day 5:**
        * **Email 3:** Small incentive (10% off coupon code) to encourage their first purchase.  Include a link directly to a popular product or category. Button: "Shop Now"
* **Key Metrics:** Open Rate, Click-Through Rate (CTR), Conversion Rate (to website visit or first purchase)
* **Tone:** Friendly, enthusiastic, informative

**Campaign 2: Problem/Solution – Nurturing (Medium Length – 7-10 Days)**

* **Goal:** Identify customer needs/pain points, establish your brand as a solution, and build trust.
* **Target Audience:**  Subscribers who have shown some interest (e.g., visited certain product pages, downloaded a lead magnet)
* **Timing:** Days 7, 9, 11
* **Subject Lines:**
    * Day 7: "Struggling With [Problem Area]? We Can Help"
    * Day 9: "3 Ways [Your Product/Service] Solves [Pain Point]"
    * Day 11: "Real Customers, Real Results"
* **Content:**
    * **Day 7:**
        * **Email 1:** Address a common pain point your audience experiences.  Frame the problem in a way that resonates.  Don’t immediately sell – focus on empathy and understanding.  Link to a blog post or resource that expands on the problem.
    * **Day 9:**
        * **Email 2:** Present 3-4 specific ways your product/service directly addresses the pain point. Use bullet points and clear language.  Include screenshots or short video clips demonstrating the solution.
    * **Day 11:**
        * **Email 3:**  Social proof – showcase a customer testimonial or case study where someone successfully used your product/service to overcome the problem.  Button: “Read the Case Study”
* **Key Metrics:** Open Rate, CTR, Link Clicks (to blog posts, case studies), Lead Magnet Downloads
* **Tone:** Helpful, authoritative, trustworthy
