## Retention Analysis Plan - D1/D7/D30 Benchmarks

**Goal:** To understand user behavior after onboarding and identify key areas for improvement to maximize user retention. This plan outlines a phased approach focusing on critical benchmarks: Day 1, Day 7, and Day 30.

**1. Data & Metrics:**

* **Definition:** Retention is defined as users actively engaging with the product/service. We'll track the following metrics:
    * **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Users who used the product/service on a given day.
    * **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** Users who used the product/service during a given week.
    * **Monthly Active Users (MAU):** Users who used the product/service during a given month.
    * **Session Duration:** Average time users spend within the product/service per session.
    * **Feature Usage:** Tracking adoption of key features -  this will inform targeted interventions.
    * **Churn Rate:** Percentage of users who stopped using the product/service within a defined period (crucial for overall assessment).
* **Data Sources:**
    * **Analytics Platform:** (e.g., Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude) - Primary source for tracking user activity.
    * **CRM/User Database:**  Used for cohort analysis and segmenting users.
    * **User Feedback Channels:** (e.g., Surveys, Support Tickets) - Provides qualitative insights.

**2. Cohort Analysis & Segmentation:**

* **Cohort Definition:** We will group users based on their acquisition date (e.g., users signed up in June 2024). This will allow us to track retention trends over time for each cohort.
* **Segmentation:**  We’ll segment cohorts based on key characteristics to identify specific drop-off points:
    * **Acquisition Channel:** (e.g., Organic Search, Paid Advertising, Referral) – Understand which channels are driving the highest retention.
    * **User Segment:** (e.g., New Users, Power Users, Trial Users) – Different segments require tailored strategies.
    * **Demographics:** (e.g., Age, Location) – Explore potential demographic influences on retention.
    * **Behavioral Data:** (e.g., Feature usage, Session frequency) –  Pinpoint specific actions leading to churn.
