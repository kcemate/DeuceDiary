Okay, let's build a competitive metrics tracking plan. This plan is designed to be adaptable and scalable, so you can tailor it to your specific business and goals.

**I. Executive Summary**

* **Goal:**  To consistently monitor and understand our competitive landscape, identify opportunities for improvement, and ultimately inform strategic decisions to drive market share and revenue growth.
* **Frequency:** Weekly (Detailed Monitoring), Monthly (Reporting & Analysis), Quarterly (Strategic Review)
* **Key Focus:** Identifying key competitive differentiators, understanding market trends, and tracking the effectiveness of our own strategies against the competition.

**II. Scope & Categories of Competition**

* **Define "Competition":** Be specific. Are you competing with:
    * **Direct Competitors:** Companies offering similar products/services to the same target market. (e.g., Netflix vs. Disney+)
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Companies offering alternative solutions that address the same customer need. (e.g., Spotify vs. Traditional Radio)
    * **Potential Competitors:**  New entrants or companies expanding into your market.
* **Categorize Competitors:** Create a matrix with key competitors, broken down by segments based on:
    * **Product/Service Offering:** (e.g., Premium, Basic, Value)
    * **Target Market:** (e.g., Enterprise, SMB, Consumer)
    * **Pricing Strategy:** (e.g., Low-Cost, Premium, Value-Based)
    * **Distribution Channels:** (e.g., Online, Retail, Wholesale)

**III. Key Metrics to Track (Broken Down by Categories)**

Here's a breakdown of metrics categorized for easier tracking.  Use a spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Excel) to manage this data.

**A. Marketing & Sales Performance:**

* **Website Traffic:**
    * **Source:**  Google Analytics (Track traffic by channel - organic, paid, social, referral)
    * **Metrics:** Sessions, Users, Bounce Rate, Pages per Session, Average Session Duration
* **Lead Generation:**
    * **Metrics:** Number of Leads, Lead Conversion Rate (Lead to MQL, MQL to SQL, SQL to Customer), Cost Per Lead (CPL)
* **Social Media Engagement:**
    * **Metrics:** Followers, Likes, Shares, Comments, Reach, Engagement Rate (Engagement/Reach) – Track across all relevant platforms.
* **Paid Advertising Performance:**
    * **Metrics:** Impressions, Clicks, Click-Through Rate (CTR), Cost Per Click (CPC), Conversion Rate, Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) –  For Google Ads, Social Media Ads, etc.
* **Brand Mentions:**
    * **Tool:** Social Listening Tools (Mention, Brand24, Hootsuite Insights)
    * **Metrics:** Number of Mentions, Sentiment (Positive, Negative, Neutral), Reach of Mentions

**B. Product & Service Performance:**

* **Feature Adoption Rate:** (If applicable - for SaaS or software) Track which features users are utilizing.
* **Net Promoter Score (NPS):**  Measure customer loyalty and willingness to recommend.
* **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT):**  Directly ask customers about their satisfaction levels.
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:**  Monitor reviews on platforms like G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon, etc.
* **New Product/Feature Release Tracking:** Measure adoption, user feedback, and impact on key metrics.

**C. Competitive Analysis (Gathered from various sources):**

* **New Product Launches:**  Monitor competitor announcements and release dates.
