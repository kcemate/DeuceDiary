**I. Overall Philosophy: Data-Driven & Iterative**

* **Focus:** Not just "make money," but maximize *sustainable* revenue by attracting and retaining high-value users.
* **Measurement is Key:**  Establish clear Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) across all stages (see section IV).
* **A/B Testing:**  Fundamental to every decision. Don't rely on gut feelings.
* **Segmentation:** Understand user behavior based on demographic, usage patterns, and value.

**II. Paywall Strategy – When & How**

* **Phase 1: Awareness & Exploration (0-7 Days)**
    * **Free Content:**  Offer a substantial amount of high-quality free content – articles, videos, tutorials, templates – to build a library.
    * **No Immediate Paywall:** Don’t trigger the paywall during this phase.
* **Phase 2: Engagement & Value Demonstration (8-30 Days)**
    * **Gentle Nudges:** Introduce contextual reminders about premium features and benefits.  "Unlock unlimited access," "Get the full experience," etc.  These should be subtle and non-intrusive.
    * **Limited Paywall Activation (Segmented):** This is where the experimentation begins.
        * **Behavior-Based:**  Users who spend > X hours a week, read Y number of articles, or engage with specific premium features are shown the paywall. (X & Y are data-driven thresholds).
        * **Time-Based:** Gradually increase the frequency of paywall prompts over time.
* **Phase 3: Conversion Push (31+ Days)**
    * **Limited Time Offers:**  Promote discounted subscriptions during specific periods.
    * **Feature Highlights:** Emphasize the benefits of the paid tier that aren't available in the free version.


**III. Free Trial Length & Pricing Experiments**

* **Free Trial Length:**  This is highly product-dependent, but we can start with these options and test:
    * **7-Day Trial:** Standard, conservative.
    * **14-Day Trial:**  More generous, allows users to experience a broader range of features.
    * **30-Day Trial:**  Ideal for products with significant value and a longer ramp-up period. (Higher risk, higher potential reward).
* **Pricing Experiments:**
    * **Tiered Pricing:**  Essential. Examples:
        * **Basic:**  Limited features, lower price.
        * **Pro:**  Full features, mid-range price.
        * **Premium:**  Advanced features, highest price (often includes support, training, etc.).
    * **A/B Test Different Price Points:**  Don't just pick a price – test different levels within each tier.  Start with $9.99, $19.99, $49.99, etc.
    * **Bundle Pricing:** Offer discounts for packages combining different products or services.
    * **Usage-Based Pricing:** (If applicable) Charge based on consumption of resources – e.g., API calls, storage.
