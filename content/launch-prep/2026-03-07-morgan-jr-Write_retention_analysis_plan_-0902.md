**Retention Analysis Plan**

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

*   **Overall Goal:** Understand and optimize user retention to maximize long-term value.
*   **Specific Objectives:**
    *   Identify key drivers of initial and sustained engagement.
    *   Pinpoint areas of user churn (where users are dropping off).
    *   Establish baseline retention rates to track improvements.
    *   Inform product decisions, onboarding improvements, and communication strategies.

**II. Data Collection & Metrics:**

*   **User Cohorts:** Define cohorts based on acquisition date (e.g., users who signed up in January, February, etc.). This is CRITICAL for accurate analysis.
*   **Key Retention Metrics:**
    *   **D1 Retention:** Percentage of users who returned to your product/service the day after signup.
    *   **D7 Retention:** Percentage of users who returned to your product/service 7 days after signup.
    *   **D30 Retention:** Percentage of users who are still actively using your product/service 30 days after signup.
    *   **DAU/MAU (Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users):**  A key indicator of overall engagement. Monitor changes in this ratio over time.
    *   **Feature Usage:** Track which features users are engaging with, especially in the early days.
    *   **Session Duration:** How long users are spending in your product/service per session.
    *   **User Actions (Events):** Define key actions within your product that signify engagement (e.g., creating a project, sending a message, completing a task, uploading content).  This provides granular insights.
    *   **Churn Reason (if available):**  If you can gather feedback (e.g., exit surveys, customer support interactions), understand *why* users are leaving.
    *   **Acquisition Channel:**  Track the source of each user (e.g., organic search, paid advertising, referral).

**III. Benchmarking & Targets (Example - Adjust Based on Your Industry & Product):**

| Benchmark  | Target Retention (%) | Notes                                                                                                                            |
| :--------- | :------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **D1**     | 30-40%               | A strong D1 retention rate indicates a good initial onboarding experience and immediate value proposition.  Aim for at least 30%. |
| **D7**     | 15-25%               | A drop-off at D7 suggests issues with continued engagement, perhaps users haven't discovered the full value or they've lost interest.  |
| **D30**    | 5-10%                | D30 is your long-term retention goal.  This reflects the user's sustained commitment. A 5-10% is often a good starting point.        |
