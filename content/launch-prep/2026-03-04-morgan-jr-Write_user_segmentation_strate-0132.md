**I. Understanding the Goal & Context**

Before diving into the segments themselves, we need to define *why* we're segmenting. This will drive all subsequent decisions.

* **What are we trying to achieve?** (e.g., improve marketing ROI, develop better product features, increase customer retention, launch a new product)
* **What data do we have access to?** (e.g., CRM data, website analytics, social media data, purchase history, surveys, demographic data)
* **What's our business?** (Industry, product/service, target market - this heavily influences relevant segments)

**II. Segmentation Methods – Choosing the Right Approach**

There are several ways to segment users. We'll likely use a combination for the richest insights.

* **Demographic Segmentation:** This is the most common starting point.
    * **Age:** (e.g., Millennials, Gen Z, Baby Boomers)
    * **Gender:** (Male, Female, Non-Binary)
    * **Income:** (Ranges – Low, Middle, High)
    * **Education:** (Level of Education)
    * **Location:** (Country, Region, City Size, Urban vs. Rural)
    * **Family Status:** (Single, Married, With Children, Empty Nesters)
* **Psychographic Segmentation:** Delves into *why* people make decisions. This is often more valuable than demographics.
    * **Lifestyle:** (Active, Homebody, Adventurous, Luxury)
    * **Values:** (Environmentalism, Family Values, Social Responsibility)
    * **Interests:** (Hobbies, Activities, Entertainment Preferences)
    * **Attitudes:** (Brand Loyalty, Risk Tolerance, Innovation Adoption)
* **Behavioral Segmentation:** Based on how users interact with your product/service.
    * **Purchase Frequency:** (New, Frequent, Loyal, Churn Risk)
    * **Purchase Value:** (High-Value Customers, Low-Value Customers)
    * **Product Usage:** (How often they use features, which features they use most)
    * **Website Activity:** (Pages visited, time spent on site, content downloaded)
    * **Engagement:** (Social Media Interactions, Email Opens/Clicks)
* **Needs-Based Segmentation:** Focuses on what users *want* or *need*.
    * **Pain Points:** (What problems are they trying to solve?)
    * **Goals:** (What are they trying to achieve?)
    * **Benefits Sought:** (What specific advantages are they looking for?)
* **Technographic Segmentation:**  (Becoming increasingly important)
    * **Device Type:** (Mobile, Desktop, Tablet)
    * **Operating System:** (iOS, Android, Windows)
    * **Internet Speed:** (Slow, Moderate, High)
