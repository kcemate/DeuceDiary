## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines a framework for tracking competitive performance, identifying key trends, and informing strategic decisions. It’s designed to be adaptable and should be reviewed and updated regularly (at least quarterly) based on evolving market dynamics.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:**  To gain a deeper understanding of the competitive landscape, identify opportunities and threats, and ultimately improve our own performance by benchmarking against the best.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key competitors and their strengths & weaknesses.
    * Track competitor pricing, promotions, and product offerings.
    * Monitor competitor market share and customer acquisition strategies.
    *  Identify emerging trends and disruptive technologies.
    *  Provide data-driven insights to inform strategic decisions (product development, marketing, sales, pricing).


**II. Key Competitors Identification & Prioritization:**

* **Tier 1 Competitors (High Priority):** These are the companies directly competing with us for our core target market and offering similar products/services.  Focus detailed monitoring on these.  (Example: Google, Apple, Amazon)
* **Tier 2 Competitors (Medium Priority):**  Companies that operate in adjacent markets or target a slightly different segment of our target market. Monitor regularly for changes. (Example: Spotify, Netflix)
* **Tier 3 Competitors (Low Priority):** Smaller companies or startups that may represent potential threats or offer innovative solutions.  Monitor sporadically for significant shifts.

**III. Metrics Tracking Categories & Specific Metrics:**

This is broken down into categories to ensure a comprehensive view:

**A. Product & Feature Performance:**

* **New Product Launches:** Track launch dates, features, pricing, and initial reviews.
* **Feature Updates:** Monitor release frequency, new feature additions, and impact on user feedback.
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** Track reviews on platforms like G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon, etc.  Analyze sentiment and key themes. (Quantify: Average rating, number of reviews, specific feature ratings)
* **Net Promoter Score (NPS):**  Regularly measure customer loyalty and willingness to recommend.


**B. Pricing & Promotions:**

* **Pricing Changes:**  Monitor competitor price adjustments – both standard and promotional. (Track frequency and magnitude)
* **Promotional Offers:** Track discounts, bundles, free trials, and other promotional activities. (Track type of offer, duration, and reach)
* **Value Propositions:** Analyze how competitors are positioning themselves in terms of value (e.g., “best value,” “premium features”).

**C. Marketing & Sales Activities:**

* **Website Traffic & Engagement:**  Analyze competitor website traffic, bounce rates, time on page, and top landing pages. (Use tools like SimilarWeb, SEMrush)
* **Social Media Activity:** Monitor follower counts, engagement rates, content types, and brand sentiment across relevant platforms.
* **Advertising Spend & Channels:**  Track competitor advertising spend across platforms (Google Ads, Social Media Ads, Display Ads).
* **SEO Performance:**  Analyze competitor keywords, rankings, and backlinks. (Use tools like Ahrefs, Moz)
* **Content Marketing:** Track the quantity and quality of competitor content (blogs, videos, case studies).
* **Lead Generation Tactics:** Observe competitor lead generation methods (e.g., webinars, free trials, gated content).


**D. Market Share & Customer Acquisition:**

* **Market Share Data:** Obtain estimates of market share from industry reports, research firms, or internal sales data. (Difficult to obtain exact figures, focus on trends)
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**  Estimate competitor CAC based on available marketing data.
* **New Customer Growth:** Track competitor new customer acquisition numbers (if publicly available).
