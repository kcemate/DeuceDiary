## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook - Version 1.0

**Executive Summary:** This playbook outlines our response strategy to potential crises surrounding the launch of Deuce Diary, focusing on rapid communication, transparent action, and proactive mitigation. We’ll prioritize maintaining user trust and managing brand reputation.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be open and honest with our users, media, and stakeholders about issues and our response.
* **Speed:** React quickly – delay equals damage.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge user frustration and demonstrate we understand their concerns.
* **Data-Driven Decisions:**  Base responses on real-time data – app analytics, social listening, review trends.
* **Team Coordination:** Establish clear roles & responsibilities within the core launch team (Marketing, Engineering, PR, Customer Support).

**II. Crisis Scenarios & Response Plans:**

**Scenario 1: Server Crash**

* **Likelihood:** High - Especially during initial launch.
* **Impact:** User frustration, app un-usable, immediate negative PR.
* **Immediate Response (0-30 mins):**
    * **Engineering Lead:** Confirm the scope of the crash, identify root cause, and begin immediate stabilization efforts.  Provide regular, brief updates to the core team.
    * **Communicate:** Release a short, genuine statement on all channels (Twitter, Facebook, App Store, website) acknowledging the issue: “We’re experiencing technical difficulties. Our team is working urgently to resolve them. We appreciate your patience.”
    * **Status Page:**  Set up a simple status page (e.g., Statuspage.io) for real-time updates.
* **Ongoing Response (30 mins - 4 hours):**
    * **Frequent Updates:** Continue updating users on progress, estimated resolution time.
    * **Dedicated Support:**  Customer Support ramps up to handle increased inquiries.
    * **Post-Mortem:**  Begin investigating the cause and identify preventative measures for future incidents.

**Scenario 2: 1-Star Reviews & Negative Sentiment**

* **Likelihood:** Moderate -  Initial users will always have feedback.
* **Impact:** Damage to reputation, potential for negative word-of-mouth.
* **Immediate Response (0-60 mins):**
    * **Customer Support:**  Prioritize responding to negative reviews – acknowledge, apologize for the negative experience, and offer a solution (e.g., troubleshooting steps, support contact).
    * **Sentiment Analysis:**  Utilize social listening tools (e.g., Hootsuite Insights, Brandwatch) to understand the nature of the complaints.
* **Ongoing Response (60 mins - 24 hours):**
    * **Identify Themes:**  Analyze recurring issues to pinpoint areas for app improvement.
    * **Address Specific Concerns:**  Respond directly to individual reviewers where appropriate.
    * **Solicit Positive Feedback:**  Actively encourage satisfied users to leave positive reviews.

**Scenario 3: Apple Rejection**

* **Likelihood:** Low - Apple is strict, but rejection can still occur.
* **Impact:** Delay in launch, potential user frustration, negative publicity.
* **Immediate Response (0-60 mins):**
    * **Legal/Technical Review:**  Rapidly review Apple's rejection notification to understand the specific reasons.
    * **Engineering Lead:**  Work with engineering to immediately address the violation identified by Apple.
