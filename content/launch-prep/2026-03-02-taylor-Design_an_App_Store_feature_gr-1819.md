**Overall Concept:** The graphic will lean into a sense of unease and distorted beauty. It won’t be overly flashy, but rather subtly unsettling and visually intriguing, hinting at the strangeness within the story.

**1. Colors:**

* **Dominant:** Deep, bruised purple (#663399) - This is the core color, representing the darkness, secrets, and emotional weight of the story.
* **Accent 1:** Pale, sickly yellow (#FAD7A0) - Used sparingly for key elements like the eyes or the diary itself. This adds a jarring, unsettling contrast and symbolizes sickness or delusion.
* **Accent 2:** Charcoal Grey (#333333) - Provides grounding and reinforces the dark, almost monochrome aesthetic.
* **Background:** A subtle, textured gradient fading from the bruised purple at the top to a slightly lighter, almost washed-out purple at the bottom. Think a blurred, aged paper texture subtly embedded.


**2. Layout:**

* **Central Element:**  A slightly off-kilter, partially open antique leather-bound diary. It's the focal point. Its cover is a dark, distressed brown with the faint, almost ghostly image of a single, staring eye subtly embossed onto it (more on this below).
* **Layered Effect:** The diary isn't perfectly sharp. It’s slightly out of focus, adding to the dreamlike, unsettling feeling.  Use a subtle motion blur effect.
* **Foreground Element:**  One highly detailed, unnaturally large, pale yellow eye is positioned within the diary’s opening. It's not a fully formed eye; it’s partially obscured by the pages, with a disconcerting, vacant stare.  This immediately grabs attention and symbolizes the story’s key themes. 
* **Minimalist Background:** The textured gradient background should be subtle, allowing the diary and eye to be the stars.
