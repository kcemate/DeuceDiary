**I. Understanding Your Target Influencers (Phase 1 - 1-2 Weeks)**

* **Define Your Brand/Content:** Be *extremely* clear on the type of bathroom humor you’re promoting. Are you a meme creator, a short-form video creator, a podcast host, or something else? Knowing this will dictate who you target.
* **Identify Key Platforms:** Where does your humor thrive? Primarily:
    * **TikTok:** Huge potential, especially for short, easily shareable content.
    * **Instagram Reels:** Similar to TikTok, great for visual humor.
    * **YouTube Shorts:** Growing platform for quick, punchy content.
    * **Reddit (r/Showerthoughts, r/funny, r/memes):**  Important for organic reach and community engagement – be mindful of subreddit rules!
* **Research Potential Influencers - Go Beyond Follower Count:**
    * **Micro-Influencers (1k-10k followers):**  Highest ROI potential. They have a more dedicated audience and often respond better to genuine outreach. *Focus on this group initially.*
    * **Niche Down:** Don't just look for “comedy influencers.” Search for:
        * **Meme Account Creators:** Those actively making and sharing bathroom humor memes.
        * **Dark Humor Creators:** Folks who specialize in the darker side of bathroom humor.
        * **Toilet Humor Creators:**  Specifically those who lean into the… obvious.
        * **"Dad Jokes" & Awkward Humor Creators:** Sometimes bathroom humor is just really bad jokes delivered with a straight face.
    * **Tools to Use:**
        * **NinjaPromo:**  Excellent for finding micro-influencers based on niche and engagement.
        * **Upfluence:**  More robust (and pricier) tool for deeper influencer analysis.
        * **Manual Research:**  Use hashtags like #bathroomhumor, #toiletmemes, #showerthoughts, #awkwardmemes, etc. on Instagram and TikTok to find creators.
* **Create a Spreadsheet:** Track influencers – Name, Platform, Follower Count, Engagement Rate (likes, comments, shares), Content Style, Contact Info (email/DM link).
