**I. Overall Strategy: Layered Approach - Focus on Value & Friction Reduction**

Our core strategy will be a layered approach, starting with a highly accessible experience and strategically introducing paywalls based on demonstrated value. We'll avoid a ‘hard’ paywall initially and prioritize reducing friction to conversions.

**II. Key Components & Tactics**

**1. When to Show Paywall (Trigger Points & Segmentation)**

* **Phase 1: Awareness & Engagement (0-7 Days)**
    * **Trigger:** First 3-7 visits – Show a highly engaging “Welcome” flow. This should highlight key benefits, perhaps a short demo video, and showcase the most compelling features.
    * **Paywall:** None. Focus on education and user onboarding.
* **Phase 2: Initial Value Demonstration (8-14 Days)**
    * **Trigger:** User spends > 30 minutes in the product, completes 2-3 key features, or interacts with a key educational resource.
    * **Paywall:** "Limited Access" -  Offer a snippet of premium content (e.g., 3-5 key tutorials, a reduced number of tasks, or access to a limited set of features) *behind* a soft paywall. Clearly state the value they're missing.  This is about getting them to *want* more.
* **Phase 3: Conversion Push (15-30 Days)**
    * **Trigger:** User interacts with the “Limited Access” paywall, shows intent to continue using the product, or completes a specific key action (e.g., signs up for a newsletter).
    * **Paywall:** Full Paywall - Offer the full premium version.
* **Segmentation:**  Crucially, segment users based on behavior:
    * **High-Intent:**  Users engaging with limited access and showing interest.  Target with personalized offers & incentives.
    * **Low-Intent:** Users quickly abandoning the product. Retarget with a more compelling explanation of value or simplified onboarding.
    * **Stuck Users:** Offer personalized support or a short call to address any roadblocks.


**2. Free Trial Length – Strategic Calibration**

* **Short Trial (7 Days):** For products with a steep learning curve or where the core value comes quickly.  This forces users to commit and demonstrates the immediacy of the benefits.
* **Medium Trial (14 Days):**  A good balance for most SaaS products, allowing users enough time to deeply engage and see ROI. This is our recommended starting point.
* **Long Trial (30 Days):**  Suitable for products with a longer adoption cycle or where users need significant time to integrate the product into their workflow. (Requires strong user onboarding and support.)
* **Key Metric:**  Conversion Rate from Trial to Paid – This is the ultimate measure of trial effectiveness.
