**Retention Analysis Plan**

**I. Goals & Objectives**

* **Overall Goal:**  To understand and improve user retention by identifying key drivers of churn and implementing targeted interventions.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify the primary reasons for users dropping off at D1, D7, and D30.
    * Quantify the impact of specific events, features, or onboarding experiences on retention.
    * Develop data-backed strategies to increase retention rates at each benchmark.

**II. Data Collection & Metrics**

* **Defining "Retention":**  Crucially, *what does retention mean for you?*  This will shape your metrics. Examples:
    * **DAU/MAU (Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users):** A standard measure of engagement.
    * **30-Day Retention:** Percentage of users who used the product/service 30 days after their initial signup. (This is the core of your plan)
    * **Churn Rate:** Percentage of users who stopped using the product/service within a specific period. (Tracked by cohort - see below)
    * **Feature Adoption Rate:** Percentage of users who used key features within a certain timeframe.
    * **Session Length:** Average duration of user sessions.
    * **Frequency of Use:**  How often users are returning to the product.
* **Cohort Analysis:** This is *essential*. Group users based on their signup date (e.g., all users who signed up in January 2024).  This allows you to see how retention varies over time *within* specific groups. This is the foundation of your D1/D7/D30 analysis.
* **Data Sources:**
    * **Product Analytics:** (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Firebase Analytics, etc.) – For tracking user behavior within your product.
    * **CRM/Customer Database:**  For storing user information and tracking interactions (emails, support tickets).
    * **User Surveys:** (NPS, exit surveys, in-app feedback prompts) – Qualitative data to understand *why* users are leaving.
    * **Marketing Automation Platform:**  To track user journeys and identify drop-off points in the onboarding process.
