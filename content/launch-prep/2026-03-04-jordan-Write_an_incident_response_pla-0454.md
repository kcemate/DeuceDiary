**1. Introduction**

This Incident Response Plan (IRP) outlines the procedures for identifying, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and learning from security incidents affecting [Your Organization Name].  The goal is to minimize damage, restore operations quickly, and prevent future incidents. This plan is regularly reviewed and updated.

**2. Definitions**

* **Incident:** Any event that could potentially violate security policies, compromise confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information assets, or disrupt business operations. This includes but is not limited to malware infections, unauthorized access, data breaches, denial-of-service attacks, and phishing attempts.
* **Severity Levels:**
    * **P0 – Critical (Immediate Response Required):**  Significant impact on business operations, data loss, legal ramifications, or major reputational damage.  Requires immediate activation of the entire IRP.
    * **P1 – High (Prompt Response Required):**  Moderate impact on business operations, potential for data compromise, or noticeable security vulnerability. Requires a rapid response within [e.g., 2 hours].
    * **P2 – Medium (Investigative Response Required):**  Limited impact on business operations, potential minor security vulnerability, or isolated incident. Requires a response within [e.g., 8 hours].
    * **P3 – Low (Monitoring & Documentation Required):**  Minimal impact on business operations, potential minor disruption, or informational incident. Requires documentation and monitoring.
