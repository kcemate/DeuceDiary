## Deuce Diary Launch: Crisis Playbook

**Goal:** Minimize negative impact on brand reputation, maintain positive momentum, and quickly resolve issues during the launch of “Deuce Diary.”

**Core Principles:** Transparency, Empathy, Speed, and Control.

**I. Risk Assessment & Trigger Points (Pre-Launch)**

* **High Priority Risks:**
    * **Technical Glitches:** Website crashes, payment processing errors, functionality bugs.
    * **Negative Reviews (Early):**  Bad initial feedback highlighting issues with content, quality, or expectations.
    * **Social Media Backlash:**  Viral negativity due to controversial content, perceived misrepresentation, or insensitive messaging.
    * **Inventory Issues (If applicable):** Stock shortages, shipping delays.
    * **Misinterpretation of Content:** Audience misunderstanding key themes or intended messages.
* **Trigger Points:**
    * Website downtime exceeding 5 minutes.
    * Over 50 negative reviews in 24 hours.
    * Significant spike in negative social media mentions (trending hashtags related to the brand).
    * Reports of fraudulent activity.

**II. Roles & Responsibilities (During Crisis)**

* **Crisis Lead (Initially: [Marketing Director/Head of Communications])**: Overall responsibility, decision-making authority, escalation point.
* **Social Media Manager:** Monitor social media channels, manage responses, amplify positive sentiment, escalate issues.
* **Community Manager:** Engage with comments and questions on the website and forums, moderate discussions.
* **Customer Support Team:** Handle direct customer complaints and inquiries, gather information.
* **Technical Team:** Investigate and resolve technical issues, provide updates.
* **PR/Communications Team:** Draft and disseminate official statements, manage media inquiries.


**III. Response Phases & Actions**

**Phase 1: Immediate Response (0-2 Hours - Recognition & Initial Assessment)**

* **Monitor:** Constantly monitor all channels – website analytics, social media (Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook), review sites (Amazon, Goodreads), email inboxes.
* **Activate Alert System:** Trigger the “Crisis Team” notification.
* **Initial Statement (Social Media - Fast, Concise):** "We're aware of the situation and working to address it.  Thank you for your patience."  (Avoid lengthy explanations)
* **Technical Team Investigation:** Begin immediate investigation into reported technical issues.

**Phase 2: Containment & Damage Control (2-6 Hours - Addressing the Root Cause)**

* **Public Statement (Website/Social Media – More Detailed):** Acknowledge the problem, express regret, explain the steps being taken to resolve it. (Example: “We experienced a temporary website issue due to high traffic. Our team is working to scale our infrastructure to prevent this from happening again. We apologize for the inconvenience.”)
* **Customer Support Focused:**  Prioritize resolving individual customer issues. Empower support team to offer refunds, discounts, or alternative solutions where appropriate.
* **Social Media Engagement:**  Actively respond to comments and messages.  Address concerns directly and empathetically. Don’t delete negative comments unless they are abusive or violate platform guidelines.
* **Update Tech Team & Track Progress:**  Regular updates from technical team on resolution progress.

**Phase 3: Long-Term Resolution & Reputation Repair (6+ Hours - Proactive Communication)**

* **Detailed Explanation (Website/Blog Post/FAQ):** Provide a more thorough explanation of the issue, the root cause, and the solutions implemented.
* **Proactive Communication:**  Regularly update customers and the public on progress. Consider a short video addressing the concerns.
* **Community Engagement:**  Run a Q&A session with the author or creative team.  Solicit feedback and demonstrate a willingness to learn.
* **Monitor & Adapt:** Continue monitoring
