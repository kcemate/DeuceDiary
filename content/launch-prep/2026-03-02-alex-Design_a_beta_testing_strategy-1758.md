**I. Overall Goals & Philosophy**

* **Goal:** Identify and resolve critical bugs, gather user feedback on core diary features (entry creation, organization, privacy controls), and refine the user experience before a wider public launch.
* **Philosophy:** Start small, learn quickly, and iterate based on real-world usage. Prioritize ease of feedback collection and quick turnaround on bug fixes.

**II. Beta Group Segmentation & Invitations**

We'll use a tiered approach, gradually increasing the size and engagement of the beta group.

* **Phase 1: The "Founding Diary Keepers" (5-10 Users)**
    * **Selection Criteria:** Individuals who represent our core target demographic (likely young adults, creative types, potentially those interested in self-improvement or journaling).  Crucially, they need to be technically comfortable and actively engage with apps.
    * **Recruitment:** Found through early interest via social media, pre-launch signup list, and potentially through a small community forum we've quietly built.
    * **Invitation Process:** Personalized invites through TestFlight, emphasizing the opportunity to shape the app.
* **Phase 2: “Early Adopters” (25-50 Users)**
    * **Selection Criteria:** Users from Phase 1 who consistently engaged with the app and provided helpful feedback.  We'll also target those who fit our demographic but haven’t yet engaged.
    * **Recruitment:** Expansion of the initial invite list, potentially offering small incentives (e.g., early access to a premium feature, a small gift card).
    * **Invitation Process:** TestFlight invites based on positive feedback & engagement from Phase 1.
* **Phase 3: “Community Test Group” (75-100 Users)** - *This phase starts after Phase 2 is stable.*
    * **Selection Criteria:** Users from Phases 1 & 2.  We'll prioritize users who've consistently provided valuable insights and demonstrated a willingness to experiment.
    * **Recruitment:** Expand through targeted social media advertising (Facebook/Instagram/TikTok), app store reviews (actively soliciting reviews after initial users have had a chance to try), and potentially through partnerships with relevant blogs or communities.



**III. Feedback Collection Strategy**

* **In-App Feedback Tools:**
    * **TestFlight Feedback Form:**  TestFlight provides a basic feedback form for reporting bugs and general impressions. We'll actively encourage users to utilize this.
    * **Short Surveys (Within the App):** Implement short, targeted surveys (e.g., using Typeform or Google Forms) triggered after specific actions (e.g., creating a new entry, using a particular feature).  Keep these *very* concise (3-5 questions max).
    * **Rating Prompts:**  Implement a simple rating prompt after a user completes a diary entry. ("How would you rate your experience creating this entry?")
* **Dedicated Communication Channel:**
    * **Discord Server/Slack Channel:** Create a dedicated channel for beta testers to discuss the app, share ideas, and quickly get help.  This encourages peer-to-peer learning and faster problem identification.
* **Regular Check-ins:**
    * **Weekly Team Debriefs:**  The development team will hold brief weekly meetings to discuss the feedback received, prioritize issues, and plan for the next iteration.
    * **Scheduled 1:1s:**  Assign a lead beta tester to have regular one-on-one sessions to delve deeper into specific areas.
