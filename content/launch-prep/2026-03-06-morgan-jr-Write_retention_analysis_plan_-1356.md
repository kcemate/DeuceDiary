**III. Key Metrics & Definitions**

* **Retention Rate:**  The percentage of users who are still active after a given period.  (Calculated as: (Number of Users Active at Day X – Number of New Users Acquired at Day X) / Number of Users Acquired at Day X) * 100
* **Cohort:** A group of users who started using the product within the same time period (e.g., all users who signed up in January).  This allows us to analyze retention trends for specific user groups.
* **DAU (Daily Active Users):**  The number of unique users who engage with the product on a daily basis.
* **WAU (Weekly Active Users):**  The number of unique users who engage with the product on a weekly basis.
* **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** The number of unique users who engage with the product on a monthly basis.
* **Churn Rate:** The inverse of retention rate, representing the rate at which users stop using the product.
* **Time to First Value (TTFV):** The time it takes a new user to experience the core value proposition of the product.  (Critical for early retention).
* **Feature Usage:**  Tracking which features users are utilizing, and the frequency of use.
* **User Segmentation:**  Grouping users based on demographics, behavior, and other relevant criteria (e.g., by industry, user role, engagement level).
