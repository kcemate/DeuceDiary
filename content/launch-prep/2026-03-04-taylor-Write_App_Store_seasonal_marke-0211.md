**Important Notes Before We Begin:**

* **App Category Matters:** This calendar is general. The relevance of themes and activities will shift depending on your app's category (e.g., a fitness app will focus heavily on New Year’s resolutions, while a cooking app will emphasize holiday recipes).
* **ASO (App Store Optimization) is Ongoing:** Don’t just think about seasonal marketing. Continuous ASO – keyword research, title/description optimization, screenshot/video updates – is *essential* for success, especially during peak seasons.
* **Paid Advertising:** Consider paid Apple Search Ads, Google App Campaigns, and social media ads to boost reach during key times.
* **Tracking & Analytics:** Use App Store Connect Analytics, Firebase, and other tools to track your campaign performance and make data-driven adjustments.


**I. Spring (March – May)**

* **Theme:** Renewal, Fresh Starts, Outdoors, Gardening, Wellbeing
* **Key Dates:** March - Spring Equinox; April - Easter; May - Mother's Day
* **Marketing Activities:**
    * **Launch/Promote New Features:** Spring is a great time to highlight features related to wellbeing, outdoor activities, or new content.
    * **Run Contests/Giveaways:** “Share your spring goals” photo contest, related to your app’s functionality.
    * **Blog Posts & Content:**  “5 Spring Wellness Tips,” “Easy Spring Recipes,” “How to Plan Your Garden” (if relevant). Link to these from your app’s store page.
    * **Social Media Campaigns:**  Focus on bright, visually appealing content.
    * **ASO:**  Update keywords to reflect spring-related searches.
* **Metrics:** Downloads, Installs, Conversion Rate (from impression to install), App Store Ranking, Social Media Engagement.

* **Metrics:** Downloads, Installs, Conversion Rate, App Store Ranking, Website Traffic, Social Media Reach.
