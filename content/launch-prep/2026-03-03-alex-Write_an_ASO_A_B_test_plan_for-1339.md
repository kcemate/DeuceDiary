## ASO A/B Test Plan: App Store Optimization

This plan outlines an A/B testing strategy for optimizing key App Store elements to drive installs, engagement, and ultimately, revenue.

**I. Goals & Metrics:**

* **Primary Goal:** Increase App Installs & Conversions
* **Secondary Goals:** Improve App Store Ranking, Drive Engagement (Retention, Session Length)
* **Key Metrics:**
    * **Install Rate (IR):** Percentage of users who install the app after viewing the app page. (Crucial)
    * **Conversion Rate (CR):** Percentage of users who click the “Install” button.
    * **Click-Through Rate (CTR) – Title:**  Percentage of users who view the app page after seeing the title.
    * **CTR – Keywords:** Percentage of users who view the app page after seeing the keywords.
    * **Reviews & Ratings:** Average Rating & Number of Reviews.
    * **Session Length:** Average time users spend in the app.
    * **Retention Rate:** Percentage of users who return to the app after a specific period.

Here's a breakdown of elements we’ll test, along with our hypotheses:

| **App Title**       | [Current Title]           | [Slightly Shorter Title] | Shorter titles tend to perform better on screen size devices. | IR, CTR (Title)        | 2-4 Weeks     |

**III. Testing Methodology:**

* **A/B Testing Platform:** Utilize a dedicated ASO platform like AppFollow, Mobile Action, or Sensor Tower that supports A/B testing.  These provide automated testing, statistical analysis, and reporting.
* **Traffic Allocation:** Start with a 50/50 split between Variants A and B. This allows for initial data gathering.
* **Statistical Significance:** Define a minimum statistical significance level (e.g., 95% confidence interval). Most ASO tools automatically calculate this.  Generally, aiming for a sample size of at least 1000 installs per variant is recommended.
* **Segmentation:** Consider segmenting your audience for more granular analysis (e.g., by device type, country, user cohort).
