## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines how to effectively track and analyze your competition, allowing you to identify opportunities, understand threats, and ultimately improve your strategy.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:**  Gain a comprehensive understanding of your competitive landscape to inform strategic decision-making, identify areas for improvement, and stay ahead of the curve.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key competitors and their strengths/weaknesses.
    * Monitor competitor activities (product launches, pricing changes, marketing campaigns).
    * Benchmark your performance against competitors across key metrics.
    * Detect emerging trends and potential disruptive threats.
    * Support strategic planning (product development, marketing, sales).


**II. Competitor Identification & Prioritization:**

* **List Competitors:** Create a comprehensive list of direct, indirect, and potential competitors.
    * **Direct:** Companies offering similar products/services to the same target market.
    * **Indirect:** Companies offering alternative solutions addressing the same customer need.
    * **Potential:** Companies that could enter your market or expand their offerings.
* **Prioritization Matrix:** Rank competitors based on their:
    * **Market Share:** % of the market they control.
    * **Revenue:**  Their overall revenue figures (if available).
    * **Growth Rate:** How quickly they are expanding.
    * **Strategic Importance:**  Their potential impact on your business.
    * **Frequency of Changes:** How frequently they adjust their offerings and strategies.
    * **Example Matrix:**
        | Competitor | Market Share | Revenue | Growth Rate | Strategic Importance | Frequency of Changes | Priority |
        |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
        | Competitor A | 30% | $50M | 15% | High | High | 1 |
        | Competitor B | 15% | $25M | 20% | Medium | Medium | 2 |
        | Competitor C | 5% | $10M | 25% | Low | Low | 3 |


**III. Metrics Tracking Categories & Specific Metrics:**

Divide your tracking into key categories to ensure you're capturing the most relevant data.

**A. Product/Service Metrics:**

* **New Product Launches:** Date, features, pricing.
* **Product Roadmap:** (If publicly available - a strong indicator of future direction)
* **Features & Functionality:** Track additions and improvements.
* **Innovation Rate:**  How frequently they release new features or services.
* **Customer Reviews & Ratings:** Monitor sentiment across various platforms (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, etc.)
* **Technical Debt:** (If possible to assess - indicates potential future development needs)

**B. Marketing & Sales Metrics:**

* **Website Traffic:** (Source, bounce rate, time on site) - Use tools like Google Analytics.
* **Social Media Engagement:** (Follower growth, likes, shares, comments) - Track across relevant platforms.
* **Advertising Spend:** (Platforms, budget, target audience) - Use SEMrush, SpyFu.
* **Content Marketing:** (Blog posts, ebooks, webinars - downloads, views) – Track website analytics & content platforms.
* **Lead Generation Metrics:** (Cost per lead, lead conversion rates) –  Track within CRM.
* **Sales Cycle Length:** (Average time to close a deal) –  Track within CRM.
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**  Total marketing and sales spend / number of new customers.
