**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store**

1. **Violating Apple's App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the biggest culprit. The guidelines are complex, constantly evolving, and incredibly strict. They cover almost every aspect of an app.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that falsely advertise their features, collect data without consent, or trick users into spending money are almost guaranteed to be rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  This goes beyond simply collecting data. Apple scrutinizes how data is collected, stored, and used. Apps need clear privacy policies and proper user consent mechanisms.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted content (music, images, software) without permission is a major red flag.
5. **Malfunctioning Apps / Bugs:** Apps with significant bugs, crashes, or unreliable performance are rejected.
6. **Security Vulnerabilities:**  Apps must not have known security flaws that could expose users to harm. This includes data breaches and vulnerabilities to malware.
7. **Lack of Clear Functionality:**  Users need to understand what an app *does* immediately.  Ambiguous descriptions or unclear UI can lead to rejection.
8. **Inappropriate Content:** This includes violence, hate speech, sexually explicit material, and content that is otherwise deemed offensive or harmful.
9. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases, unclear pricing, or violating Apple’s payment terms can lead to rejection.
10. **App Store Metadata Issues:** Problems with the app’s title, subtitle, description, keywords, screenshots, and category can all trigger a rejection.
