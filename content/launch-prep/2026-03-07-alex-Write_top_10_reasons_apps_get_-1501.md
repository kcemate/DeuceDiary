**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (According to Apple's Review Guidelines)**

1. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted images, music, trademarks, or code without permission. This is the *most* common reason for rejection.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Behavior:**  Apps that falsely claim to perform certain functions, make unrealistic promises, or trick users into spending money.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting user data without proper consent, failing to clearly explain data usage practices (privacy policy), or violating data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA).
4. **Malware/Security Risks:** Apps containing viruses, malware, or any code that could compromise user devices or data.
5. **Functionality Issues:** App crashes, bugs, or doesn’t work as advertised. This includes poor user experience, confusing navigation, and generally unreliable performance.
6. **Content Policy Violations:**  This covers a wide range of issues, including:
   *   **Adult Content:**  Sexually explicit material, nudity (unless approved for specific categories).
   *   **Violence & Graphic Content:** Depictions of graphic violence, cruelty, or illegal activities.
   *   **Hate Speech & Discrimination:** Content promoting discrimination or hatred based on race, religion, gender, etc.
7. **App Store Guidelines Violations (General):** Any violation of Apple’s broad set of rules, including submitting apps that aren’t properly categorized, have inappropriate descriptions, or misleading screenshots.
8. **Payment Issues:**  Incorrect payment information, failing to comply with Apple’s payment processing requirements, or deceptive in-app purchase practices.
9. **Tracking Issues:**  Using tracking technologies (like third-party analytics) without proper disclosure and user consent, particularly for targeted advertising.
10. **App Not Meeting Technical Requirements:** Apps that don't meet Apple's minimum technical requirements for iOS versions, screen sizes, or operating systems.
