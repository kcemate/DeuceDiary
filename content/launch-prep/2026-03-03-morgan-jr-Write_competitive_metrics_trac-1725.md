## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines a framework for consistently monitoring and analyzing your competitor's activities to inform your strategy and maintain a competitive edge. It focuses on key areas and provides a roadmap for implementation and ongoing review.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:** To proactively identify competitor strengths, weaknesses, and strategies to optimize our own performance and maintain/gain market share.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Understand competitor product offerings and pricing strategies.
    * Identify competitor marketing and sales tactics.
    * Monitor competitor online presence and brand reputation.
    * Respond effectively to competitor moves and disruptions.

**II. Key Metric Categories & Specific Metrics:**

This plan categorizes metrics for clarity and focuses on actionable data.

**A. Product & Innovation:**

* **New Product Launches:** Track frequency, features, and messaging. (Quantitative - Number of launches per quarter, Feature analysis)
* **Product Roadmap:**  Monitor announced updates and future plans (Gather via company announcements, industry reports, competitor websites)
* **Innovation Rate:**  Measure the introduction of disruptive features or technologies. (Qualitative -  Assess impact and frequency of innovation)
* **Feature Adoption:** Track the usage of competitor features (Requires access to market research or analyst reports).

**B. Pricing & Promotions:**

* **Pricing Changes:** Monitor fluctuations in pricing across product lines. (Quantitative - Price tracking spreadsheets, competitive price comparison tools)
* **Promotional Offers:** Track frequency, type, and duration of discounts and promotions. (Quantitative - Monitor competitor websites, emails, and social media)
* **Bundling Strategies:** Identify and analyze bundled product offerings. (Qualitative – Analyze bundle contents and value proposition)

**C. Marketing & Sales:**

* **Website Traffic:** Monitor competitor website traffic using tools like SEMrush, Ahrefs, or SimilarWeb. (Quantitative - Monthly/Weekly traffic trends, bounce rate, time on site)
* **SEO Ranking:** Track competitor keywords and search engine rankings. (Quantitative - Keyword ranking tools)
* **Social Media Engagement:** Analyze competitor social media metrics:
    * **Follower Growth:** Number of followers on key platforms. (Quantitative - Monthly/Weekly growth rates)
    * **Engagement Rate:** Likes, shares, comments per post. (Quantitative - Calculated percentage of engagement)
    * **Content Strategy:** Types of content, frequency, and messaging. (Qualitative - Content audit)
* **Advertising Spend:** (Difficult to track directly, relies on industry reports and analyst estimates) – Estimate based on ad volume and platform data.
* **Lead Generation Tactics:**  Identify channels competitor utilizes (e.g., webinars, content marketing, paid advertising). (Qualitative – Analysis of competitor marketing materials)

**D. Customer Experience & Reputation:**

* **Online Reviews:** Monitor reviews on platforms like G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Yelp. (Quantitative - Review volume, star ratings, sentiment analysis)
* **Social Listening:** Track brand mentions and conversations related to your competitors. (Qualitative -  Sentiment analysis, identify trends & concerns)
* **Customer Support Channels:**  Analyze competitor support channels (e.g., chat, email) to understand common issues and pain points. (Qualitative -  Review support transcripts or analyze support forums).


**III. Tracking Tools & Resources:**

* **Competitive Intelligence Platforms:** SEMrush, Ahrefs, SimilarWeb, SpyFu, Moz
* **Social Listening Tools:** Mention, Brand24, Hootsuite Insights
* **Review Monitoring Tools:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Yelp (API integration possible for automation)
* **Spreadsheet Software:** Google Sheets, Microsoft Excel (For data consolidation and analysis)
* **CRM System:** Integrate competitor data into your CRM for a holistic view.
