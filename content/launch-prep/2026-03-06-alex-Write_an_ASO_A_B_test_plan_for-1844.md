## ASO A/B Test Plan: App Store Elements

This plan outlines an A/B testing strategy for key App Store elements, focusing on optimizing for installs, engagement, and ultimately, revenue.

**1. Goals & Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

* **Overall Goal:** Increase app installs, user engagement, and potentially revenue.
* **KPIs:**
    * **Install Conversion Rate (ICR):** Percentage of users who view the app page and install the app. (Primary Metric)
    * **Click-Through Rate (CTR) - App Icon:** Percentage of users who see the app icon in search or browsing and click on it.
    * **Click-Through Rate (CTR) - App Preview Video:** Percentage of users who see the app preview video and click on it.
    * **Keyword Ranking:** Monitor keyword rankings for target terms. (Secondary Metric – reflects performance)
    * **App Page Conversion Rate (APC):** Percentage of users who view the app page and install the app. (Secondary Metric - To understand overall impact)
    * **Session Length/Engagement Metrics:** (If applicable & trackable - e.g., number of levels completed in a game)

**2. Elements to Test (A/B Variables)**

We’ll focus on testing the following elements, one at a time for focused analysis:

* **App Icon:** Different color palettes, shapes, icons, and even simplified versions.
* **App Preview Video:**  Different scripts, music, visuals, length (short vs. long), calls to action.
* **App Title:** Shortened vs. Full title, incorporating relevant keywords.
* **App Subtitle:** Different value propositions and concise descriptions.
* **App Keywords:** Testing different combinations of primary and secondary keywords.
* **App Description (Short):** Headlines, key benefits, and a strong call to action.
* **App Description (Long):** Rephrasing benefits, adding social proof, showcasing features.


**3. A/B Test Structure & Process**

* **Test Duration:** 2-4 Weeks per element. Longer for lower volume areas.
* **Traffic Allocation:**  Start with a 50/50 split (A/B) and gradually increase the percentage allocated to the winning variant based on performance.
* **Test Groups:**
    * **Group A (Control):**  Existing version of the app store element.
    * **Group B (Variant):**  The new element being tested.
* **Monitoring & Analysis:**
    * **Daily Monitoring:** Track KPIs in App Store Connect (iOS) or Google Play Console (Android).
    * **Weekly Reporting:** Analyze the performance of both groups and identify trends.
    * **Statistical Significance:**  Utilize A/B testing tools (e.g., Apptimize, Adjust, Branch) to determine statistical significance of the results. A margin of error of 5-10% is generally considered acceptable for mobile app A/B testing.

**4. Example Test: App Icon – Color Palette Variation**

* **Hypothesis:** A brighter, more vibrant color palette in the app icon will result in a higher CTR.
* **Variants (B):**
    * **B1:** Existing App Icon (Control)
    * **B2:** App Icon with a brighter blue and orange palette.
* **Metrics:** CTR (App Icon), Install Conversion Rate
* **Timeline:** 2 Weeks
* **Decision Criteria:** If B2 shows a statistically significant (5-10% or higher) increase in CTR compared to B1, it becomes the new standard.
