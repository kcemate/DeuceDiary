**I. Overall Philosophy: Data-Driven & Iterative**

* **Core Principle:**  Maximize revenue without sacrificing user acquisition or long-term engagement. We'll treat this as an experiment, constantly testing different strategies and measuring their impact.
* **Key Metrics:**  We’ll track everything!
    * **Conversion Rates:** Free-to-Paid (FTP) rate, Trial-to-Paid rate, Page-View-to-Conversion rate.
    * **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)**
    * **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
    * **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV)** – *Crucial* for informing decisions.

**II. Paywall Strategy - When & How**

* **Phase 1: Early Access/Discovery (0-7 Days)**
    * **Strategy:** Full access - No paywall. Goal:  Maximize initial engagement & feature discovery.
    * **Rationale:**  Allows users to deeply experience the product's value before introducing a barrier.
* **Phase 2: Gentle Introduction (8-14 Days)**
    * **Trigger:**  Based on User Behavior:
        * **Low Engagement:** Users exploring only a small number of features.
        * **Limited Session Duration:**  Frequent logouts, short sessions.
        * **High Bounce Rate:** Leaving specific pages quickly.
    * **Paywall Type:** “Soft Paywall” –  Restrict access to premium features *specifically* those contributing to the core value.  Perhaps limit advanced analytics, collaborative tools, or higher resolution exports.
    * **Messaging:** “Unlock Advanced Features” – Focused on the immediate benefit of the restricted content.
* **Phase 3: Targeted Prompts (15-30 Days)**
    * **Trigger:** Based on user actions - If a user repeatedly attempts to access restricted content after the soft paywall.
    * **Paywall Type:**  “Feature-Based” – Offer specific premium features as in-app upsells.
    * **Messaging:** “Take your [task] to the next level!” – Tailored to the user's current activity.
* **Phase 4:  Limited Time Offers (30+ Days)** -  For users who haven't converted.
    * **Offer:** Discounted subscription, bonus content, or extended trial.


**III. Free Trial Length - Data-Driven Experimentation**

* **Baseline:** 7 Days -  A common starting point, but not universally optimal.
* **Experimentation:**  Run A/B tests with different trial lengths:
    * **3 Days:**  High intensity, rapid value demonstration - potentially higher FTP rate for time-sensitive users.
    * **14 Days:**  Good balance – allows for sufficient exploration and habit formation.
    * **30 Days:**  Longer, more immersive – ideal for products with a steep learning curve or complex features.
* **Metrics to Watch:** FTP rate, engagement during trial, churn rate during trial.
