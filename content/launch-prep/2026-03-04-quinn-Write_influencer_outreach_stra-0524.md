Okay, let's craft an influencer outreach strategy specifically designed for bathroom humor creators – think guys like Ryan George, Tom Papa, and even some of the more sophisticated takes like Joe Santagato. This strategy needs to acknowledge their niche and build genuine connections.

**I. Understanding Your Goals & Target Influencers**

* **What are you promoting?** Be crystal clear. Is it a brand, a product, a service, or simply driving traffic to your own content? This dictates your messaging.
* **Define "Bathroom Humor":** It’s broader than just crude jokes. It’s clever, observational, self-deprecating, often with a bit of philosophical or psychological undercurrent.  Your influencer selection should align with this.
* **Tiered Influencer Approach:**
    * **Micro-Influencers (1K-10K followers):** Highest potential ROI. Often more engaged audiences, more open to genuine collaborations. Focus on relationship building.
    * **Mid-Tier Influencers (10K-100K followers):** Wider reach, but can be harder to connect with authentically.
    * **Macro-Influencers (100K+ followers):** Biggest reach, but usually more expensive and may require a more formal approach. (Generally, only target these if you have a significant budget).


**II. Identifying & Researching Influencers**

* **Platform Focus:** Primarily YouTube & TikTok (but also consider Instagram & even Twitch if their content is live).
* **Search Terms:** Don't just search “bathroom humor.” Use variations like:
    * “Dad jokes”
    * “Observational comedy”
    * “Dry humor”
    * "Dark humor"
    * "Philosophical jokes"
    * “Funny thoughts”
* **Tools:**
    * **Social Blade:** Excellent for researching follower counts, engagement rates, and trends.
    * **BuzzSumo:** Find influencers based on content engagement.
    * **Manual Search:**  Browse relevant YouTube channels, TikTok feeds, and comment sections.
* **Key Metrics to Evaluate:**
    * **Engagement Rate:**  (Likes + Comments) / Followers – This is *crucial*.  High follower counts mean nothing if engagement is low. Aim for 3-5% or higher.
    * **Audience Demographics:**  Make sure their audience aligns with your target demographic.
    * **Content Style:**  Does their humor style mesh with your brand?  Don’t try to force a square peg into a round hole.
    * **Authenticity:**  Do they seem genuine? Are they consistently creating high-quality content?

* **Personalized Messages - NO COPY PASTE!** This is *essential* for bathroom humor creators. They’ve heard it all.
    * **Reference Specific Content:**  "I really enjoyed your video on [specific topic] – it had me thinking..." This shows you've actually *watched* their work.
