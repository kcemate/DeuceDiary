## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook

This playbook outlines our response strategy for potential crises during the launch of Deuce Diary. It’s designed to be adaptable and prioritize user experience, brand reputation, and rapid problem-solving.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be upfront and honest with our users. Don’t hide problems.
* **Speed:** Rapid response is crucial, especially in the initial days.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge user frustrations and demonstrate we understand.
* **Collaboration:** Internal teams (Dev, Marketing, PR, Customer Support) will work together seamlessly.
* **Data-Driven:** Use analytics and user feedback to inform our decisions.


**II. Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Server Crash - (Highest Priority)**

* **Likelihood:** Medium - Launch traffic can strain initial infrastructure.
* **Impact:** Significant user frustration, loss of potential users, negative word-of-mouth.
* **Immediate Actions (0-15 mins):**
    * **Internal Alert:** Dev team receives immediate notification and is mobilized.
    * **Monitoring:** Intensify server monitoring and track the extent of the crash.
    * **Status Page:** Immediately update our Status Page (e.g., Status.io) with clear, concise information: "We are experiencing technical difficulties. Our team is working to resolve the issue and restore full functionality."  Include estimated time to resolution if possible.
    * **Social Media:** Post to all social media channels acknowledging the issue and directing users to the Status Page. Example: "We're aware some users are experiencing issues accessing Deuce Diary. Our team is on it! Check our Status Page for updates: [Link]"
* **Short-Term Actions (15-60 mins):**
    * **Troubleshooting:** Dev team identifies the root cause and implements a fix.
    * **Scaling:** Begin scaling server resources if possible.
    * **Communication Updates:** Regularly update Status Page and social media with progress.
* **Long-Term Actions (60+ mins):**
    * **Post-Incident Review:** Analyze the cause of the crash to prevent future occurrences. 
    * **Proactive Communication:**  Reach out to affected users directly (via email or in-app message) to apologize and provide updates.


**Scenario 2: 1-Star Reviews - (High Priority)**

* **Likelihood:** High - Initial users will be critical.
* **Impact:** Damage to app store rating, discourages new users, reflects underlying problems.
* **Immediate Actions (0-30 mins):**
    * **Monitor App Stores:** Track review volume and content across Apple App Store & Google Play.
    * **Identify Themes:** Group negative reviews by common complaints (UI, bugs, content, value).
    * **Customer Support Response:**  Immediately respond to negative reviews - acknowledge the issue, apologize, and offer a direct solution (e.g., support ticket, beta access).
* **Short-Term Actions (30-60 mins):**
    * **Prioritize Bug Fixes:** Dev team prioritizes addressing the most frequent complaints identified in reviews.
    * **A/B Testing:**  Run A/B tests on UI elements frequently flagged in reviews.
* **Long-Term Actions (60+ mins):**
    * **Review & Iterate:** Use review data to inform future app updates and improvements.
    * **Proactive Outreach:** Identify and engage with users leaving negative reviews – offer assistance and demonstrate responsiveness.



**Scenario 3: Apple Rejection - (High Priority)**

* **Likelihood:** Low -  But a significant disruption.
* **Impact:** Delayed launch, frustrated users, potential loss of momentum.
