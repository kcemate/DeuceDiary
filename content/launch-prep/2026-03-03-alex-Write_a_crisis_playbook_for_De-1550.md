## Deuce Diary Launch: Crisis Playbook

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** October 26, 2023
**Purpose:** To provide a clear, actionable plan for responding to potential crises during the launch of “Deuce Diary.”
**Key Teams:** Marketing, PR, Customer Support, Social Media, Legal

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be honest and upfront with our audience.
* **Speed:** Respond quickly, even if initial information is incomplete.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge concerns and show understanding.
* **Proactive Communication:** Don’t wait for problems to escalate; anticipate and address potential issues.
* **Centralized Command:** This playbook is the central source of truth; all communications should be vetted and coordinated.


**II. Potential Crisis Categories & Response Protocols:**

**1. Technical Issues (Website Downtime, Payment Failures, Bugs):**

* **Severity:** High - Immediate Action Required
* **Timeline:** 15 minutes - 1 hour
* **Response:**
    * **Team Lead:** CTO/Lead Developer - Initial assessment, escalation.
    * **Communication:**
        * **Social Media (PR):**  “We're aware of an issue affecting [specific problem] and our team is working to resolve it ASAP. We’ll provide updates every [X minutes/hour].  Thank you for your patience!” (Include a link to status updates - a dedicated status page is ideal).
        * **Customer Support:**  Prepare FAQ addressing common issues, prioritize affected customers.
        * **Website/Email:**  Automated message confirming issue and estimated resolution time.
    * **Resolution:** Prioritize bug fixes and system stabilization. Communicate progress regularly.


**2. Negative Social Media Sentiment (Backlash, Criticism, Troll Activity):**

* **Severity:** Medium - Monitor & Respond
* **Timeline:** Immediate - Ongoing Monitoring
* **Response:**
    * **Team Lead:** Social Media Manager
    * **Monitoring:** Utilize social listening tools to track mentions of “Deuce Diary” and related keywords.
    * **Response Strategy:**
        * **Ignore Trolls:** Don’t engage with disruptive behavior.
        * **Positive Engagement:** Respond to genuine positive comments and questions.
        * **Address Concerns:**  Respond directly to legitimate criticisms with respectful, factual information.  “We appreciate your feedback. [Explain our reasoning behind X].  We’re always striving to improve.”
        * **Escalate:**  If the criticism contains threats, hate speech, or personal attacks, immediately escalate to Legal.

**3. Legal & Copyright Concerns (Similarity to Existing Works, DMCA Takedowns):**

* **Severity:** High - Legal Counsel Required
* **Timeline:** 1-2 hours
* **Response:**
    * **Team Lead:** Legal Counsel
    * **Immediate Action:**  Temporarily suspend any content flagged by DMCA or other legal notices.
    * **Investigation:**  Legal team investigates the claims – scope, validity, and potential liability.
    * **Communication:**  Legal Counsel will draft a statement addressing the issue, coordinating with PR & Marketing.

**4. Customer Support Overload (Rapid influx of inquiries, overwhelming support channels):**

* **Severity:** Medium - Monitor & Scale
* **Timeline:** Immediate - Ongoing
* **Response:**
    * **Team Lead:** Customer Support Manager
    * **Monitoring:** Track volume of inquiries across all channels.
    * **Scaling:**  Increase staffing, implement chatbot responses for basic questions, prioritize urgent issues.
    * **Knowledge Base:**  Ensure a comprehensive FAQ and help documentation is readily available.
