**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024 - App Store policies evolve!)**

1. **Policy Violations:** This is the *biggest* culprit. Apple's App Store Review Guidelines are incredibly detailed and can change frequently. Common policy violations include things like misleading users, infringing copyrights, or violating age restrictions.
2. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted images, music, trademarks, or code without permission is a near-guaranteed rejection.
3. **Functionality Issues:** Bugs, crashes, poor performance, or lack of functionality that the app promises are major red flags.
4. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that trick users into making purchases, hide costs, or make false claims are rejected.
5. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting or using user data in a way that violates Apple's privacy guidelines (especially around tracking and location) leads to rejection. This is *especially* critical with newer App Tracking Transparency requirements.
6. **Lack of Clear Information:** Missing descriptions, screenshots, or support information make it difficult for Apple to assess the app.
7. **Apple ID/Developer Issues:** Problems with your developer account (e.g., outdated information, incorrect payment details) will cause rejection.
8. **Adult Content (Even Mild):** The App Store has strict rules around nudity, suggestive content, and violence, even if the app isn't overtly explicit.
9. **Unusual or Suspicious Behavior:** Apps that exhibit behaviors that Apple flags as potentially malicious (e.g., excessive data usage, unauthorized access) are often rejected.
10. **Inconsistent App Size:**  Your app's size must match what's declared during the submission process.  Significant discrepancies can cause issues.
