**I. Overall Philosophy: Prioritize Value & Engagement**

Our overarching goal isn't just to maximize revenue, but to maximize *customer value* and build long-term relationships.  A paywall shouldn’t feel punitive; it should be presented strategically when the customer has demonstrated sufficient value and is ready to invest further.

**II. Paywall Strategy - When & How**

* **Trigger Points for Paywall Display:**
    * **Session Duration:** After 30-60 minutes of active engagement (defined as significant interaction with key features).
    * **Content Consumption:**  User accesses 3 or more premium pieces of content.
    * **Feature Utilization:** User heavily utilizes 2 or more of the core premium features.
    * **User Persona:** Specific user segments identified as high-value (based on behavior – see Lifetime Value below) are exposed to the paywall earlier.
* **Paywall Types:**
    * **Gradual Ramp-Up:** Initially, offer a limited number of "unlocks" per day/week within the free tier. This encourages continued engagement.
    * **Contextual Paywall:** Display paywall prompts directly within the content they’re consuming, highlighting the value they're missing. (e.g., "Unlock this full analysis" - with a clear pricing callout).
    * **“Behind the Scenes” Paywall:** Offer a short, exclusive piece of content (“Behind the Scenes of X”) as a paywall incentive – a lower-barrier trial.
* **Free Trial Length:**  **7 Days** – This is a generally proven sweet spot.
    * **Phase 1 (Days 1-3):**  Full access to all features. Emphasis is on exploration and engagement.
    * **Phase 2 (Days 4-7):**  Limited access to key features, gradually introducing the value proposition of the premium tier.


**III. Pricing Experiments - A/B Testing**

* **Tier Structure:** Start with a 3-Tier model:
    * **Basic (Free):**  Limited access, basic features, advertising.
    * **Standard:** Core features, increased content access, no ads.
    * **Premium:** All features, priority support, exclusive content.
* **Pricing Points:** Conduct A/B tests with different price points for each tier.  Consider:
    * **Value-Based Pricing:** Aligning prices with the perceived value of the product/service.
    * **Psychological Pricing:** Utilizing $9.99 instead of $10.
    * **Freemium Model Adjustments:** Experiment with feature limitations to drive upgrades.
* **Metrics to Track:** Conversion rates, average revenue per user (ARPU), customer churn, feature usage.
* **Example Experiment:** Test $19.99 vs. $29.99 for the Standard tier – analyze which generates more revenue and higher retention.

**IV. Feature Gating Strategy – Increasing Perceived Value**

* **Tiered Access:** As outlined in pricing, features are gated by tier.
* **“Beta” Features:** Introduce new features to a subset of users (potentially the Premium tier) to gather feedback and build excitement.
* **Progressive Disclosure:**  Don't overwhelm users with *all* features at once. Reveal advanced features based on their usage and demonstrated understanding.  (e.g., more advanced analytics available only to Premium users.)
* **Gamification:** Consider adding elements like badges, progress tracking, and leaderboards to encourage feature exploration and engagement – especially for the free tier.
