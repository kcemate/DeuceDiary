## Retention Analysis Plan - With D1, D7, and D30 Benchmarks

This plan outlines a strategy for tracking and analyzing user retention across a product or service, utilizing key milestones of D1 (Day 1), D7 (Day 7), and D30 (Day 30) to understand user behavior and identify areas for improvement.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Understand User Drop-off:** Identify where users are leaving the product/service at each stage.
* **Identify Key Drivers of Retention:** Determine the factors contributing to users sticking around or leaving.
* **Optimize Onboarding & Engagement:** Use data to improve the initial user experience and guide users towards continued value.
* **Segment Users:**  Identify specific user groups with varying retention rates to tailor strategies.


**II. Data Sources:**

* **Product Analytics Platform:** (e.g., Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics) - Primary source for tracking user behavior.
* **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):**  Allows linking activity to specific users and identifying customer lifecycle stages.
* **Marketing Automation Platform:**  Provides data on user acquisition channels and their impact on initial engagement.
* **User Surveys & Feedback:**  Qualitative data to understand *why* users are leaving or engaging.


**III. Key Metrics & Definitions:**

* **Retention Rate:** Percentage of users who return to use the product/service after a specific period. Calculated as:  (Number of Users at Time Period X / Number of Users at Start of Time Period X) * 100
* **Day 1 Retention:** Percentage of users who return the day after signup.
* **Day 7 Retention:** Percentage of users who return 7 days after signup.
* **Day 30 Retention:** Percentage of users who return 30 days after signup.
* **Churn Rate:**  Percentage of users who stop using the product/service over a specific period. (100 - Retention Rate)
* **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Number of unique users who use the product/service each day.
* **WAU (Weekly Active Users):** Number of unique users who use the product/service each week.
* **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** Number of unique users who use the product/service each month.
