**I. Overall Philosophy: Value-Based Pricing & Personalized Experiences**

The core principle is to understand the *value* a user derives from your product and then tailor the experience to maximize that value while gently guiding them toward paid options. We'll prioritize a personalized experience based on user behavior and data.

**II. Paywall Strategy - When to Show & How**

* **Trigger-Based Paywall (Progressive Disclosure):** Avoid overwhelming users with a paywall immediately. Instead, use triggers based on engagement:
    * **Phase 1: Free (Full Access - Initial Exploration):**
        * **Duration:** 7-14 days - Long enough to allow users to deeply explore the core value proposition.
        * **Rationale:** This allows users to truly experience the product's capabilities and build a genuine need.
    * **Phase 2: Limited Access (Gentle Introduction):**
        * **Trigger:**  After 7-14 days, if a user has engaged with 3-5 key features or spent more than 30 minutes in the product, introduce limited access. This could be:
            * **Feature Gated Content:**  Restrict access to premium content, advanced analytics, or collaboration features. (This is the *most* effective early paywall)
            * **Usage Limits:** Cap on daily/weekly usage of certain features.
        * **Duration:** 3-7 days -  This forces the user to consider the benefits of full access.
    * **Phase 3: Full Paywall (After Significant Value Demonstrated):**
        * **Trigger:**  After the limited access period, and if a user continues to demonstrate high engagement (e.g., daily use, feature exploration, sharing), offer full paywall access.
        * **Rationale:** By this point, the user understands the full potential and is more likely to convert.

* **Paywall Type:**  Start with a tiered model based on usage and features.  Consider:
    * **Freemium:** Basic access + limited features (good for broad adoption).
    * **Tiered Subscription:**  Different levels of access and features based on the user's needs.

**III. Free Trial Length - Beyond the Standard**

* **Dynamic Free Trials (Recommended):** The 7-14 day standard is a starting point.  Leverage user behavior to dynamically adjust.
    * **High Engagement:** Extend the trial by 7 days.
    * **Low Engagement:** Shorten the trial by 3 days.  This minimizes wasted time and investment on users who aren’t a fit.
* **Focus on ‘Aha!’ Moment:**  Design the trial to quickly lead users to their "aha!" moment – the point where they realize the value of the product.

**IV. Pricing Experiments – A/B Testing is Key**

* **Start Simple:** Begin with 2-3 core pricing tiers.
* **Experiment with:**
    * **Feature Bundles:**  Vary the features included in each tier.
    * **Subscription Lengths:**  Monthly, quarterly, annual – each has different customer lifetime value considerations.
    * **Discount Offers:**  Offer a percentage discount for annual subscriptions.
    * **Freemium Tier Changes:** Test changes to the limits of the free tier.
* **Tools:** Utilize A/B testing tools (e.g., Optimizely, VWO) to track conversion rates, customer acquisition cost (CAC), and average revenue per user (ARPU) for each variation.
