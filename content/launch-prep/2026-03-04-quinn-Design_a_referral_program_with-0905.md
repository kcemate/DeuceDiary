Okay, let's design a referral program with tiered incentives. This design aims to be engaging, rewarding, and effective in driving growth.

**Program Name:** "Share the [Your Brand Name] Love" (Replace "[Your Brand Name]" with your actual brand name)

**Overall Goal:** Increase brand awareness and user acquisition through word-of-mouth marketing.

**I. Core Mechanics**

* **Referrer:** The existing customer who refers new users.
* **Referee:** The new user who is referred.
* **Referral Link:** Each referrer receives a unique link they can share.  This link tracks the referral.
* **Tracking:** Implement robust tracking (Google Analytics, referral program software, or custom tracking) to monitor referrals, conversions, and engagement.

**II. Tiered Incentive Structure**

This is crucial for motivation.  We’ll use a three-tier system:

**Tier 1: Basic Referral (Easy Entry)**

* **Referrer Reward:** 10% Discount on their next purchase.  (Value:  $5-$10 depending on average order value)
* **Referee Reward:** 10% Discount on their first purchase. (Value: $5-$10)
* **Trigger:**  Referee makes a purchase of $25 or more.
* **Ease of Use:** This is the simplest tier, encouraging everyone to participate.

**Tier 2:  Silver Referral (For Active Users)**

* **Referrer Reward:** $25 Store Credit
* **Referee Reward:** 15% Discount on their first purchase.
* **Trigger:** Referee makes a purchase of $50 or more *and* the referral is active (has made a purchase).
* **Emphasis:** This tier rewards users who are engaging with your product/service and seeing value.


**Tier 3: Gold Referral (Top Performers)**

* **Referrer Reward:** $50 Store Credit *or* A Premium Product/Service (e.g., extended warranty, extra features, VIP access - tailor to your business).
* **Referee Reward:** 20% Discount on their first purchase *and* a small welcome gift (e.g., branded sticker, small sample).
* **Trigger:** Referee makes a purchase of $100 or more *and* the referral is active (has made a purchase).
* **Focus:** This tier incentivizes your most loyal customers to actively promote your brand.

**III.  Incentive Values (Example - Adjust Based on Your Business)**

* **Average Order Value (AOV):**  Crucially important to base your incentives on.  Let's assume an AOV of $50 for this example.
* **Discount Percentages:**  Can vary, but 10-20% is generally effective.
* **Store Credit:**  A good alternative if discounts aren't appealing.
* **Premium Items:**  Use strategically for high-value users.

**IV.  Program Communication & Promotion**

* **Dedicated Landing Page:** Create a landing page explaining the referral program clearly.  Include screenshots, examples, and FAQs.
* **Email Marketing:**
    * **Welcome Email:** Immediately introduce the program to new users.
    * **Referral Email:** Send a personalized email to existing users reminding them to refer.
    * **Reward Claim Email:**  Notify users when they’ve earned a reward and how to redeem it.
