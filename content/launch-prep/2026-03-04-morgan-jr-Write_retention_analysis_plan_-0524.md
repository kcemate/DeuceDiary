**Retention Analysis Plan**

**1. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:** Understand how many users continue to engage with our product after specific periods (D1, D7, D30) to identify areas for improvement and optimize for long-term retention.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key drop-off points in the user journey.
    * Determine what factors contribute to higher or lower retention rates.
    * Track the impact of specific onboarding changes or feature releases on retention.
    * Establish baseline retention rates to measure future improvements.

**2. User Definition:**

* **Clearly Define “User”:** Be precise. Is it a registered user?  Someone who completed a key action? (e.g., uploaded a file, made a purchase, connected a third-party app).  This depends on your product.
* **Segment Users:**  Segment your users based on key characteristics. This will allow you to see if retention differs across groups.  Examples:
    * **Acquisition Channel:** (e.g., Facebook Ads, Organic Search, Referral)
    * **User Persona:** (e.g., Newbie, Power User, Business User)
    * **Device Type:** (iOS, Android, Web)
    * **Engagement Level:** (e.g., Daily Active Users, Weekly Active Users)

**3. Data Tracking & Metrics:**

* **Key Metric: Retention Rate:**  The percentage of users who are still active after a specific period.  Formula:  ((Users at Day 30 - New Users at Day 30) / Users at Day 1) * 100
* **Day 1 Retention (D1):**  Percentage of users who return to use the product within 24 hours of signing up. – *Focus: Initial Onboarding & First-Time Value*
* **Day 7 Retention (D7):**  Percentage of users who return to use the product within 7 days of signing up. – *Focus: Core Value Proposition & Initial Engagement*
* **Day 30 Retention (D30):**  Percentage of users who return to use the product within 30 days of signing up. – *Focus: Long-Term Value & Habit Formation*
* **Secondary Metrics (To Analyze Alongside Retention):**
    * **Churn Rate:** The percentage of users who *stop* using the product during a specific period. (Inverse of Retention)
    * **Daily Active Users (DAU):** The number of unique users who engage with the product each day.
    * **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** The number of unique users who engage with the product each week.
    * **Session Length:**  Average time users spend in the product per session.
    * **Feature Usage:** Which features are being used, and how frequently?
    * **Time Since Last Activity:** How long has it been since the user last interacted with the product?
