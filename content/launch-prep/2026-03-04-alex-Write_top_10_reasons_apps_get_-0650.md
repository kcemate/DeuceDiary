**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024)**

1. **Violating Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines (HIG):** This is the *biggest* culprit.  It’s a massive, constantly evolving document. Issues can range from poor UI design, confusing navigation, inconsistent design, and visual clutter.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive App Stores:**  Apple wants to ensure the app delivers on its promises. Apps that overpromise and underdeliver, or use misleading descriptions/screenshots, are rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  This is now *extremely* scrutinized.  Apps must be transparent about data collection, use it responsibly, and obtain user consent appropriately. Failure to comply with iOS privacy guidelines is a rapid route to rejection.
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Copying artwork, trademarks, music, or other copyrighted material without permission is a serious offense.
5. **Malware or Security Vulnerabilities:** Apps must be secure and not contain malware or vulnerabilities that could harm users or their devices.  Apple performs thorough security reviews.
6. **Lack of Clear Functionality:**  If the app’s core functionality is unclear or ambiguous, it can lead to rejection due to usability concerns. Users shouldn’t have to struggle to understand what the app does.
7. **App Not Working as Promised:**  This is closely related to #2, but it specifically refers to apps that simply don’t function correctly. Bugs, crashes, and performance issues are a major rejection factor.
8. **Payment Issues:**  Issues with in-app purchases, subscription models, or billing processes can lead to rejection.
9. **App Store Guidelines Violations (Specific to Categories):** Certain categories (e.g., Health, Finance) have *much* stricter rules than others.  Even minor deviations can trigger a rejection.
10. **Duplicate Apps:** Submitting an app that is substantially similar to an already existing app in the App Store.
