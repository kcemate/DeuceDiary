**I. Executive Summary**

* **Goal:** To establish a robust system for continuously monitoring and analyzing competitor activity, identifying emerging trends, and informing our strategic decision-making to maintain a competitive advantage.
* **Frequency:** This plan will be reviewed and updated quarterly, with ongoing monitoring occurring monthly.
* **Ownership:** [Assign a specific team or individual – likely Marketing, Product, or Strategy – to oversee the plan’s execution.]

**II. Key Areas of Competitive Intelligence**

We’ll focus on tracking across these categories:

1. **Product/Service Offering:**
   * **Metrics:**
      * **New Product Launches:** Number and timing of new product/feature releases.
      * **Feature Updates:** Frequency and type of feature updates (e.g., adding functionality, improving UX).
      * **Product Roadmap Visibility:**  Where possible, analyze competitor roadmaps (even if partial).
      * **Technology Stack:** Track shifts in technologies they’re utilizing (e.g., adopting a new programming language, moving to a cloud platform).
   * **Data Sources:** Competitor websites, press releases, product blogs, industry reports, app stores, social media.

2. **Pricing & Promotions:**
   * **Metrics:**
      * **Price Changes:** Document all price adjustments – both headline pricing and tiered pricing.
      * **Promotional Offers:** Track discounts, bundles, rebates, free trials, and referral programs.
      * **Payment Models:** Changes in payment structures (e.g., subscription tiers, usage-based pricing).
   * **Data Sources:** Competitor websites, email marketing, social media, advertising, online marketplaces, price comparison sites.

3. **Marketing & Sales Strategies:**
   * **Metrics:**
      * **Advertising Campaigns:**  Identify and analyze their advertising channels (Google Ads, social media, display ads), messaging, and creative.
      * **Content Marketing:** Track the types of content they’re creating (blog posts, eBooks, webinars, infographics) and their distribution channels.
      * **Social Media Activity:**  Monitor their social media presence – content, engagement rates, audience growth, brand sentiment.
      * **Sales Tactics:** (More difficult to track directly, focus on marketing that drives sales) -  Sales promotions, lead magnets, etc.
   * **Data Sources:** Competitor websites, social media, advertising platforms (e.g., SEMrush, SpyFu for ad tracking), content marketing analytics, social listening tools.

4. **Customer Experience & Support:**
   * **Metrics:**
      * **Customer Reviews:** Track reviews on sites like Trustpilot, G2, Capterra, and industry-specific review sites.
      * **Customer Support Channels:**  Identify the channels they’re using for support (e.g., phone, email, chat, self-service knowledge base).
      * **Response Times & Resolution Rates:**  (Difficult to get exact data, often requires using their services and tracking your own experience).
   * **Data Sources:** Review sites, social media (sentiment), their website, and potentially by using their service yourself.
