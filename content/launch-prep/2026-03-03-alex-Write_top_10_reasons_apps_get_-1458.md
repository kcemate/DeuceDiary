**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (2024)**

These reasons are based on Apple's App Review Guidelines and common developer experiences. Keep in mind, Apple's criteria can shift slightly, but these are consistently cited issues.

1. **Violation of the App Store Review Guidelines:** This is the umbrella reason for many rejections. It covers almost everything else on this list. Apple's guidelines are incredibly detailed and complex.
2. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that falsely advertise their functionality, collect data without consent, or trick users into spending money are immediately rejected.
3. **Privacy Violations:** This is *hugely* important now. Apps must clearly explain data collection practices, obtain user consent, and comply with regulations (like GDPR and CCPA).
4. **Intellectual Property Infringement:** Using copyrighted material (music, images, trademarks, etc.) without permission is a major red flag.
5. **Functionality Issues (Bugs & Crashes):** Apps must be stable and functional. Excessive crashes, bugs, or poor performance are grounds for rejection.
6. **App Store Metadata Issues:**  Inaccurate descriptions, misleading screenshots, or incorrect category selections.
7. **Payment Issues:** Problems with in-app purchases, requiring developer approval for every purchase, or failing to comply with Apple’s payment processing standards.
8. **Use of Third-Party SDKs:** Third-party SDKs (Software Development Kits) must be approved by Apple and adhere to their guidelines. Many are blocked entirely.
9. **Adult Content (Even If Not Explicit):**  Anything that could be perceived as inappropriate for a general audience—even subtle references—can be rejected.
10. **Performance Issues (Slow Load Times, Battery Drain):** Apple is increasingly prioritizing user experience. Apps that drain battery excessively or have slow load times will be rejected.
