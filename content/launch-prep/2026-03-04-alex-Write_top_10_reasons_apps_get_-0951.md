**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024)**

1. **Violating Apple's Human Interface Guidelines (HIG):** This is the *biggest* reason.  It covers a huge range of issues including inconsistent UI, poor navigation, confusing design, and visual clutter.
2. **Misleading Representations:** This includes deceptive screenshots, inaccurate descriptions, or implying capabilities the app doesn’t actually have. “Fake” premium features are a common example.
3. **Privacy Violations:**  Collecting excessive user data, failing to obtain proper consent for data collection, or not complying with privacy regulations (like GDPR and CCPA) gets you rejected.
4. **Intellectual Property Issues:** Copyright infringement (using copyrighted music, artwork, code, or character designs), trademark violations, or confusingly similar apps to existing ones.
5. **Malware/Security Issues:** This encompasses viruses, spyware, data theft, vulnerabilities, and any code that compromises user security.
6. **Functionality Problems:**  Bugs, crashes, instability, or simply not performing as advertised.  Apple rigorously tests apps.
7. **Inappropriate Content:**  Content deemed offensive, violent, sexually suggestive, or harmful to children.
8. **App Store Guidelines Violations (Specific):**  Rules around in-app purchases, advertising, location services, etc., that are not followed.
9. **Lack of Necessary Information:** Missing required App Store Connect information (like screenshots, descriptions, app metadata, privacy policy).
10. **Scams/Fraudulent Activity:** Attempts to manipulate users into making unauthorized purchases, or any activity that could be considered deceptive.
