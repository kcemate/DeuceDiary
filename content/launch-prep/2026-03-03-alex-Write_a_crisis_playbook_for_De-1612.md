**II. Initial Response Steps (Applies to ALL Levels)**

1. **Alert & Mobilize:**  Immediately notify the Crisis Team via [Designated Communication Channel – e.g., Slack Channel #deuce-diary-crisis].
2. **Assess the Situation:**
    * **Verify:** Is the issue real? Is it widespread or isolated?
    * **Gather Facts:** What exactly happened? Who is affected? What is the scale?
    * **Document Everything:**  Time, actions taken, evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)
3. **Containment:** Take immediate steps to stop the spread of misinformation or prevent further harm. (e.g., pause social media posts, flag comments, initiate website maintenance)


**III. Specific Crisis Response Plans by Category**

**A. Social Media Crisis (Most Likely)**

* **Trigger:** Viral negative comments, misinformation, inappropriate content.
* **Level 1:** Monitor, acknowledge, and politely correct. Respond with a factual statement and direct users to official channels. (Example: “We appreciate your feedback! We’re addressing this and want to clarify…”).
* **Level 2:**  Increase monitoring, take down offensive content (with platform support), issue a more formal statement addressing concerns. Consider a community management response.
* **Level 3:** Immediate halt to all social media activity. Engage a PR firm. Coordinate a comprehensive response across all platforms.

**B. Website/Technical Crisis**

* **Trigger:** Website downtime, error messages, broken features, security vulnerabilities.
* **Level 1:**  Initiate standard website monitoring and troubleshooting.  Update status page with accurate information.
* **Level 2:**  Escalate to technical support team and hosting provider. Communicate regularly to users about progress.
* **Level 3:**  Immediate website lockdown.  Activate incident response plan.  Notify users via email and social media of the outage.

**C. Customer Service Crisis**

* **Trigger:** Overwhelming volume of complaints, negative reviews, unmet expectations.
* **Level 1:**  Increase staffing, prioritize urgent tickets, offer empathetic responses.
* **Level 2:**  Identify common issues, escalate to product development and marketing.  Communicate proactively to affected customers.
* **Level 3:**  Implement emergency support channels (e.g., phone hotline).
