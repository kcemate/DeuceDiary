**Our Commitment to Accessibility**

Deuce Diary is committed to providing a welcoming and accessible experience for all users, including those with disabilities. **What We’ve Done**

We have taken several steps to make Deuce Diary more accessible, including:

* **Semantic HTML:** We use semantic HTML elements (like `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<article>`, `<aside>`, `<footer>`) to structure our content logically, which helps screen reader users understand the page layout and content.
* **Alt Text for Images:**  All images on our site have descriptive alternative text (alt text) that conveys the image's content and purpose.  We're actively working to ensure *all* images have appropriate alt text.
* **Keyboard Navigation:** The entire website is navigable using a keyboard alone, without requiring a mouse.  We’ve tested and refined our keyboard focus order.
* **Color Contrast:** We’ve paid attention to color contrast ratios to ensure sufficient readability for users with low vision. However, we acknowledge that color is only one aspect of readability.
* **Resizable Text:** The website is designed to allow users to resize text to a comfortable level without loss of content or functionality.  The text scales well on various screen sizes.
* **Video Captions & Transcripts:** All video content is provided with accurate captions and transcripts to make it accessible to users who are deaf or hard of hearing. We are continually improving the quality and accuracy of these captions.
* **Form Accessibility:** Form elements have clear labels and instructions, and error messages are presented in a way that is easy to understand and correct.
* **Regular Audits:** We conduct regular accessibility audits of our website to identify and address any new issues.
