## Competitive Metrics Tracking Plan

This plan outlines a framework for consistently monitoring and analyzing your competition to inform strategic decisions and maintain a competitive edge. It focuses on a data-driven approach and prioritizes actionable insights.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Overall Goal:**  Understand the competitive landscape to identify opportunities, threats, and areas for improvement within our own business.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Identify key competitors and their strategies.
    * Track competitor pricing, promotions, and product offerings.
    * Analyze competitor strengths and weaknesses.
    * Understand competitor marketing and sales tactics.
    * Identify emerging trends and potential disruptions.
    *  Inform strategic decision-making regarding product development, pricing, marketing, and sales.


**II. Competitor Identification & Prioritization:**

* **List of Competitors:** Create a comprehensive list, categorizing them based on relevance (direct, indirect, potential).
    * **Direct Competitors:** Offer similar products/services to the same target market. (Highest Priority)
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Offer alternative solutions to the same customer need. (Medium Priority)
    * **Potential Competitors:** New entrants or companies expanding into your market. (Low Priority - Monitor Regularly)
* **Prioritization Matrix:** Rank competitors based on:
    * **Market Share:** % of the market they control.
    * **Growth Rate:**  Their recent growth trajectory.
    * **Revenue:**  Relative size of their business.
    * **Strategic Significance:**  Their potential impact on your market.
* **Regular Review:**  Revisit and update the competitor list and prioritization matrix at least quarterly, or more frequently if significant changes occur.


**III. Key Metrics to Track:**

This section is broken down by category.  Choose the metrics most relevant to your business and industry.

**A. Product/Service Metrics:**

* **Product Features & Functionality:** Track new features, updates, and product roadmap announcements. (Source: Press releases, Product updates, Social Media)
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** Monitor reviews on major platforms (Amazon, Trustpilot, Google Reviews, Capterra). (Source: Review Platforms)
* **Innovation Pipeline:**  Assess competitor patents, research & development activity, and new product announcements. (Source: Patent Databases, Industry Reports, Trade Shows)


**B. Pricing & Promotion Metrics:**

* **Pricing Changes:** Record all changes to pricing structures, discounts, and promotions. (Source: Competitor Websites, Email Campaigns, Social Media)
* **Promotional Offers:** Track the frequency, type, and value of promotions (e.g., BOGO, discounts, bundles). (Source: Competitor Websites, Advertising, Social Media)
* **Competitive Pricing Benchmarking:** Regularly compare your pricing to the competition’s –  adjust accordingly. (Source: Competitor Websites)


**C. Marketing & Sales Metrics:**

* **Website Traffic & Engagement:** Track competitor website traffic, bounce rates, time on site, and conversion rates. (Source:  SimilarWeb, SEMrush, Google Analytics - with limitations)
* **Social Media Activity:** Monitor competitor social media channels - likes, shares, comments, follower growth, content strategy, engagement rates. (Source: Social Media Analytics Tools - Hootsuite, Sprout Social)
* **Advertising Campaigns:** Track competitor advertising spend, channels (Google Ads, Social Media Ads, Display Ads), and messaging. (Source: SEMrush, SpyFu, Facebook Ad Library)
* **Lead Generation Tactics:** Analyze competitor lead generation strategies – content marketing, webinars, events, sales promotions. (Source:  Website, Social Media, Industry Events)
