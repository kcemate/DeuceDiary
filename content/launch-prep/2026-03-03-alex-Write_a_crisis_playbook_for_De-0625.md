## Deuce Diary Launch: Crisis Playbook

**Purpose:** This playbook outlines the procedures and responsibilities for responding to potential crises during the launch of “Deuce Diary,” ensuring a swift, effective, and brand-appropriate response.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency:** Be open and honest with the public. Avoid spin or obfuscation.
* **Empathy:** Acknowledge and validate the concerns of readers, critics, and the community.
* **Speed:** Respond quickly and decisively. Time is of the essence.
* **Accuracy:** Ensure all information released is factually correct and verified.
* **Consistency:** Maintain a unified message across all communication channels.


**II. Team & Responsibilities:**

* **Crisis Lead (CL):** [Name - e.g., Marketing Director] – Overall responsibility for coordinating the response. Final approval for all statements.
* **Social Media Lead (SML):** [Name - e.g., Social Media Manager] – Monitors social media channels, drafts and publishes quick responses, engages with the community.
* **PR Lead (PRL):** [Name - e.g., PR Agency Contact/Internal PR] – Manages media inquiries, drafts press releases, and coordinates external communications.
* **Content Lead (CLT):** [Name - e.g., Head Writer] – Provides factual information, clarifies details, and ensures content accuracy.
* **Legal Counsel (LC):** [Name – Internal or External Legal Team] – Provides legal advice and approves all legal statements.


**III. Potential Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Technical Issues (Website Downtime, Payment Problems)**

* **Severity:** High – Immediate impact on user experience and potential revenue.
* **Response Time:** Within 15 minutes
* **Actions:**
    * SML:  Announce downtime on all social media channels, acknowledging the issue and providing a timeline for resolution.  "We’re experiencing technical difficulties and working to get Deuce Diary back online ASAP. We appreciate your patience!"
    * CLT:  Investigate the root cause with the development team.
    * Development Team: Prioritize fixing the issue.
    * SML: Provide regular updates on progress (every 30-60 minutes) as available.
    * PRL:  Prepare a brief statement for media outlets if the issue escalates.

**Scenario 2: Negative Criticism/Backlash (Content deemed inappropriate, controversial)**

* **Severity:** Medium - Can damage brand reputation and lead to negative sentiment.
* **Response Time:** Within 30-60 minutes
* **Actions:**
    * SML: Monitor sentiment on social media. Acknowledge negative comments respectfully. "We're listening to your feedback and taking your concerns seriously." (Do NOT engage in arguments).
    * CLT & CLT:  Investigate the specific criticism. Determine if there’s a misunderstanding or misinterpretation.
    * CLT: Draft a statement addressing the criticism, offering context or clarification if appropriate. (Focus on intent, not necessarily defending the content.)
    * PRL: Prepare a formal statement for wider distribution if the backlash is significant.
    * LC: Review the statement for legal implications.

**Scenario 3: Misinformation/Fake News (Incorrect details about the book or author)**

* **Severity:** High - Can severely damage credibility and lead to significant reputational harm.
* **Response Time:** Within 30 minutes
* **Actions:**
    * SML: Flag and quickly debunk misinformation on all social media platforms. "This information is inaccurate.  Please refer to [Official Website/Author Bio] for verified details."
