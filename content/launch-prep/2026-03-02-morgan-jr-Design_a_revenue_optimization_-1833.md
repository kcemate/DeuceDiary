**I. Overall Philosophy: Data-Driven & User-Centric**

* **Focus:** Maximize revenue while maintaining a positive user experience and building long-term value.
* **Key Metrics:**

**II. Paywall Strategy – When to Show & How**

* **Phase 1: Initial Awareness & Value Demonstration (0-7 Days)**
    * **No Paywall Initially:**  Users are free to explore the core functionality and experience the value. This builds trust and encourages engagement.
    * **Targeted Messaging:**  Introduce the value proposition subtly through in-app messages, onboarding flows, and email campaigns.
* **Phase 2: Progressive Disclosure (Day 8-14)**
    * **Contextual Paywall Prompts:** Introduce the paywall *after* users have shown clear engagement. This could be based on:
        * **Time Spent in Key Features:** If they're deeply using a powerful feature, a gentle reminder of what they're missing could be effective.
        * **Feature Usage Threshold:**  After a certain number of uses of a feature, prompt with a “Unlock All Features” offer.
        * **Content Consumption:** Users who are consistently consuming premium content are more likely to convert.
    * **Framing:**  Don’t frame it as “blocked.”  Instead, use phrases like “Unlock Premium Features for Full Access” or “Get Even More Value.”
* **Phase 3: Strategic Calls-to-Action (Day 15+)**
    * **Time-Limited Offers:**  "Try Premium for 7 Days and See the Difference" – Create urgency.
    * **Feature-Specific Paywalls:** "Unlock Advanced Reporting - Start Your Free Trial."
    * **Personalized Messaging:** Leverage user behavior to tailor the paywall prompt (e.g., “We noticed you’re a data analyst – access premium reporting”).



**III. Free Trial Length - Strategic Testing**

* **Short Trial (3-7 Days):** Higher conversion rate, but requires immediate value delivery. Best for highly engaged users or features that provide quick wins.  Good for SaaS products.
* **Medium Trial (14-21 Days):**  Allows users to deeply experience the product.  Requires more compelling onboarding and ongoing value delivery. A common starting point.
* **Long Trial (30 Days):**  Lower conversion rate initially, but potentially higher CLTV if users find genuine value.  Ideal for products with a longer onboarding curve or those requiring significant time to see results.
* **Experimentation:** *Crucially*, A/B test different trial lengths to see what performs best for your specific audience.


**IV. Pricing Experiments - Dynamic & Iterative**

* **Tiered Pricing:**  Offer multiple tiers based on features and usage.  Start with 3-4 tiers (Basic, Pro, Premium).
* **Feature-Based Pricing:** Price based on the features users access.  This is often more flexible and transparent.
* **Usage-Based Pricing:** (Pay-as-you-go) – Suitable
