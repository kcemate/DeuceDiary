**2. Defining Retention & Key Metrics:**

* **Retention Rate:** The percentage of users who remain active after a specific period (e.g., D1, D7, D30).  Calculated as:  ((Users at Time Period – New Users During Time Period) / Users at Beginning of Time Period) * 100
* **DAU/MAU (Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users):** A core indicator of engagement.  A declining DAU/MAU ratio signals potential retention issues.
* **Session Length:** The average time users spend in your product during a session.  Shorter sessions can indicate disengagement.
* **Feature Usage:** Which features are users actively using?  Lack of usage of core features is a strong churn predictor.
* **Time to Value (TTV):**  How long does it take a new user to experience the core value proposition of your product?  Long TTV = Higher churn.
* **Cohort Analysis:** Group users based on their acquisition date (e.g., “January Users”) to track retention patterns over time and identify trends.
* **Churn Rate:** The inverse of retention rate (the percentage of users who stop using the product over a specific period).
