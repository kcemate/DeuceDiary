**Overall Goal:**  To quickly assess the success of the launch, identify areas for optimization, and track progress against key goals.

**Sections & Metrics:**

We’ll divide the dashboard into logical sections for clarity.

**1. Acquisition - Where are our Users Coming From?**

* **Metric:** **Total Downloads/Sign-Ups:** (Daily, Weekly, Monthly) - The overall number of users acquiring the product.  This is the top-level metric.
* **Metric:** **Source Breakdown:** (Pie Chart or Bar Chart) -  Breakdown of where users are coming from:
    * App Store (iOS)
    * Google Play Store (Android)
    * Website Sign-Ups
    * Paid Ads (Facebook, Google, etc.) -  Crucial for understanding ROI.
    * Influencer Marketing
    * Organic Search (Google, etc.)
* **Metric:** **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):** (Weekly, Monthly) – Calculated for paid channels. This shows how much it costs to acquire a new user.
* **Visualization:**  Map showing geographical distribution of users (if relevant and available).


**2. Initial Engagement - Are Users Trying It Out?**

* **Metric:** **Daily Active Users (DAU):**  Tracks users active each day. (Trend Line Chart) –  A key indicator of initial interest.
* **Metric:** **Weekly Active Users (WAU):**  Tracks users active each week. (Trend Line Chart) -  Provides a longer-term view.
* **Metric:** **Monthly Active Users (MAU):** (Trend Line Chart) – Gives a overall picture of user base size.
* **Metric:** **Session Duration:** (Average, Distribution) - How long users are spending in the app/website per session. (Histogram or Box Plot) -  Indicates engagement depth.
* **Metric:** **Screens Per Session:** (Average, Distribution) - How many screens users visit per session. (Histogram or Box Plot) – Indicates exploration.
* **Metric:** **Feature Usage:** (Bar Chart or Heatmap) -  Which features are being used most and least? (e.g., Journal Entry Creation, Habit Tracking, Mood Tracking, Calendar Integration, etc.)  This is *critical* for prioritization.
* **Visualization:** User flow chart showing common paths users take through the app/website.


**3. Retention - Are Users Coming Back?**

* **Metric:** **Day 1 Retention:** (Percentage) –  Percentage of users who return to the app/website one day after signing up.
* **Metric:** **Day 7 Retention:** (Percentage) – Percentage of users who return to the app/website seven days after signing up.  This is a vital metric for success.
* **Metric:** **Day 30 Retention:** (Percentage) – Percentage of users who return to the app/website 30 days after signing up.
* **Metric:** **Churn Rate:** (Percentage - Monthly) –  Percentage of users who stop using the app/website in a given month. (Important for understanding if retention is a problem)
* **Visualization:** Cohort analysis – Visualizing retention across different signup cohorts (e.g., users who signed up in June vs. July) will reveal patterns.
