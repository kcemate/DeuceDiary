**I. Executive Summary**

* **Goal:** To establish a robust system for continuously monitoring key competitive metrics, identify trends, and inform strategic decisions to improve market position and performance.
* **Frequency:**  Data will be reviewed and analyzed **weekly** for high-level trends and **monthly** for deeper analysis and reporting.
* **Ownership:**  [Assign a specific role/team - e.g., Marketing Analytics Team, Competitive Intelligence Team] will be responsible for data collection, analysis, and reporting.

**II.  Defining the Competition**

* **Identify Competitors:**  Create a clear list of your key direct and indirect competitors.  Categorize them based on market share, product/service offering, target audience, and geographic reach.
    * **Direct Competitors:** Businesses offering similar products or services to the same target audience. (e.g., Coca-Cola vs. Pepsi)
    * **Indirect Competitors:** Businesses offering alternative solutions to the same customer need. (e.g., McDonald’s vs. Subway)
* **Tiered Approach:** Consider a tiered system:
    * **Tier 1 (Primary):**  The 3-5 most significant competitors that directly challenge you. Focus the most effort here.
    * **Tier 2 (Secondary):** Competitors you monitor regularly for trends or disruptive activity.
    * **Tier 3 (Monitor):**  Competitors to be tracked occasionally, primarily for broader industry trends.

**III. Key Metric Categories & Specific Metrics**

This is the most crucial section.  Tailor these metrics to your specific industry and business model.  Here's a breakdown by category:

**A. Pricing & Promotions**

* **Pricing Data:**
    * **Competitor Pricing:** Track prices of comparable products/services on their websites, in stores, and through distributors. (Automated scraping tools can be helpful here)
    * **Promotional Offers:**  Frequency, types (e.g., discounts, BOGO, coupons), and conditions of competitor promotions.
* **Sentiment around Pricing:** Monitor online reviews and social media for mentions of competitor pricing – is it perceived as high, low, fair?

**B. Product/Service Offering**

* **New Product Launches:** Track competitor product releases – features, pricing, marketing.
* **Product Updates:** Monitor changes to existing products – improvements, bug fixes, added features.
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** Analyze competitor product reviews on sites like Amazon, Trustpilot, and Capterra. (Look for trends in positive/negative feedback)
* **Innovation Metrics:** (Specific to your industry) - e.g., Patents filed, R&D spending, number of new features released.

**C. Marketing & Sales**

* **Website Traffic:**  Monitor competitor website traffic using tools like SimilarWeb, SEMrush, or Ahrefs. (Compare overall traffic, traffic sources, bounce rates, and time on site.)
* **Social Media Engagement:** Track follower counts, likes, shares, comments, and mentions across relevant platforms.
* **Advertising Spend:** (Difficult to get exact figures, but can infer from ads seen) - Estimate or track competitor advertising spend across platforms (Google Ads, Social Media Ads).
* **Content Marketing:** Analyze competitor blogs, whitepapers, ebooks, and videos – topics, quality, and reach.
* **Lead Generation Tactics:**  Observe competitor lead magnets, webinars, and sales processes.
