**About Deuce Diary**

“Deuce Diary” is a graphic novel series by Liam Thorne, offering a brutally honest and visually arresting exploration of adolescence, isolation, and the unsettling edges of reality.  The series, beginning with *Deuce Diary: Volume 1*, follows the experiences of Deuce Miller, a 17-year-old living in the perpetually overcast town of Blackwood, Oregon.  Deuce documents his life in a worn, spiral-bound notebook – a diary filled with sketches, observations, and increasingly fragmented narratives that blur the line between memory, hallucination, and a creeping sense of dread.

**Synopsis (Volume 1 - *Deuce Diary: Volume 1*)**

Blackwood isn’t known for much. It's a place where rain hangs heavy, and people drift apart. Deuce Miller is perfectly content, or so he tells himself, keeping to his routines: graveyard shifts at the local diner, sketching in his notebook, and avoiding the pitying glances of his parents. But lately, things have shifted. He starts seeing things – fleeting shadows, distorted faces in the rain, whispers that aren't quite his own. His drawings become darker, more obsessive, filled with recurring images of a skeletal figure he calls "The Collector." As Deuce’s grip on reality loosens, the lines between his diary and his life become increasingly indistinguishable, forcing him to confront a terrifying truth about himself and the secrets hidden within Blackwood.
