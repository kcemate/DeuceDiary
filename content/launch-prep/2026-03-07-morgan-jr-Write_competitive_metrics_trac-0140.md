Okay, let's craft a competitive metrics tracking plan. This plan will focus on a strategic approach to understanding and reacting to your competition. It's broken down into phases and categories to ensure it's comprehensive and actionable.

**I. Overall Goals & Objectives:**

* **Primary Goal:** To proactively identify competitive threats and opportunities, inform strategic decision-making, and ultimately improve our market share and performance.
* **Specific Objectives:**
    * Monitor key competitor activities (product launches, pricing changes, marketing campaigns, partnerships).
    * Understand competitor strengths and weaknesses.
    * Identify shifts in the market landscape.
    * Develop counter-strategies to mitigate threats and capitalize on opportunities.

**II. Phase 1: Identification & Prioritization (Weeks 1-4)**

* **Competitor Selection:**
    * **Tier 1 (Direct Competitors):**  Companies offering similar products/services to the same target market. (e.g., if you sell SaaS, identify the top 3-5 SaaS companies in your niche)
    * **Tier 2 (Adjacent Competitors):** Companies offering related products/services or targeting a slightly different segment of the market. (e.g., a CRM company might be a Tier 2 competitor to a sales automation tool)
    * **Tier 3 (Potential Disruptors):** Companies with innovative technologies or business models that could pose a future threat. (Keep a watch on startups and emerging technologies)
* **Prioritization:**  Focus on 3-5 Tier 1 competitors initially.  Rank them based on:
    * **Market Share:**  Percentage of the market they control.
    * **Growth Rate:**  How quickly they’re expanding.
    * **Innovation Level:**  How frequently they release new products/features.
    * **Threat Level:** Based on the above factors, estimate the potential impact they could have on your business.
