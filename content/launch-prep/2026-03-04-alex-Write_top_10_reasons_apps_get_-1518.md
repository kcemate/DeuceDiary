**Top 10 Reasons Apps Get Rejected from the App Store (as of Late 2023/Early 2024)**

1. **Violation of Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines (HIG):** This is the *biggest* culprit. Apple's design standards are incredibly strict. Issues include inconsistent UI, confusing navigation, poor typography, and generally not feeling intuitive.
2. **Functionality Issues:** Bugs, crashes, and overall poor performance are major rejection reasons. Apple expects a smooth, reliable experience.
3. **Misleading or Deceptive Practices:** Apps that falsely represent themselves, collect data without consent, or engage in deceptive advertising get rejected immediately.
4. **Privacy Violations:**  Apps must adhere to strict data privacy rules (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).  Lack of clear privacy policies, improper data handling, or tracking user data without consent are red flags.
5. **Intellectual Property Violations:** Copying copyrighted images, music, trademarks, or using protected code without permission leads to swift rejection.
6. **App Store Guidelines Violations (Specific Rules):** These are constantly evolving. Examples include:
    *   **In-App Purchases:** Problems with implementation, misleading descriptions, or bypassing age restrictions.
    *   **Personal Information:** Collecting excessive personal data without justification.
    *   **Content Restrictions:**  Adult content (unless properly categorized) or content that violates Apple’s rules around violence, hate speech, etc.
7. **Lack of Required Information:** Not providing a complete app store listing, including screenshots, app description, keywords, and category.
8. **Malicious Activity:** Apps with security vulnerabilities, malware, or behaviors that compromise user devices are rejected.
9. **Incorrect Metadata:** Inaccurate app title, description, keywords, or category can lead to rejection.
10. **Duplicate Apps:** Submitting the same app with different names or misleading descriptions.
