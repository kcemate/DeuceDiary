Okay, let's craft a competitive metrics tracking plan. This plan will focus on gathering data, analyzing it, and using it to inform your strategy. It’s designed to be adaptable, so you'll need to tailor it to your specific business, industry, and goals.

**I. Executive Summary**

* **Purpose:** To monitor and understand the competitive landscape, identify opportunities and threats, and ultimately, improve our performance relative to competitors.
* **Frequency:**  Regular (Weekly/Monthly – depending on the industry and dynamism), with deeper dives quarterly.
* **Ownership:** This plan will be overseen by [Marketing Lead/Business Intelligence Team/Dedicated Analyst] with input from relevant departments (Sales, Product, Customer Support).

**II. Scope & Objectives**

* **Define Your Key Competitors:**  Be specific. List at least 3-5 direct competitors, and 2-3 indirect competitors.  Categorize them by market share, target audience, and strategic approach.
* **Set Clear Objectives:** What do you want to learn? Examples:
    * Identify competitor pricing strategies.
    * Understand competitor marketing campaigns.
    * Determine competitor product features/strengths.
    * Assess competitor customer satisfaction.
    * Monitor competitor market share changes.


**III. Metric Categories & Specific Metrics**

This is the heart of the plan. We'll categorize metrics for better organization.

**A. Product/Service Metrics:**

* **Feature Tracking:**  (Monthly) - Track new features released by competitors and rate their perceived value. (Scale: Low, Medium, High)
* **Product Reviews & Ratings:** (Weekly/Monthly) - Monitor reviews on major platforms (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon reviews, etc.). Track average rating, sentiment analysis, and frequently mentioned concerns.
* **Product Roadmap Analysis:** (Quarterly) -  Analyze competitor product roadmaps to anticipate future developments.

**B. Marketing & Sales Metrics:**

* **Website Traffic:** (Weekly) - Track website traffic, bounce rate, time on site, and top landing pages compared to competitors. (Tools: Google Analytics, SimilarWeb)
* **Social Media Engagement:** (Weekly) - Monitor competitor social media activity – follower counts, engagement rates (likes, shares, comments), content themes, and ad campaigns. (Tools: Social Listening Tools – Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Brand24)
* **Ad Spend & Campaigns:** (Monthly) – Estimate competitor ad spend (using tools like SEMrush, Ahrefs) and analyze their targeting, messaging, and creative.
* **Content Marketing:** (Monthly) - Analyze competitor blog posts, whitepapers, ebooks, and videos – topic relevance, quality, and distribution.
* **SEO Performance:** (Monthly) -  Compare keyword rankings and organic traffic to competitors.

**C. Customer Metrics:**

* **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** (Quarterly) - Compare your NPS to that of competitors.
* **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT):** (Monthly) – Similar to NPS, track CSAT scores.
* **Customer Churn Rate:** (Monthly) - Compare churn rates with competitors (this requires access to churn data).
* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** (Monthly) - Benchmark CAC against competitors.
* **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV):** (Quarterly) – Understand the long-term value of customers compared to competitor benchmarks.

**D. Pricing & Promotion Metrics:**

* **Pricing Analysis:** (Monthly) -  Track competitor pricing for comparable products/services.
* **Promotions & Discounts:** (Weekly) - Monitor competitor promotional offers – types of discounts, timing, and messaging.
