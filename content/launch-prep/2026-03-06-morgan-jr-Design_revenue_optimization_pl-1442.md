Okay, let's design a revenue optimization plan. This isn't a quick fix; it's a continuous process. This plan is broken down into phases with specific actions, metrics, and responsible parties.

**I. Phase 1: Assessment & Diagnosis (Weeks 1-4)**

* **Goal:** Understand current revenue performance, identify key pain points, and establish a baseline.
* **Actions:**
    * **Data Audit:**
        * **CRM Review:** Analyze sales data, lead sources, customer demographics, purchase history, churn rate, and lifetime value (LTV).
        * **Website Analytics (Google Analytics, etc.):**  Track conversion rates, bounce rates, time on site, popular pages, traffic sources, and user behavior.
        * **Marketing Data:** Review campaign performance (email, social, paid advertising) - ROI, click-through rates, engagement.
        * **Product Data:** Analyze product mix, pricing tiers, best-selling items, and any associated promotions.
        * **Customer Service Data:**  Analyze customer support tickets, common complaints, and resolution times.
    * **Stakeholder Interviews:** Talk to sales, marketing, product, and customer support teams to understand their challenges and opportunities.
    * **Competitive Analysis:** Understand competitor pricing, offerings, and marketing strategies.
* **Metrics:**
    * **Current Revenue:** Total revenue generated.
    * **Revenue Growth Rate:** Percentage change in revenue.
    * **Average Order Value (AOV):** Total revenue / number of orders.
    * **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Total marketing & sales cost / number of new customers.
    * **Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV):** Predicted revenue a customer will generate during their relationship with the company.
    * **Conversion Rates (Website, Lead to Customer):** Percentage of visitors or leads that convert to paying customers.
* **Responsible Party:**  Marketing Manager, Sales Manager, Data Analyst
