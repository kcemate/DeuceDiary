Okay, let's craft a robust revenue optimization plan. This will be a multi-faceted approach, incorporating data-driven decisions across several key areas.

**I. Overall Philosophy:  Layered Value & Personalized Experiences**

Our core philosophy will be to deliver increasing value to users as they engage with our product/service. We'll avoid a “one-size-fits-all” approach and leverage data to tailor the experience based on individual behavior and potential. We’ll focus on driving *conversion* – moving users from free to paid – rather than just maximizing revenue from existing paid users.

**II. Paywall Strategy – When & How**

* **Trigger Points for Paywall Introduction:**
    * **Stage 1: Early Engagement (0-7 Days):** Minimal Paywall. Focus on onboarding and demonstrating value.  Perhaps a subtle prompt: “Unlock even more features with a Premium subscription.”
    * **Stage 2: Active Usage (8-30 Days):** Introduce targeted prompts based on activity:
        * **Heavy Feature Usage (top 20%):**  Higher probability of paywall suggestion - “Upgrade to Premium to access advanced analytics.”
        * **Feature Access Limit Reached (e.g., 10 projects):** “Expand your capabilities – unlock unlimited projects with Premium.”
        * **Time Spent in App:**  Showcasing premium content or features based on user interests.
    * **Stage 3: Consistent Engagement (31+ Days):**  More aggressive prompts, highlighting the value proposition.
* **Paywall Types:**
    * **Progressive Paywall:** Start with limited access to core premium features and gradually unlock more as the user engages.
    * **Feature Bundles:** Offer specific feature bundles at different price points (e.g., “Creator Pack,” “Pro Pack”).
* **A/B Testing:** Constantly test different paywall placements, messaging, and even the *timing* of the first paywall prompt.


**III. Free Trial Length & Structure**

* **Initial Length:** 14-30 Days. This is a sweet spot – long enough to let users experience genuine value, but short enough to avoid prolonged free usage.
* **Phased Trials (Highly Recommended):**
    * **Phase 1 (7 Days):** Core Value Demonstration – Focus on highlighting the key benefits. Limited access to advanced features.
    * **Phase 2 (7-14 Days):**  Increased Access & Engagement – Grant access to more features and encourage deeper usage.
    * **Phase 3 (Remaining Days):**  Reinforcement & Upsell –  Remind them of the value they're missing and offer a discounted upgrade.
* **Trial "Milestones":** Design the trial to lead users toward key milestones that demonstrate the value of the premium offering (e.g., creating a certain number of projects, generating a specific type of report).



**IV. Pricing Experiments**

* **A/B Testing is Crucial:** Don’t stick to a single price point.
* **Experiment Types:**
    * **Tiered Pricing:** Offer 3 or 4 tiers (Basic, Standard, Pro) with varying feature sets and price points.
    * **Price Point Variation:**  Test different price points within each tier.
    * **Bundled Pricing:**  Combine features or services into a bundle at a discounted rate.
    * **Usage-Based Pricing (if applicable):**  Charge based on consumption (e.g., number of API calls, storage space).
* **Key Metrics to Track:** Conversion rates, average revenue per user (ARPU), churn rate, customer acquisition cost (CAC).
