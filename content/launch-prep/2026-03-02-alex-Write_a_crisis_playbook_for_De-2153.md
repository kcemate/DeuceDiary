## Deuce Diary Launch Crisis Playbook - Operation "Ink & Fire"

**Executive Summary:** This playbook outlines immediate responses to potential crises during the launch of *Deuce Diary*. It focuses on rapid communication, proactive problem-solving, and maintaining a positive brand image. We'll prioritize transparency, empathy, and quick action.

**I. Core Principles:**

* **Transparency is Key:** Honesty and open communication build trust. Don’t hide issues, address them directly.
* **Prioritize User Impact:** User experience is paramount. Any crisis response must prioritize minimizing user frustration.
* **Rapid Response Team:** Designated team members will handle each stage of the crisis. (See Appendix for roles & responsibilities)
* **Document Everything:** Meticulous record-keeping of all communication, issues, and actions taken.
* **Stay Calm & Focused:** Panic is contagious. Maintain a level-headed approach.


**II. Crisis Scenarios & Response Protocols:**

**Scenario 1: Server Crash**

* **Severity:** High - Immediate impact on user access & potential revenue loss.
* **Timeline:** 0-30 Minutes
    * **Detection:** Monitoring tools (New Relic, Datadog, etc.) trigger alerts.
    * **Immediate Action:**  Tech team (DevOps, Backend) immediately investigate and restore service.  Implement traffic throttling if necessary.
    * **Communication (Lead: Head of Marketing/PR):**
        * **Status Page (Crucial):**  Update public status page with: "We're aware of an issue and our team is working to resolve it ASAP. We'll provide updates here." – Link to status page.
        * **Twitter/Social:** Tweet: "We're experiencing technical difficulties. We’re on it & working to restore service. Thanks for your patience."  (Include a link to the status page).
        * **Email (Automated):** Send automated email to users notifying them of the issue and ETA updates (if available).
* **Timeline:** 30-60 Minutes
    * **Investigation:** Continue diagnostic efforts.
    * **Transparency:** Provide more detailed updates on the cause and resolution timeframe.
    * **Customer Support:**  Prepare Customer Support for influx of inquiries.


**Scenario 2: 1-Star Reviews & Negative Feedback**

* **Severity:** Medium - Impacts user perception & potentially slows downloads.
* **Timeline:** 0-24 Hours
    * **Monitoring:**  Actively monitor app store reviews, social media sentiment, and support channels.
    * **Identify Themes:** Categorize negative feedback (bugs, confusing UI, lack of content, etc.)
    * **Response (Lead: Community Manager/Customer Support):**
        * **Direct Replies:** Respond to individual negative reviews – Acknowledge the frustration, apologize, and offer a solution (e.g., "We're sorry you're experiencing this. Please contact support with details.").
        * **Aggregate Response:** Post a general response on the app store/social media acknowledging the feedback and stating that the team is actively working on improvements.
        * **Prioritize Bug Fixes:**  Immediately escalate critical bug reports to the development team.
* **Timeline:** 24-72 Hours
    * **Feature Requests:**  Use feedback to prioritize new features and UI improvements.



**Scenario 3: Apple Rejection**

* **Severity:** High – Delays launch, potentially significant reputational damage.
* **Timeline:** 0-24 Hours
    * **Immediate Investigation:** Legal & Dev teams collaborate to understand the reason for rejection.
    * **Corrective Action:**  Swiftly address the rejected points (e.g., UI changes, content review).
    * **Appeal Support:** Legal
