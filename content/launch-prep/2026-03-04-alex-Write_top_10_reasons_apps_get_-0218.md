5. **Malfunctioning Apps/Bugs:** Apps must be stable and function as described. Frequent crashes, freezing, or performance issues lead to rejection.
6. **Lack of Proper Metadata:**  Incomplete or inaccurate app titles, descriptions, keywords, or categories can hinder visibility and, if the information is misleading, can lead to rejection.
7. **Violations of Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines (HIG):** This covers everything from UI design, navigation, accessibility, and overall user experience.  Poor UI/UX can be grounds for rejection.
8. **Inappropriate Content:**  Anything sexually suggestive, violent, or harmful is automatically rejected.  Even borderline content can be problematic.
9. **Aggressive or Annoying Practices:**  Apps that bombard users with intrusive ads, excessive notifications, or disruptive behavior get flagged.
10. **Violation of App Store Policies on In-App Purchases:**  Complex or confusing in-app purchase schemes, unclear pricing, or lack of transparency can lead to rejection.
