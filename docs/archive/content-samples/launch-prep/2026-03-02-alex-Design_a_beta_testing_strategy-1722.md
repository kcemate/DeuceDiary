## Beta Testing Strategy: Deuce Diary - TestFlight Rollout

This strategy outlines a phased approach to beta testing Deuce Diary using TestFlight, aiming to identify critical issues and gather valuable feedback before a full public launch.

**I. Goals & Objectives:**

* **Identify Major Bugs & Stability Issues:** Prioritize finding game-breaking bugs, crashes, and performance problems.
* **Gather User Feedback on Core Mechanics:** Assess player understanding and enjoyment of the central diary system, events, and decision-making.
* **Validate Content & Narrative:** Confirm the story, characters, and overall experience resonates with the target audience.
* **Optimize User Interface (UI) & User Experience (UX):**  Identify areas for improvement in navigation, readability, and overall ease of use.
* **Prepare for Public Launch:** Ensure the game is polished enough to confidently release to a wider audience.

**II. TestFlight Rollout Plan - Phased Approach:**

**Phase 1: Internal Testing (1-3 Days)**

* **Participants:** The development team (programmers, artists, designers, QA).
* **Focus:** Initial stability testing, core functionality verification, and basic UI/UX checks.
* **Feedback Collection:** Bug reports (detailed steps to reproduce), direct observation during gameplay.
* **Metrics:** Crash rates, average session duration, common UI navigation issues.
* **Goal:** Identify and fix critical bugs preventing any further testing.


**Phase 2: Small Private Beta (5-7 Days)**

* **Participants:** 20-30 carefully selected users – ideally players who fit the target demographic (e.g., fans of narrative RPGs, historical fiction readers, journaling enthusiasts) recruited through:
    * **Discord Community:** If you have one, this is a perfect place to start.
    * **Social Media:** Targeted posts on platforms like Twitter, Reddit (r/gaming, r/indiegaming, r/narrativedev) - emphasizing early access and feedback requests.
    * **Email List:**  Subscribers who have expressed interest in Deuce Diary.
* **Selection Criteria:** Diversity in gaming experience, age range (within target), willingness to provide detailed feedback.
* **Feedback Collection:**
    * **TestFlight Feedback Form:** Structured questions focusing on:
        * **Overall Impression:** (Rating scale 1-5, open-ended comment)
        * **Diary System:** Clarity of entry mechanics, ease of navigation, usefulness.
        * **Events & Choices:**  Interestingness, clarity of consequences, impact on story.
        * **UI/UX:**  Readability, ease of use, aesthetics.
        * **Technical Issues:**  Crashes, performance, glitches.
    * **In-Game Prompting:** Encourage users to report issues directly through a simple "Report Bug" button.
    * **Weekly Check-in:** Short video call with a few beta testers to discuss their experience and gather more nuanced feedback.
* **Metrics:** Crash rates, session duration, frequency of bug reports, user ratings, qualitative feedback themes.


**Phase 3: Expanded Beta (7-10 Days)**

* **Participants:** 50-100 beta testers – expand recruitment based on learnings from Phase 2.
* **Focus:**  Addressing top-reported issues, exploring edge cases, gathering broader feedback on the game's overall appeal.
* **Feedback Collection:** Same methods as Phase 2, with increased emphasis on usability testing (watching testers play and observing their behavior).
* **Metrics:** All metrics from Phase 2, plus:
    * **Task Completion Rate:**  Percentage of users successfully completing specific tasks within the diary system.
    * **Sentiment Analysis:** Using automated tools to analyze the tone and sentiment of user feedback.
