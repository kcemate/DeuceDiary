**Conversion Friction**

1. The page has 11+ sections (Hero → Stats → Demo Feed → Features → Mid-Page CTA → How It Works → Bonus Features → Testimonials → Mobile App Teaser → FAQ → Final CTA). Most users will never reach the final CTA. This is an extremely long scroll-to-convert funnel.

2. The mid-page CTA section is too thin to earn a conversion — just one sentence and a button, placed before the user has seen enough to be persuaded. It interrupts the narrative flow between features and how-it-works without adding value.

3. The sticky mobile CTA only appears after 500px of scroll — an arbitrary threshold that may fire too late on tall screens or too early on short ones. There's no equivalent persistent CTA for desktop users at all.

4. The demo feed animation deliberately delays showing content (4 items × 400ms + 200ms = 1.8s). A conversion-critical section is actively hiding its own content from users who scroll quickly.

5. The "How It Works" secondary CTA in the hero is an `<a>` tag visually styled to look like a button with equal visual weight to the primary CTA. Two equally prominent actions create decision paralysis rather than a clear primary path.

6. `forceRedirectUrl="/"` on every `SignInButton` redirects users back to this landing page after sign-in, not to the actual app dashboard. Newly signed-in users land on a marketing page instead of their product.

7. The "Mobile App Teaser" section tells users a better native experience is "launching soon" — this creates a reason to wait rather than sign up now. It undermines the web app's value proposition.

**Visual Polish Gaps**

8. The social proof stats show "500+ Active Streakers" and "100+ Squads Competing" — these are objectively low numbers. Highlighting weak adoption on a landing page hurts credibility more than having no stats at all.

9. The demo feed's "NEW" badge uses hardcoded Tailwind green classes (`bg-green-100 text-green-700 border-green-200`) with a `dark:` variant for background only. `text-green-700` on `dark:bg-green-950/20` will have poor contrast in dark mode.

10. The streak rescue teaser uses `text-red-600 dark:text-red-400` — `text-red-400` on `dark:bg-red-950/20` is likely below WCAG AA contrast requirements.

11. The `<details>` FAQ items use `list-none` on the `<summary>`, but Safari still renders a default disclosure triangle regardless. The custom `+` indicator will coexist with the native triangle.

12. The feature cards use both `border border-border` and `border-l-4 border-l-primary` — the left border color override may lose against the shorthand `border-border` depending on Tailwind's class generation order.

13. The `btn-shimmer` CSS animation class is referenced but has no `prefers-reduced-motion` safeguard. Motion-sensitive users will have no way to disable it.

14. The demo feed has no `max-height` or `overflow` constraint. On very small viewports, four feed items plus the streak rescue banner could consume most of the viewport.

**Copy That Doesn't Convert**

15. "The Strava of Poop Tracking" relies on the user knowing what Strava is. For anyone outside fitness-tech circles, this is meaningless. It also uses a trademarked brand name in marketing copy.

16. "Because your best ideas happen on the throne" is a cliché that doesn't communicate what the product does. It's clever but not clear — clarity converts better than cleverness.

17. "Everyone poops — now you can finally prove you're the best at it" — "finally" implies a longstanding unmet need that doesn't exist. Nobody has been waiting for this. The forced humor undermines credibility.

18. The exact line "everyone poops — now you can finally prove you're the best at it" is repeated verbatim in the FAQ. Lazy copy reuse signals inauthenticity.

19. "Porcelain Premium" appears in the bonus features grid and FAQ but is never explained. Users don't know what it includes, what it costs, or why they'd want it.

20. "My doctor asked how I track my habits. I showed him Deuce Diary. He downloaded it." — This testimonial is not believable. Fabricated-sounding social proof erodes trust rather than building it.

21. "10K+ Deuces Logged" as a headline stat is unimpressive. 10K total actions across all users signals very low engagement.

22. The "throne" metaphor is used in the hero headline, features section, how-it-works, mid-page CTA, and final CTA. By the third occurrence it's exhausted; by the fifth it's actively grating.

**Redundant / Low-Value Sections**

23. The "Bonus Features Strip" lists table-stakes features as differentiators: "Dark Mode," "Privacy First," "Mobile Native." These are expected, not selling points. "Privacy First" is a claim with zero evidence behind it — just an emoji and a label.

24. The "How It Works" section reframes the same content already covered in the features section (sign up → squads → streaks). It's redundant — features already explained what streaks, squads, and logging are.

25. The mid-page CTA section is structurally redundant — it's a weaker version of the hero CTA placed at an arbitrary scroll depth.

**Missing Best Practices**

26. No urgency or scarcity anywhere. "Free forever" explicitly removes urgency. There's no early-adopter incentive, no limited-time offer, no reason to sign up today versus tomorrow.

27. No logo bar, press mentions, or recognizable brand trust signals. The only social proof is three suspicious testimonials and weak stats.

28. Desktop users have no persistent CTA after scrolling past the hero. They must scroll to the mid-page or final CTA — no sticky nav, no floating button, nothing.

29. The footer contains only Privacy Policy and Terms of Service — no contact email, no support channel, no social links. This reduces perceived legitimacy.

30. The "Sign Up Free" step says "One tap with Google or Apple" but Clerk's `SignInButton` may present multiple sign-in methods (email, Google, Apple, etc.). The copy overpromises on frictionlessness.

31. The demo feed is static mockup data, but the caption says "Live — your feed updates in real-time." This is a false claim about the demo, which could erode trust.

**Accessibility Issues**

32. The hero 🚽 emoji has `role="img" aria-label="Toilet emoji"` — "Toilet emoji" is not a meaningful description. Screen readers announce literal "toilet emoji" which communicates nothing of value.

33. The demo feed entries are presented as real content to screen readers (usernames, thoughts, locations, reactions) with no indication they are sample/demo data. Screen reader users will encounter these as factual entries.

34. The `<details>` elements lack ARIA expanded state management. The `+` indicator is `aria-hidden="true"`, but the open/closed state isn't reliably communicated to assistive technologies across browsers.

35. The sticky mobile CTA uses `role="complementary"` — this is semantically wrong. It's a primary action bar, not supplementary content. Screen readers may deprioritize or mischaracterize it.

36. `text-[10px]` with `text-muted-foreground` (timestamps, location text in the demo feed) is very likely below WCAG minimum contrast at that size.

37. Feature card hover effects (`hover:shadow-lg`) have no `:focus-visible` equivalent. Keyboard users see zero visual change when navigating to these cards.

38. The "How It Works" anchor in the hero has no visible focus ring styling defined. Default browser focus styles may be invisible against the card background.

39. The sticky mobile CTA is at the bottom of the DOM, so keyboard users must tab through the entire page to reach it — it's not in the logical tab order matching its visual position.

40. When demo feed items animate in, there's no `aria-live` region to announce new content to screen reader users.

**Code-Level Bug**

41. In `DemoGroupFeed`'s `useEffect`, the cleanup function `return () => { timers.forEach(clearTimeout); clearTimeout(pulseTimer); }` is returned *inside* the `IntersectionObserver` callback, not from the `useEffect`. It never executes. The actual cleanup is only `observer.disconnect()`, so timers and the pulse timer are never cleared on unmount.
