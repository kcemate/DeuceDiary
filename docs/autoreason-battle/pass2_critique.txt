1. **Mobile Placement Blindness**: The placement logic relies on `onCellHover` to show a preview of where the ship will go, but mobile users don't have hover states. They are forced to tap the grid blindly, which leads to accidental placements or constant error toasts interrupting the flow.
2. **Hidden Destructive Tap**: Tapping an unplaced ship in the inventory selects it, but tapping an already-placed ship immediately removes it without confirmation. A user trying to re-select a placed ship to review it will accidentally destroy their placement, causing frustration.
3. **Confusing Auto-Advance**: After successfully placing a ship, the selection auto-advances to the next unplaced ship. If the user isn't watching the inventory list closely, they may attempt to place another of the previous ship type and accidentally place the wrong one.
4. **Vague Timer Consequence**: When the placement timer expires, the UI says "ships will auto-deploy when ready." It is completely unclear what this means. Does the game randomly place the remaining ships? Does the player forfeit? The player is left in a state of uncertainty about their agency.
5. **Weak Touch Targets**: The orientation toggle buttons use `py-2` and `text-xs`. These are small, thin targets that are difficult to hit accurately on a mobile device, especially for an action that needs to be toggled frequently during placement.
6. **Personality Void**: The game has a clear bathroom humor theme (emojis like 💩, 🚽, ship names like "Battleshit"), but the instructional copy is sterile ("Phase 1: Deploy Your Fleet", "Select a ship, then tap the grid to place it"). This disconnect makes the game feel like a dry tutorial rather than a thematic experience.
7. **Missing Stakes & Progression**: The data model includes `xpAwarded` and `streakCount`, but the placement screen displays none of this. The player has no visual indication of what they are fighting for or how this match impacts their overall progression.
8. **No Opponent Presence**: There is no visual representation of the opponent (name, avatar, or stats) during the placement phase. The screen feels like a solo puzzle rather than a head-to-head battle, which reduces emotional investment and urgency.
9. **Obstructive Error Feedback**: Invalid placements trigger a toast notification. On mobile, toasts can obscure the grid and require the user to wait for them to disappear or manually swipe them away, adding friction to a phase that should be rapid and fluid.
10. **Unused Social Mechanics**: The `TAUNTS` array is defined in the code but is completely absent from the placement UI. This is a missed opportunity to let players start interacting with their opponent early, building social tension and investment before the battle even begins.
